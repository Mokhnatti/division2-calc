## At a Glance

Murakami Industries is the brand that runs the status meta. If you've ever looked at a Heroic mission and thought "I want everyone in this room to be on fire, blinded, and bleeding simultaneously" - this is the brand that builds that vision. The combination of Status Duration, massive Skill Cooldown Reduction, and Status Effects amplification in a single brand is unmatched, and the synergy with Eclipse Protocol and the Iron Lung backpack is one of the most devastating combos in current PvE.

Murakami's design philosophy is patient violence. Status effects in The Division 2 don't kill quickly - they burn, bleed, disorient, and stack over time. Murakami amplifies all three pillars of status play: how long they last, how strong they are, and how often you can apply them. The 2pc Skill Cooldown Reduction in particular is a build-defining bonus that removes the biggest weakness of skill builds (downtime).

| Slot | Bonus | What it does |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1 piece | +15% Status Duration | Burns last longer, blinds stick longer, every DoT scales |
| 2 pieces | +35% Skill Cooldown Reduction | Massive uptime improvement for status-applying skills |
| 3 pieces | +18% Status Effects | Amplifies the magnitude of every status you apply |

The 2pc CDR bonus alone is one of the largest single-source CDR bonuses in the game. For Eclipse Protocol gear set users (whose damage scales with status applications), Murakami is mandatory.

## Bonuses Detail

### 1-Piece: +15% Status Duration

Status Duration extends how long every applied status effect lasts. Burn ticks for longer, blind sticks for longer, bleed continues to drain HP for additional seconds. For burn-focused builds especially, every additional second of burn time is more damage on the target.

The 15% bonus stacks with rolled Status Duration attributes and other Status Duration sources, multiplying your effective DoT damage and crowd-control uptime. This is the foundation bonus for any status build.

### 2-Piece: +35% Skill Cooldown Reduction

This is the brand's defining bonus. 35% Skill CDR on a single 2pc is one of the largest CDR bonuses available. For comparison, most CDR sources in the game are 5-15% per attribute or piece. Getting 35% from two brand slots means your skills come back online dramatically faster, which directly translates to more status applications, more damage, and more crowd control.

For Eclipse Protocol players, this is non-negotiable. The gear set damage scales with status applications, and faster cooldowns mean more applications, which means more damage. The 2pc bonus essentially solves the skill-build downtime problem.

### 3-Piece: +18% Status Effects

Status Effects amplifies the magnitude of every status you apply. Burn ticks bigger. Bleed drains more HP per tick. Blind reduces enemy accuracy more aggressively. This is the multiplier on top of duration, and combined with the 1pc bonus, you get longer-lasting AND stronger status effects.

Pair this with Wyvern Wear's 2pc (+18% Negative Effects) and you stack two amplification multipliers, making your status effects roughly 36% stronger before any rolled attributes or gear set bonuses.

## Best Use Cases

- **Eclipse Protocol Builds:** the canonical use. Eclipse Protocol gear set damage scales with status applications, and Murakami is the brand that maximizes status output. 3pc Murakami + Eclipse Protocol + Iron Lung is the burn meta.
- **Iron Lung Backpack Builds:** Iron Lung applies burn status when activated. Murakami's status duration AND status effects bonuses scale the burn directly. Synergy is built into the design.
- **Status Spread Hybrid:** 2pc Murakami + 2pc Wyvern Wear + 2pc Hana-U Ceremonial gives you a 2/2/2 status powerhouse with cooldown, status amplification, and skill damage all stacked.
- **Solo Skill Burner:** Eclipse Protocol + Murakami + Firestarter Chem Launcher + Iron Lung. Solo Heroic mission rooms turn into furnaces. Stand back, throw chem, and watch the room burn.

Murakami is *not* a great fit for pure DPS builds (no crit/HSD), pure tank builds (no defensive bonuses), or sniper one-shot setups. It's a status-focused skill brand and shines in that lane.

## Items in Brand

Murakami Industries has a modest but high-value named pool, with several pieces appearing in mission-specific targeted loot rotations. The brand is reliably farmable through Warlords of New York content and DC zone rotations.

### Named Pieces in Murakami Industries

The brand contains named gear that synergizes with status-focused builds. Notable named pieces in the Murakami pool include status-amplifying mask and gloves slots that pair directly with the brand's identity.

The named talent pool focuses on status-related multipliers and skill efficiency, making them desirable cherry picks even for non-status builds that want skill cooldown bonuses.

### Recommended Slot Distribution

For a 3pc Murakami status build:
- **Gloves + Kneepads + Backpack:** keeps mask and chest open for named pieces (Coyote's Mask alternative, Iron Lung backpack if not slotted from named pool, Eclipse Protocol pieces).
- **Mask + Gloves + Kneepads:** alternative when you want Iron Lung in the backpack slot from a different brand.

The flexibility allows pairing Murakami with the Eclipse Protocol gear set's required slots and the Iron Lung backpack without losing brand bonuses.

## Top Builds Using This Brand

### Eclipse Protocol Burn Meta (PvE Heroic)

- 3pc Murakami Industries (Gloves, Kneepads, Holster)
- 3pc Eclipse Protocol gear set (chest, backpack, mask or other slots)
- Iron Lung backpack (if not on Eclipse) OR Eclipse backpack
- Firestarter Chem Launcher + Striker drone
- Goal: maximum burn uptime + status amplification. Throw chem, drone shoots, room burns. Eclipse Protocol scales damage with status applications and Murakami feeds it constantly.
- Result: Heroic missions become attrition fights you always win. Even if enemies don't die immediately, they burn down on their own.

### Hybrid Status Spread (Group Heroic / Legendary)

- 3pc Murakami Industries
- 2pc Wyvern Wear (mask + gloves) - +18% Negative Effects
- 1pc filler (Hana-U or Ceska)
- Eclipse Protocol or Firestarter Chem Launcher
- Iron Lung backpack
- Goal: stack two status amplifiers (Murakami 3pc + Wyvern 2pc) for ~36% status effect amplification combined.

### Solo Burner (Open World / Countdown)

- 3pc Murakami Industries
- 1x Iron Lung backpack
- 1x Coyote's Mask or Determined chest
- 1x Hana-U or Ceska filler
- Firestarter Chem Launcher + Striker drone or Stinger hive
- Best for solo players who want to clear rooms with skills and let burn DoT finish what the drone starts.

### Pure Cooldown Skill Hybrid (No Eclipse)

- 2pc Murakami Industries (just for the 35% CDR)
- 2pc Hard Wired or 2pc Empress International (Skill Tier)
- 2pc Wyvern Wear (Skill Damage + Status)
- Status-applying skills + skill mod attribute focus
- Goal: keep skills always available, never wait for cooldowns. The 2pc Murakami CDR makes this build feel completely different from cooldown-starved skill setups.

## Pros & Cons

- 35% Skill CDR on 2pc is one of the largest CDR bonuses in the game
- 1pc + 3pc combine for ~33% status amplification (duration + effects)
- Mandatory for Eclipse Protocol gear set builds
- Synergy with Iron Lung backpack is built into the design

## FAQ

**Q: Is Murakami Industries worth running without Eclipse Protocol?**
A: Yes, especially the 2pc bonus. 35% Skill CDR is huge for any skill build, even without the gear set. The full 3pc shines most with Eclipse Protocol but works on hybrid status builds too.

**Q: Does the 1pc Status Duration affect Iron Lung's burn?**
A: Yes. Iron Lung applies a burn status effect, and the 1pc Status Duration bonus extends how long that burn ticks. The 3pc Status Effects bonus also amplifies the burn's magnitude. Iron Lung + Murakami is one of the cleanest synergies in the game.

**Q: Should I run 2pc Murakami or 3pc?**
A: 3pc for full status builds. 2pc is excellent as a CDR cherry on non-status skill builds, where you skip the 1pc and 3pc bonuses for the cooldown alone.

**Q: How does Murakami compare to Hard Wired for skill builds?**
A: Hard Wired is for skill-tier and skill-haste maximizers. Murakami is for status-focused builds. Different roles. Hard Wired makes turret damage huge. Murakami makes status applications constant and devastating.

**Q: Does the 3pc Status Effects stack with Wyvern Wear's 2pc Negative Effects?**
A: Yes. Both bonuses amplify status magnitude and stack additively into the status amplification pool. Combining them is a common high-tier status build pattern.

**Q: Is Murakami still meta in TU22.1?**
A: Absolutely. The 2pc CDR is unmatched and the 3pc Status Effects bonus is core to Eclipse Protocol and Iron Lung builds. Status meta has held strong through multiple balance passes.

## Closing

Murakami Industries is the brand that turns "I want enemies to suffer" into a viable build philosophy. Status effects in The Division 2 are slow, patient damage - they don't burst, they accumulate. Murakami amplifies every dimension of that accumulation: how long it lasts, how often you can apply it, and how strong each application is. For Eclipse Protocol burn builds and Iron Lung synergy setups, no other brand even comes close.

The 2pc Skill Cooldown Reduction deserves special mention. 35% CDR from two slots is the kind of bonus that fundamentally changes how a build plays. Skills you used to wait 10 seconds for now come back in 6.5. Status applications go from "occasional" to "constant," and a Heroic mission room transforms from a firefight into a furnace.

If you want to set the world on fire and watch it burn, Murakami is your brand.
