## At a Glance

Richter and Kaiser GmbH is the support-tank brand. It does not push raw armor stacking like Uzina or Palisade - instead it amplifies the rate at which you receive repair and shores up skill health and hazard protection at the same time. For builds that bring their own healing (Reviver Hive, Reinforcer chem launcher, Restorer hive, party medic) or that pair with a healer in a group, Richter and Kaiser converts incoming repair into much more effective sustain.

It is also one of the most flexible "second brand" options in the game. Three pieces alongside a damage brand gives a damage build hazard protection and skill health it would otherwise lack, without giving up raw offense.

## Bonuses

### 1 Piece - Plus 15 Percent Incoming Repair

Incoming repair scales how much armor or health you actually receive from any healing source. A 100K Reinforcer chem launcher pulse becomes a 115K pulse on a Richter and Kaiser 1-piece. The bonus applies to:

- Self-healing skills (Restorer Hive, Fixer Drone, Reinforcer)
- Healing from teammates' skills
- Talent-based regen (Bloodlust, Patience, Concussion, et cetera)
- Armor regeneration ticks
- Self-repair from skill talents like Empress's Capacitor pulse

It is one of the most universally useful 1-piece bonuses in the brand pool because there is almost always a healing source somewhere in the build.

### 2 Piece - Plus 20 Percent Skill Health

A flat skill health bonus that keeps deployables alive longer. Twenty percent is the same headline number as China Light Industries' 3-piece, but here you get it from just two pieces, freeing the third slot for the hazard protection bonus.

For shield builds, this is meaningful: a Bulwark or Striker shield gets 20 percent more health, which directly translates to more uptime under fire. For drone tanks, the defender drone takes more hits before going down. For hive supports, the Restorer hive lasts longer in a Heroic firefight.

### 3 Piece - Plus 15 Percent Hazard Protection

Hazard protection on the 3-piece, layered on top of the skill health and incoming repair bonuses below. Fifteen percent is less than Palisade Steelworks's 20 percent but still substantial - it stacks with hazard protection rolls and pushes the build toward the in-game cap.

The 3-piece completes a full defensive package: more healing in, more skill HP, and less status damage taken. For hybrid builds that want to avoid full pure-armor brands, the Richter and Kaiser 3-piece is the cleanest single-brand defensive sheet.

## Best Use

Richter and Kaiser GmbH fits builds that want defensive depth without sacrificing offense. Specifically:

- **Group medic / support** - the incoming repair amplifies allied healing, skill health keeps the heal field alive
- **Foundry Bulwark with healing skills** - shield uptime plus repair scaling on the player behind it
- **Hardwired healer** - Restorer hive on Hardwired loops, with Richter's incoming repair stretching each pulse
- **Hybrid weapon DPS with self-heal** - Reviver Hive plus a damage brand plus three Richter pieces for the safety net
- **Raid healers** - especially in Iron Horse or Dark Hours, where keeping the team topped up matters

## Item Pool

Richter and Kaiser drops in all six slots and rotates through standard targeted loot. No exotics are tied to the brand, but several named variants offer elevated rolls.

Recommended attribute priorities:

1. **Skill tier** if running skill-tank or healer hybrid (so the 2-piece skill health pays off)
2. **Armor** for hybrid tank chassis
3. **Hazard protection** as a tertiary to stack with the 3-piece

- 3 pieces Richter and Kaiser

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- Most universally useful 1-piece bonus in the tank brand pool (incoming repair is rarely wasted)
- Three different defensive stats from one brand - no other brand offers this breadth in three pieces
- Skill health on 2-piece pays off across all skill archetypes
- Excellent secondary brand for hybrid builds that need defensive depth without losing offense
- Pairs cleanly with both pure-tank brands (Uzina, Palisade) and healer-leaning skill brands (Empress)

### Cons

- No raw armor multiplier - on a pure sponge build, Uzina or Palisade outperform
- Skill damage is absent; this is a defensive skill brand, not an offensive one
- Hazard protection is below Palisade's, so for pure status-resistance builds Palisade still wins
- Brand value drops on builds that have no incoming repair source - the 1-piece becomes a dead bonus

## FAQ

### Does +15 percent incoming repair stack with allied healing skills?

Yes. Allied chem launcher pulses, restorer hive ticks, and any team-wide repair source are amplified by the 1-piece bonus on the receiving player. Group medic builds become significantly more effective when the recipients have one piece each.

### Is Richter and Kaiser better than Palisade Steelworks?

Different roles. Palisade is the pure-defense brand for Heroic and Legendary tanks. Richter and Kaiser is the hybrid brand that pairs with damage builds or healers. For raid tanks, Palisade. For raid healers and hybrid builds, Richter.

### Does the 2-piece skill health affect shields specifically?

Yes - all deployables, including Bulwark shields, get the +20 percent skill health bonus. This is one of the strongest pieces of the brand for shield builds.

### Will the 1-piece incoming repair improve armor regen?

Yes. Armor regeneration is a self-repair source and is amplified by incoming repair. The interaction is sometimes overlooked but is significant on builds with high regen rolls.

### Is the brand viable in solo play?

Yes, especially on Reviver Hive or Restorer Hive solo builds. The incoming repair amplifies your own self-heal, and the skill health keeps the Reviver alive long enough to actually trigger.

## Closing Notes

Richter and Kaiser GmbH is the swiss-army-knife of defensive brands. It rewards thoughtful build composition - if you bring a healing source, a deployable, and any kind of status-heavy content, every line on the brand is doing real work. The breadth is the point: three different defensive stats from one brand cover three different damage scenarios, and that flexibility is what makes Richter and Kaiser endure across every patch.

It is rarely the flashiest pick, but it is consistently useful in hybrid loadouts, group play, and any build that wants a defensive cushion without giving up its offensive identity.
