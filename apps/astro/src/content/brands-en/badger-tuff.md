## At a Glance

Badger Tuff is one of The Division 2's most distinctive niche brands, built around the close-range firepower duo of shotguns and pistols. The brand's identity is unapologetically aggressive: get in close, blast through enemies with a high-damage shotgun, and finish stragglers with your sidearm. With a small armor cushion sitting in the middle, Badger Tuff offers just enough survivability for an in-your-face playstyle without giving up its damage scaling.

The brand has gained traction in TU22.1 due to the rebalancing of close-range engagements in PvE and the rise of pistol-focused builds in PvP. Players running Sweet Dreams, Lullaby, or even the Diceros pistol have found Badger Tuff to be a quietly powerful pick.

## Brand Bonuses

The Badger Tuff bonuses are tightly themed around close-range weapons:

### 1-Piece Bonus

- +10% Shotgun Damage

A flat 10% damage boost to all shotgun-class weapons. This stacks with weapon attribute rolls and weapon talents to produce some of the highest per-shot damage numbers in the game on builds running a god-roll shotgun.

### 2-Piece Bonus

- +5% Armor

A small but unconditional armor boost. The 5% supports the brand's close-range identity by giving a measurable cushion when pushing into enemies. While modest on its own, the bonus stacks with other armor multipliers cleanly.

### 3-Piece Bonus

- +25% Pistol Damage

The signature bonus. Twenty-five percent additional pistol damage is a major buff for any sidearm and a defining bonus for pistol-focused builds. Combined with weapon attribute rolls, talents, and exotic pistols, this bonus pushes pistols into legitimate primary-weapon territory.

## Best Use

Badger Tuff is best used on close-range hybrid builds, shotgun-pistol setups, and PvP brawler builds. It is not a generalist brand; using all three pieces on a build that runs neither shotguns nor pistols wastes most of the value. But on a dedicated close-quarters build, every bonus contributes meaningfully.

The 3-piece is also commonly used to power builds around exotic pistols like Liberty, Regulus, and Nemesis (when used as a sidearm in unconventional builds).

## Recommended Items

Badger Tuff drops with red and blue cores. Red cores are dramatically preferred for the brand's offensive identity:

- Mask with Headshot Damage and Critical Hit Chance for shotgun precision
- Chest with Weapon Damage core and Glass Cannon or Obliterate
- Backpack with Critical Hit Damage and Spotter
- Knee Pads with Weapon Damage and Headshot Damage
- Holster with Critical Hit Damage to layer with the pistol bonus

The brand's pieces are best used as a 3-piece block when the build is committed to shotguns and pistols. Otherwise, prefer a 1-piece or 2-piece pickup.

## Build Ideas

### The Sweet Dreams Brawler

3-piece Badger Tuff with the Sweet Dreams exotic shotgun and the Liberty exotic pistol. Stack Critical Hit Chance and Critical Hit Damage to maximum, use Hunter's Fury gear set if available. The result is a brawler build that one-shots veterans with shotgun blasts and finishes off elites with pistol crits.

### The Liberty Pistol Specialist

2-piece Badger Tuff with the Liberty exotic pistol and Diamondback. Even though Diamondback is a rifle, it pairs perfectly with the Liberty pistol's mark-and-shoot mechanic, and the Badger Tuff 1-piece and 3-piece scale Liberty into a primary-tier weapon.

### The Hunter's Fury Shotgun Build

3-piece Badger Tuff plus Hunter's Fury gear set. Use a Tactical AK or god-roll Bullet King with a backup shotgun. Hunter's Fury's close-range identity meshes perfectly with the Badger Tuff bonuses, producing one of the deadliest aggressive PvE builds.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- Best brand in the game for shotgun and pistol builds
- 25% pistol damage is one of the largest sidearm multipliers available
- 10% shotgun damage stacks cleanly with attribute rolls and talents
- 5% armor 2-piece adds a small cushion for aggressive play
- Strong in both PvE and PvP brawl builds
- Synergizes with exotic pistols and shotguns

### Cons

- Bonuses are useless on AR, LMG, MMR, and SMG builds
- Three-piece slot allocation is restrictive on hybrid builds
- 5% armor 2-piece is the weakest armor brand bonus available
- Encourages a close-range playstyle that is not viable in every encounter
- Less universal than damage brands like Providence or Petrov

## FAQ

### Is Badger Tuff still good in TU22.1?

Yes. Pistol builds have gained traction with the buffs to several exotic pistols, and shotguns remain strong in close-range PvE and PvP. Badger Tuff is the natural brand for both archetypes.

### Does the pistol damage bonus apply to exotic pistols?

Yes. The bonus applies to all pistol-class weapons, including Liberty, Regulus, and Nemesis when used as a sidearm.

### Is Badger Tuff worth running with just a shotgun?

The 1-piece is worth a partial pickup. The full 3-piece is best when both your primary and sidearm benefit from the brand bonuses.

### Can I run Badger Tuff in PvP?

Yes. The brand is excellent in Conflict for shotgun rushers and pistol-focused brawlers, especially in the new close-range PvP maps.

### Does Badger Tuff stack with weapon talents like Strained?

Yes. The brand bonuses are separate multipliers from weapon talents and attribute rolls, so they stack cleanly with Strained, Optimist, and similar talents.

## Closing Thoughts

Badger Tuff is a brand with a clear identity and a fanatical user base. It is not the right brand for every build, and trying to force it onto a generalist DPS setup will waste two of its three bonuses. But for the agent who lives the close-range fantasy, who keeps a shotgun in the primary slot and a pistol in the sidearm, who pushes corners and trades shells at point-blank range, Badger Tuff is the brand that defines that playstyle.

In TU22.1, with shotgun and pistol exotics in solid balance, Badger Tuff has found a comfortable spot in the meta. Pair it with Sweet Dreams, Liberty, Lullaby, or your favorite god-roll shotgun, and you will discover one of the most satisfying playstyles The Division 2 has to offer.
