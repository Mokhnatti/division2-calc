## Petrov Defense Group - LMG Specialist Brand

Last reviewed: 2026-04-25 · TU22.1 · Verified vs in-game

Petrov Defense Group is the brand every LMG main runs. It exists almost exclusively to push LMG damage to the ceiling, with a 1pc weapon-type bonus, a 2pc threat reduction layer for survival, and a 3pc total weapon damage multiplier that scales every gun in your loadout. If you fire belt-fed weapons in The Division 2, you are running Petrov.

### At a Glance

- 1pc: +10% LMG Damage
- 2pc: +30% Threat Reduction
- 3pc: +20% Total Weapon Damage
- Role: LMG Specialist / DPS
- Tier: S for LMG, A for any sustained-fire DPS
- Best slot count: 3pc
- Synergies: Negotiator's Dilemma, Vigilance, Glasscannon, Eyeless, Strained, Determined

The brand identity is "weapon damage with a survival layer." The 3pc 20% total weapon damage is one of the largest single brand bonuses in the game, and the 2pc threat reduction lets you sustain fire without becoming a magnet for every Black Tusk medic in the room.

### Bonuses Detail

The 1pc +10% LMG Damage is a flat damage modifier on every belt-fed weapon: Pestilence, Stop Drop & Roll, Negev, M249, MK46, RPK74, L86 LSW and the Bullet King. It stacks multiplicatively with weapon-class scaling and gear talents like Strained, which means a baseline LMG hits noticeably harder with even one Petrov piece equipped.

The 2pc +30% Threat Reduction is the underrated bonus. Threat is the AI aggression mechanic that determines who NPCs target. Reducing threat means fewer rushers, fewer grenades aimed at you, fewer flankers, and more tactical breathing room. For LMG users who benefit from holding a sustained line of fire, threat reduction is genuinely a damage stat because it keeps you stationary and shooting.

The 3pc +20% Total Weapon Damage is the headline. It's a flat multiplier on every weapon you fire, not just LMGs. That makes Petrov 3pc useful even in non-LMG builds where you want raw damage. Combined with crit, headshot, and weapon type bonuses, it pushes burst damage windows to the highest tier.

### Best Use Cases

Petrov shines in four archetypes:

1. Pestilence DOT LMG. Pestilence applies a Plague of the Outcasts debuff that ticks for damage. Petrov 3pc multiplies the bullet damage and the DOT, making Pestilence one of the top sustained-DPS weapons in TU22.1.
2. Negotiator's Dilemma tag-team. Tag enemies with the exotic gloves, then mow them down with an LMG buffed by Petrov. Tagged kills propagate damage to other tagged enemies, scaled by your weapon damage stat.
3. Strained crit-LMG. Glasscannon backpack and Strained chest with 3pc Petrov creates one of the highest sustained DPS profiles in the game.
4. Bullet King infinite ammo. Bullet King's infinite magazine pairs perfectly with Petrov 3pc total weapon damage and Strained for extended high-damage windows.

It is not the right pick for skill builds, status builds, or sniper crit-chain builds. Those want Hana-U, Empress, or Airaldi.

### Items in Brand

Petrov drops the standard six gear slots: mask, backpack, chest, gloves, holster, kneepads. Notable rolls per slot:

- Mask: Headshot Damage, Critical Hit Chance, Weapon Damage
- Backpack: Weapon Damage, Critical Hit Damage, Headshot Damage
- Chest: Weapon Damage, LMG Damage, Critical Hit Damage
- Gloves: Weapon Damage, Critical Hit Chance, LMG Damage
- Holster: Critical Hit Damage, Weapon Damage, LMG Damage
- Kneepads: Weapon Damage, Health, Hazard Protection

Almost every slot rolls Weapon Damage as a primary, which makes Petrov the easiest brand to farm for DPS rolls.

### Pros & Cons

Pros:
- Largest single weapon-damage brand bonus in the game (20% on 3pc)
- Threat reduction is uniquely useful for sustained-fire weapons
- Roll table is DPS-focused on every slot
- Stacks with every chest talent (Strained, Obliterate, Vigilance, Spotter)

Cons:
- 1pc LMG Damage is dead weight outside LMG builds
- No skill, status or armor scaling
- Threat reduction can be detrimental in solo play where you want to control aggro
- Highly contested in the meta, so rolls are farmed competitively

### FAQ

Q: Is Petrov 3pc better than Wyvern 4pc for LMG?
A: Petrov 3pc + 3pc Sokolov is the standard high-DPS split. Wyvern Wear 4pc shifts the build toward shotgun and hybrid scaling and is not LMG-specific. For pure LMG, Petrov wins.

Q: Does +20% Total Weapon Damage stack with Glasscannon?
A: Yes. Glasscannon adds a percentage damage modifier with a 25% incoming damage penalty. Petrov 3pc adds its own multiplier on top, and they stack multiplicatively for total damage.

Q: Is Threat Reduction useful in Heroic and Legendary?
A: Yes. AI aggression is more aggressive at higher difficulties, so reducing threat keeps you off the priority list of suicide rushers and engineers.

Q: Should I run 2pc or 3pc Petrov?
A: 3pc whenever possible. The 20% total weapon damage on 3pc is too valuable to skip in a DPS build. 2pc is acceptable only when you need an exotic gear slot.

Q: Petrov vs Fenris for AR?
A: Fenris has +10% AR Damage 1pc and +15% Crit Chance 3pc, which favors AR specifically. Petrov 3pc gives +20% total weapon damage which scales any weapon. For AR, Fenris is technically better at peak roll, but Petrov is more flexible for hybrid loadouts.

### Closing

Petrov Defense Group is the foundation of LMG meta in The Division 2 and a strong choice for any sustained-fire DPS. The 20% total weapon damage on 3pc is unmatched for raw scaling, the 2pc threat reduction adds tactical value, and the LMG-specific 1pc rounds out the package. Keep a full set rolled with Weapon Damage on every slot, Critical Hit Damage on backpack, and LMG Damage on chest, gloves and holster. Every endgame loadout has room for Petrov.
