## At a Glance

Legatus S.p.A. is the unconventional brand. While most of the brand pool focuses cleanly on damage, skill, or tank archetypes, Legatus stitches together three stats that do not naturally line up: weapon swap speed, a giant health bonus, and stagger damage. The result is a brand that rewards specific, deliberate builds - shotgun-rifle swap DPS, melee-and-stagger Foundry tanks, two-weapon talent rotators - rather than slotting into a generic loadout.

For the players who build around it, Legatus is one of the most distinctive brands in the game. For everyone else, it is easy to overlook because the bonuses do not advertise their value at a glance.

## Bonuses

### 1 Piece - Plus 30 Percent Weapon Swap Speed

The fastest single-source weapon swap bonus in the brand pool. Thirty percent shaves a meaningful chunk off swap animations, which directly affects:

- **Allegro talent** - swap-trigger that grants rate of fire
- **Surge talent** - reload-and-swap activated bonus damage
- **Eye of Storm and similar swap-condition talents**
- **Two-weapon DPS rotations** - shotgun close, rifle long, swap between them mid-fight
- **Gunner specialization** - quick fallback to secondary when the LMG mag empties

For builds that use swap as a mechanic rather than just a transition, this 1-piece is more valuable than a damage roll on the same slot.

### 2 Piece - Plus 70 Percent Health

The headline survivor stat. Seventy percent flat health bonus on top of base. On a 600K HP chassis, this is over 400K extra HP - a tank-grade buffer for any build that runs blue cores or hybrid armor-health setups. It is also the largest health bonus available from a brand in the entire game.

For Foundry Bulwark builds, this is mostly a gimmick - shields scale with armor, not health. But for melee, status-resist, and stagger-focused builds where the player takes hits directly, 70 percent health is a meaningful survival cushion.

### 3 Piece - Plus 30 Percent Stagger

Stagger damage measures how quickly a target's flinch / stun bar fills under fire. Thirty percent is a substantial bonus, and it has two main use cases:

- **Boss-stagger comps** - raid bosses and elite targets have stagger thresholds; faster stagger means more frequent stun windows
- **Crowd-control with shotguns** - stagger amplifies the existing flinch on close-range buckshot, locking elites in place

The 3-piece is also a synergy point with the 1-piece: shotgun-and-swap combos chain stagger windows together, with each fast swap triggering another stagger pulse.

## Best Use

Legatus S.p.A. fits niche but powerful archetypes:

- **Shotgun-and-swap DPS** - the 1-piece swap speed and 3-piece stagger compound into a stunlock loop
- **Foundry Bulwark melee-tank** - the 70 percent health pads survivability while the shield is down
- **Allegro / Surge talent rotators** - swap speed feeds the talent triggers; health keeps the build alive between rotations
- **Stagger-comp raid teams** - the 3-piece stagger stacks across team members for faster boss stun cycles

## Item Pool

Legatus drops in all six gear slots and is included in the standard targeted loot rotation. There are no exotics tied to the brand. Targeted loot weeks are the most efficient farm route for clean three-piece sets.

Recommended attribute priorities:

1. **Health** on red or blue cores for chassis that builds around the 70 percent health bonus
2. **Weapon damage** as primary if running the swap-DPS archetype
3. **Critical hit damage** or **headshot damage** to amplify the swap-talent damage windows

- ACS-12 shotgun with Allegro talent, secondary marksman rifle for ranged finishes

- 3 pieces Legatus (swap speed and health)

- 3 pieces Legatus (the 3-piece stagger)

- LMG or AR with high fire rate
- Designed for raid encounters with stagger-vulnerable bosses

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- Largest single-source health bonus on a brand (70 percent)
- Highest weapon swap speed bonus available
- Stagger damage bonus opens niche but powerful raid and CC builds
- Drops in every slot for flexible farming
- Distinctive identity - the brand enables builds no other brand can support

### Cons

- Bonuses do not naturally synergize unless the build is specifically designed around them
- 70 percent health is mostly wasted on armor-core builds
- Stagger has limited application outside specific encounter design
- Brand pays off only when the player commits to a swap-mechanic or stagger-mechanic playstyle
- Easy to overlook for general PvE - other brands offer more direct damage or defense

## FAQ

### Does +30 percent weapon swap speed affect Allegro and Surge specifically?

Yes. Both talents trigger on weapon swap, and faster swap animations mean both can be cycled more frequently. For Allegro in particular - which grants ROF on swap - the 1-piece can directly increase practical DPS by enabling more frequent swap-rotations.

### Is +70 percent health useful on a pure armor build?

Not really. Armor builds prioritize armor pool, and health is a secondary survival layer. The 70 percent health bonus pays off more on hybrid builds, melee specialists, and specific boss-fight scenarios where damage chunks past armor.

### Does the 3-piece stagger work on all enemies?

Most non-boss elites and named enemies have a stagger bar that fills under fire. Bosses sometimes have stagger thresholds tied to mechanics. For trash mobs, stagger does not have a meaningful effect because they die before the bar fills.

### Is Legatus better than 5.11 Tactical for swap builds?

Different angles. 5.11 has its own niche identity and stat sheet. Legatus is more focused on swap speed and health, while 5.11 leans toward pistol damage and weapon stability. For pure swap DPS, Legatus has a slight edge thanks to the dedicated swap-speed line.

### Does the brand have value in Dark Zone or PvP?

Yes, in specific cases. The 70 percent health is meaningful when armor breaks in PvP, and stagger can lock down opponents during peek trades. Swap speed helps with shotgun-rifle range transitions in DZ encounters.

## Closing Notes

Legatus S.p.A. is the brand for players who want to build something specific. The bonuses do not slot cleanly into generic DPS or tank loadouts - they reward commitment to a particular mechanic, whether that is swap-talent rotation, melee-stagger comp, or hybrid health-tank survival. For those builds, Legatus is the only brand that fills the role; for everything else, the brand pool offers more direct alternatives.

If you have ever wanted to build around the swap mechanic instead of treating it as a transition, or if you run stagger-comp in raids, this is your brand. For everyone else, it is a stash filler waiting for the right inspiration.
