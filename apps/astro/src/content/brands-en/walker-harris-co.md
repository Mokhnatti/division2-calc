## At a Glance

Walker, Harris & Co. is the workhorse DPS brand of The Division 2 sandbox. Where other brands chase niche identities, Walker keeps it simple: pure red stats, layered cleanly, with a three-piece bonus chain that fits more endgame builds than any other brand in the game. If you have ever opened a community spreadsheet and seen the line "two-piece Walker" without further explanation, that is because two-piece Walker has become shorthand for "I am not going to argue with the math."

The brand bonuses read as follows. One piece grants +5% Weapon Damage, two pieces add +5% Critical Hit Chance, and three pieces stack +20% Critical Hit Damage. Every tier is a clean, universally-applicable damage stat, which is exactly why the brand is so omnipresent. There is no flavor text, no situational gimmick, no weapon-specific multiplier. Walker is the chassis. You bolt your build on top of it.

The named pieces in Walker's catalog are not flashy, but they are reliable. Walker's strength has never been chasing a god-roll exotic synergy. It is being the brand you slot when you cannot justify slotting anything else, and being correct in that decision more often than not.

## Bonuses Detail

### 1-Piece: +5% Weapon Damage

Weapon Damage is the most universally valuable damage stat in the game because it multiplies every other layer of the damage formula. A flat +5% from a single brand piece is among the cleanest one-piece bonuses available, with no conditional triggers, no decay, no setup. Slot the piece, gain the damage.

This makes one-piece Walker the default flex slot for almost every endgame build that needs to fill a sixth gear piece without disrupting an existing brand stack. Running three-piece Česká Výroba and two-piece Fenris? The sixth slot is one-piece Walker. Running three-piece Sokolov and two-piece Petrov? Sixth slot, one-piece Walker. The pattern repeats across the meta.

### 2-Piece: +5% Critical Hit Chance

Five points of CHC at the two-piece tier is, on paper, slightly weaker than Česká Výroba's eight-point one-piece. In practice, Walker's two-piece is more accessible because the player has already invested in a Walker piece for the Weapon Damage bonus, so the second piece comes essentially free. The real value of the two-piece is that it stacks naturally on top of CHC core attributes and other crit-source brand bonuses without any wasted overhead.

For builds that hover just below the 60% CHC soft cap, two-piece Walker is the most reliable way to push the cap without burning a recalibration session.

### 3-Piece: +20% Critical Hit Damage

Twenty points of CHD at the three-piece tier is the highest CHD bonus in any brand's three-piece slot in TU22.1. This is the reason Walker shows up in pure-DPS dedicated builds where every other slot is contributing to the same multiplier stack. The interaction with Headshot Damage from other layers makes the +20% feel even larger in practice than the raw number suggests.

## Best Use Cases

Walker is the answer for any build that needs reliable damage scaling without committing to a specialized brand identity. The most common use cases are:

Generic four-red DPS chassis where the player wants every slot working toward damage. Hybrid two-piece slot for builds that already have a three-piece committed elsewhere. Bullet King and Bighorn LMG builds, where the lack of magazine bonuses on Walker is a non-issue because the weapons themselves bypass the reload economy. Pestilence DoT stacks, where Weapon Damage is the primary scaling layer.

Walker is less useful for status-effect skill builds, tank builds and any archetype that does not lean on critical hits. The brand's stat layout assumes a crit-stacking red build as the baseline.

## Items in Brand

Walker, Harris & Co. covers all six gear slots: mask, backpack, chest, gloves, holster and kneepads. The stat pools roll cleanly across all slots, with Weapon Damage and CHD as the most common attribute rolls. CHC, Headshot Damage and core damage attributes are also well-represented.

The named pieces in the Walker catalog are utility-focused rather than build-defining. The brand's role in the meta is as the chassis, not the centerpiece.

### Walker, Harris & Co. Mask (Composure)

Mask-slot named piece with the Composure talent, restoring CHC after damage dealt while in cover.

### Walker, Harris & Co. Backpack (Vigilance)

Backpack-slot named piece with the Vigilance talent, granting +25% Weapon Damage when health is full.

### Walker, Harris & Co. Holster (Concussion)

Holster-slot named piece, useful for headshot-heavy builds.

These pieces are workmanlike rather than game-changing, and that is consistent with Walker's brand identity overall.

## Pros & Cons

**Pros:**

- Universally applicable across nearly every red build archetype

- Highest CHD three-piece bonus in TU22.1 brand catalog
- Easy to slot one or two pieces as flex without disrupting a primary brand stack
- Drops are common, gear acquisition is forgiving

## FAQ

### Is three-piece Walker better than three-piece Česká Výroba?

For pure CHD output, Walker's +20% three-piece is the highest in the game. Česká Výroba's +15% three-piece is lower on paper, but Česká Výroba compensates with the +20% Magazine Size at two-piece and the Equalizer chest. Top-tier builds typically mix both brands rather than picking one outright.

### Should I run Walker if I am using a shotgun?

Probably not. Shotgun builds rarely benefit from CHC and CHD layers because shotgun damage is dominated by the close-range damage multiplier and pellet count. There are better brand choices for shotgun specialists.

### Does Walker's Weapon Damage bonus stack with core Weapon Damage attributes?

Yes. The brand bonus is additive to the same Weapon Damage layer that core attributes contribute to. Stacking is straightforward.

### Can I slot four pieces of Walker for additional bonuses?

No. The Division 2 brand bonus system caps at three pieces per brand, so a fourth Walker piece grants only its raw stat rolls without any additional brand bonus.

### How does Walker compare to Wyvern Wear?

Wyvern Wear is a skill-builder brand and does not compete in the DPS category. Comparing the two is apples to oranges. Run Walker for damage; run Wyvern for skill output.

### Is Walker viable in PvP?

Yes, very. CHC, CHD and Weapon Damage all apply cleanly in PvP. Walker is one of the most reliable DPS brand choices for Conflict and Dark Zone PvP encounters.
