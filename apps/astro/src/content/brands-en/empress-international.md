## Empress International - Status & Skill Hybrid Brand

Last reviewed: 2026-04-25 · TU22.1 · Verified vs in-game

Empress International sits in a unique corner of the brand roster: it doesn't lock you into pure skill damage, and it doesn't push you into raw weapon DPS. Instead, it leans hard into status effects and skill output, which makes it one of the most useful supporting brands in the game. If you build around bleed, burn, blind, shock, ensnare or disrupt, Empress almost always earns a slot.

### At a Glance

- 1pc: +10% Status Effects
- 2pc: +10% Skill Damage
- 3pc: +18% Status Duration
- Role: Status / Skill hybrid
- Tier: A for status builds, B+ for general skill
- Best slot count: 2pc or 3pc
- Synergies: Future Initiative, Hard Wired, Ongoing Directive, Sleeper, Ridgeway's Pride

The brand's identity is "make status stick longer and hit harder." That makes it powerful for hybrid loadouts where you don't want to commit to four pieces of a single skill set but still want your applied effects to matter.

### Bonuses Detail

The 1pc +10% Status Effects bonus is the keystone. This stat increases the magnitude and chance of applied statuses across the board: bleed damage ticks higher, burn DOT scales up, blind duration ramps, shock duration extends, and ensnare slows are stronger. Every status-leaning build wants this stat capped, and Empress is one of the few sources outside Hana-U gloves and Electrique that gives a flat 10% per slot.

The 2pc +10% Skill Damage adds usable skill output. It is not on the level of full skill brands, but combined with a backpack talent like Calculated or a chest like Vile, it pushes mid-tier skill numbers into respectable territory. For Striker drone, Sniper turret, Firestarter, Foam Chem, Stinger Hive and Achilles seekers, that 10% is meaningful damage scaling.

The 3pc +18% Status Duration is the closer. Duration is what makes status builds feel different from regular DPS: a longer bleed means more ticks, a longer blind means safer reloads and reposition, a longer disrupt means an enemy can't return fire while your team focuses them. 18% is a sizable chunk and stacks with backpack rolls, mod rolls and Glasscannon-style modifiers.

### Best Use Cases

Empress shines in three archetypes:

1. Ongoing Directive bleed builds. Pair Empress chest and holster with Sokolov mask for crit and Petrov gloves for weapon damage. Status Effects increase bleed pressure, Skill Damage amps your defensive seeker mines, and Status Duration keeps every enemy bleeding past the kill window.
2. Foam / Stinger / Firestarter status spam. The 2pc skill damage matters here because chem launcher and hive both scale on skill damage. The 1pc and 3pc make foam, ensnare, blind and burn last meaningfully longer.
3. Hybrid skill+gun builds where you don't want full Hana-U. Empress 3pc + 3pc Hana-U is a classic combination that gives you skill haste, skill repair, skill damage, status effects and status duration in one tidy package.

It is not the right pick for pure Marksman, pure LMG, pure shotgun or pure SMG glass-cannon builds. Those want Petrov, Airaldi, Providence or Wyvern.

### Items in Brand

Empress drops the standard six gear slots: mask, backpack, chest, gloves, holster, kneepads. Notable rolls per slot:

- Mask: rolls Headshot Damage, Critical Hit Chance, Skill Haste
- Backpack: rolls Skill Damage, Status Effects, Critical Hit Damage
- Chest: rolls Weapon Damage, Skill Damage, Armor
- Gloves: rolls Weapon Damage, Critical Hit Chance, Skill Damage
- Holster: rolls Critical Hit Damage, Status Effects, Skill Haste
- Kneepads: rolls Hazard Protection, Skill Damage, Armor

For status builds, prioritize Status Effects rolls on backpack and holster. For skill builds, prioritize Skill Damage on chest, backpack and gloves. The brand is unusually flexible because both stat lines appear on multiple slots.

### Pros & Cons

Pros:
- Unique stat combination not duplicated elsewhere
- Useful in both status and skill archetypes
- Flexible roll table - few wasted item drops
- Enables hybrid builds that don't sacrifice raw damage

Cons:
- 2pc Skill Damage is modest compared to dedicated skill brands
- Falls off in pure weapon DPS builds
- Status Duration is wasted on enemies who die instantly to crit chains
- Competes with Hana-U for skill loadouts and Electrique for status loadouts

### FAQ

Q: Is Empress better than Electrique for status builds?
A: They overlap heavily. Electrique has higher 2pc skill damage (20%) and 3pc skill cooldown, which favors active skill use. Empress has +18% Status Duration on 3pc, which favors damage-over-time pressure. For Ongoing Directive bleed, Empress wins. For Foam / Firestarter active rotation, Electrique wins.

Q: Does +10% Status Effects increase status proc chance or damage?
A: Both. The stat is a multiplier on status damage magnitude and on the proc chance of applied effects. So bleed ticks harder and applies more reliably.

Q: Should I run 2pc or 3pc Empress?
A: 2pc gives the best ratio if you only need Status Effects + Skill Damage. The 3pc Status Duration is worth the third slot only if your build relies on long DOT windows or long crowd control durations.

Q: What sidearm pairs best with Empress?
A: Carbine 7 with Pummel for stack-stacking, or Liberty for crit weapons. The brand doesn't change sidearm choice directly, but Carbine 7's bleed bonus stacks well with the duration buff.

Q: Is Empress viable in Heroic and Legendary?
A: Yes for status pressure and hybrid builds. In Legendary, the longer status duration matters more because enemies are tankier and you need every tick.

### Closing

Empress International is one of the most underrated brands in the post-TU22 meta. It doesn't dominate any single archetype the way Petrov dominates LMG or Airaldi dominates Marksman, but it slots into more builds than almost any other brand. If you run status, hybrid skill, Ongoing Directive, Negotiator's tagging, or any build where applied effects matter more than raw bullet damage, Empress earns its place. Keep at least one full set in your stash with rolls on Status Effects, Skill Damage and Skill Haste - you will use it.
