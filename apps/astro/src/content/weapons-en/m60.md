## At a Glance

The M60 is the heavy hitter of the base LMG family in The Division 2. Where the Stoner offers balance and the HK121 chases rate of fire, the M60 trades cadence for raw per-bullet damage. At 80,000 base damage and only 500 RPM, every shot lands like a hammer blow, and the 1.65x headshot multiplier is the highest in the LMG roster. A 100-round belt keeps you in the fight as long as your finger holds the trigger, but recoil punishes greedy bursts and rewards short, controlled taps.

In TU22.1 the M60 has quietly become one of the strongest options for sustained-damage builds. The damage buff that landed in October 2025 nudged its per-shot value above the Negotiator's Dilemma exotic at typical Heroic ranges, and brand sets that scale with weapon damage push it into territory normally reserved for marksman rifles. If you have ever wanted an LMG that feels like a 7.62 mm sledgehammer, this is it.

### Damage math

Sustained DPS at 500 RPM equals 500 / 60 multiplied by 80,000, giving 666,666 raw DPS. Apply a typical Heroic crit profile with 60 percent crit chance and 90 percent crit damage and the M60 climbs above 950,000 DPS before talents. Headshots scale to 1.65x, so a precise player who can land 50 percent of shots on red bars can break 1.4 million DPS without exotic gear.

## Talent slot

The M60 carries a single mod talent slot. The strongest rolls in TU22.1:

- **Optimist** - up to 35 percent weapon damage as the magazine empties. The M60 has the best per-shot damage in the family, so each Optimist tick is worth more here than on any other LMG.
- **Steady Handed** - 25 percent recoil reduction. Useful because the M60 has the heaviest base recoil in the LMG roster.
- **Boomerang** - heal on critical-hit damage. Niche but viable on builds that ignore Vigilance.

The community consensus is Optimist for damage, Steady Handed only if your build has no handling rolls. Avoid Killer on belt-fed weapons because the killer-to-killer chain is hard to maintain at this RPM.

## Top Builds

### 1. Striker M60 (PvE Heroic)

Striker stacks reward consistent hits, and the M60's slow cadence means you almost never lose stacks to overshoot. A four-piece Striker brand bonus with a Coyote's Mask, Memento backpack, and Holster delivers a plug-and-play setup that hits harder than any other LMG configuration. Expected output: 1.7 to 2.1 million DPS sustained.

### 2. Glass Cannon Burnout

Glass Cannon's plus-25 percent damage out, plus-50 percent damage in trade is extreme but rewarding. With Determined or Vigilance on the chest and a Memento backpack, the M60 reaches roughly 2.5 million DPS in a 10-second window. Use only when teammates can keep aggro off you.

### 3. Hunter's Fury Skirmish

For Conflict and Dark Zone, Hunter's Fury empowers fast pushes with armor regeneration on melee. The M60 sits oddly in PvP because of its low fire rate, but the per-shot damage is high enough that even three connecting bullets break shields and finish low-armor scouts. Steady Handed is mandatory in this build.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, the M60 is the boss-killer LMG. Its long range and high per-shot value mean that a single magazine usually chunks through the entire armor pool of a yellow elite. On Legendary content, the M60 is a top-tier choice for cleaners and outcasts because their armor segments are wide enough to forgive the slow cadence. The 4.5-second reload feels long, but the 100-round belt rarely runs dry before targets fall.

In PvP, the M60 has carved out a small niche. Time-to-kill on a 6-million-armor opponent is roughly 0.7 seconds at point-blank range, which is competitive with rifles. The risk is that your low fire rate gives an SMG or shotgun user a decisive answer if you miss the first burst. The M60 punishes mistakes harder than any other LMG, so only experienced PvP players should bring it.

## FAQ

### Is the M60 better than the Stoner LMG?

For pure damage, yes. The M60 outclasses the Stoner by roughly 12 to 15 percent in sustained DPS at typical Heroic ranges. The Stoner trades that damage for a flatter recoil curve and faster handling, so if you struggle with recoil control the Stoner remains the safer pick.

### What attachment perks should I roll?

Critical Hit Damage on the muzzle, Critical Hit Chance on the optic, and Headshot Damage on the magazine. If you are running Striker, swap the magazine perk for Damage to Targets Out of Cover.

### Does the M60 work with Negotiator's Dilemma marks?

Yes, the M60 applies and consumes marks normally, but Negotiator's Dilemma is its own exotic LMG, not a chest piece. They cannot be used together. If you want mark synergy, look at the Memento backpack or the Coyote's Mask.

### Why does Optimist outperform Killer on the M60?

Killer requires you to maintain a kill streak, which the M60's slow cadence struggles to do in target-rich environments. Optimist scales smoothly with magazine depletion, and the M60's belt size guarantees steady stack growth. Optimist out-damages Killer in real combat by roughly 8 to 12 percent.

## Closing

The M60 is the closest thing in The Division 2 to a marksman LMG. It rewards patience, accuracy, and short-burst discipline, and it punishes anyone who treats it like a Stoner. In the right hands it leads the LMG damage charts in PvE and remains the only LMG that PvP players take seriously. If you have a clean Optimist roll, build around it; the rest of the kit slots in around the weapon, not the other way around.
