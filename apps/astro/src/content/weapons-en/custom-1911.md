## At a Glance

The Custom 1911 is the upgraded, match-grade variant of the standard M1911 — a tuned, hand-fitted .45 ACP that takes everything the base 1911 does and sharpens it. With 110,000 base damage, 280 RPM, a 7-round magazine, and a class-leading 1.4-second reload, the Custom 1911 stands out as the best all-round 1911-platform pistol in The Division 2's loot pool. It hits harder than the M1911, fires faster, reloads quicker, and rolls the same talent pool. There is essentially no reason to use the standard M1911 over the Custom 1911 if both are available.

In the meta hierarchy, the Custom 1911 sits in the upper-middle of the pistol category. It does not compete with the 686 Magnum for raw per-shot damage, and it doesn't compete with the PF45 for sustained mag-dump output. Instead, it occupies a "best of both worlds" slot — high enough damage to act as a finisher pistol, fast enough cycling to feed Memento stacks, and a tight enough recoil pattern to deliver headshots reliably. For most build archetypes, the Custom 1911 is the default pistol pick when you don't have a strong reason to use something else.

Theoretical sustained DPS reaches approximately 513,000 before mods, which is the highest among standard semi-auto pistols and only beaten by the 686's per-shot ceiling on dedicated single-shot rotations. The Custom 1911's recoil profile is tighter than the M1911 — the upward kick is reduced, and shots stay closer to point-of-aim through a full magazine. This makes it one of the most accurate pistols in the game for tracking moving targets.

The 1.4-second reload is fastest in class. Reload-heavy builds (Memento, Pestilence, swap-cyclers) get genuine mileage out of this number — saving 0.1-0.2 seconds per reload cycle adds up across a Heroic mission. The 20 m optimal range is slightly extended over the M1911's 18 m, giving the Custom 1911 a small but meaningful advantage in mid-range duels.

## Talent

The Custom 1911 rolls the same standard pistol talent pool as the M1911, with **Perpetuation** as the most common high-value talent. Perpetuation grants pistol damage stacks on primary weapon reload, slotting cleanly into rotational builds.

**Pummel** is the alternative best-in-slot for rifle and marksman primary loadouts — the Custom 1911's pistol crit kills convert into rifle damage stacks, and its higher base damage means crit kills happen more reliably than with the M1911. **Vindictive** is solid for group play. Skip **Sadist** unless paired with a Bloodsucker setup, and avoid **Last Stand** entirely.

## Top Builds

The Custom 1911 fits well in several build archetypes:

**Striker Memento Hybrid:** A Striker chest, Memento backpack, Coyote's Mask build that uses the Custom 1911 as both stack-feeder and finisher. The 110k base damage is high enough to clean up wounded purples without primary swap, while the 7-round mag and 1.4s reload keep Memento cycling smooth.

**Sharpshooter Finisher:** Sharpshooter specialization with a marksman rifle primary and the Custom 1911 as a precision sidearm. The Custom 1911's tight recoil and 280 RPM make it excellent for picking off priority targets at mid-range when your MMR cycle is on cooldown or out of magazine.

**Hunter's Fury PvP Skirmisher:** In Dark Zone and Conflict, the Custom 1911 paired with Hunter's Fury delivers strong skirmisher pressure. Apply Fury procs with your primary, switch to the Custom 1911 to finish wounded opponents, then swap back to maintain proc uptime. The fast reload makes this rotation seamless.

**Pestilence Cycler:** Pestilence rewards weapon swaps, and the Custom 1911 is one of the best swap-target pistols thanks to its damage and reload profile. Apply Pestilence with primary, swap to Custom 1911 for clean-up shots, swap back to refresh stacks.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE, the Custom 1911 is a strong all-purpose pistol. It punches harder than the M1911 and PF45 for elite finishing, while still cycling fast enough to feed stack-based builds. On Heroic, expect headshots in the 8-12M range on a moderately rolled build, which is enough to clean up red-bars and chunk purples. On Legendary, it falls behind the 686 for elite per-shot output but stays competitive thanks to its talent slot.

In PvP, the Custom 1911 is one of the better sidearms for skirmisher and runner-style play. The 280 RPM and tight recoil deliver consistent damage at mid-range, and the 1.4s reload lets you re-engage faster than opponents using slower pistols. Where it loses is at extreme range against marksman builds, and at point-blank against high-RPM SMGs. Treat the Custom 1911 in PvP as a flexible mid-range tool — neither a sniper nor a CQB option, but excellent at everything in between.

## FAQ

**Is the Custom 1911 the best pistol in The Division 2?**
For all-round use, it's arguably the best pick — high damage, fast reload, tight recoil, versatile talent pool. The 686 beats it for per-shot ceiling and the PF45 beats it for sustained fire, but for a default pistol slot, the Custom 1911 is the strongest pick.

**Custom 1911 vs M1911 — what's the difference?**
The Custom 1911 wins on every stat: 110k vs 100k damage, 280 vs 250 RPM, 1.4s vs 1.5s reload. Same mag size and talent pool. There is no reason to use the M1911 if a Custom 1911 is available.

**Can the Custom 1911 one-tap headshot in PvE?**
Red-bars on Heroic, yes, with a clean headshot on a tuned build. Purples need significant amplified weapon damage and crit stacking — possible but not guaranteed. Yellow elites require multiple shots even at high gear scores.

**What talent should I prioritize?**
Perpetuation for rotational builds, Pummel for rifle primary loadouts. Both are strong picks; choose based on which primary you run.

## Closing

The Custom 1911 is the most flexible sidearm in The Division 2's standard pistol pool. It doesn't have a single defining strength — instead, it does everything competently and most things very well. For players who want a reliable default pistol that works across PvE and PvP, across Memento rotations and finisher slots, the Custom 1911 is the obvious pick. It rewards aim discipline without punishing imprecision, it cycles fast enough to keep stacks alive, and it hits hard enough to matter in combat. In a meta where pistol slots are increasingly important for stack management and Pestilence rotation, the Custom 1911's combination of damage, reload, and recoil makes it one of the most underrated weapons in the game.
