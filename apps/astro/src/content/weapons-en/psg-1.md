## At a Glance

The PSG-1 represents precision at its finest. At 220,000 base damage and 100 RPM, this weapon is deliberately slow—every shot is deliberate, every trigger pull meaningful. The 2.5x headshot multiplier is exceptional, producing 550,000 damage per critical hit. An 8-round magazine allows extended engagements without frequent reloads. The PSG-1 is a weak-point hunter's dream: locate vulnerable areas (headshots, weak spots), eliminate targets through precision rather than volume. This is the sharpshooter's weapon—one shot, one elimination, repeat.

## Stats & Performance

**Base Configuration:**
- **Damage:** 220,000 per shot
- **Fire Rate:** 100 RPM
- **Magazine Capacity:** 8 rounds
- **Reload Time:** 2.5 seconds
- **Headshot Multiplier:** 2.5x
- **Optimal Range:** 20–50+ meters

**Damage Output Breakdown:**

At 100 RPM, the PSG-1 generates 366 damage per second (time-to-empty + reload cycle). Total magazine damage is 1,760,000 (pure bodyshots), or 4,400,000 with full headshots. Magazine depletion takes 4.8 seconds at full fire rate, with reload cycles lasting 7.3 seconds.

The 2.5x headshot multiplier produces 550,000 damage per critical shot—the highest in the MMR category. Two crit headshots deliver 1,100,000 total damage (equivalent to 5–6 rifle magazine eliminations). Against single targets, the PSG-1's pure burst potential is unmatched.

## Talent Slot

The PSG-1 accepts **Marksman Rifle talents**. Suggested configurations include:

- **Ranger:** +1% damage per meter beyond 10m. At 30m, +20%; at 40m, +30%; at 50m, +40%. The damage scaling is exceptional and compounds with 220,000 base damage.
- **Headhunter:** +20% damage for 10 seconds after landing a headshot. Stacks if you land consecutive headshots. Maximizes crit build synergy.
- **In Sync:** +25% damage while in cover and looking at marked enemies. Excellent for group farming where marking is consistent.
- **Apex:** +30% damage to enemies with status effects applied. Pairs well with skill-heavy builds using stun/freeze/burn.

Ranger is the thematic choice for long-range weak-point hunting; Headhunter for sustained crit builds.

## Top Builds

**Long-Range Weak-Point Hunter (PvE)**
Stack Critical Hit Chance to 60% and Critical Hit Damage to +140%. Use Ranger talent. Engage at 30–50m distance, targeting headshots exclusively. Build for Weapon Damage rolls and use Marksman specialization for weapon handling. Rotation: identify target, aim for headshot, fire (550,000+ crit damage), eliminate target before repositioning. This playstyle suits Heroic content where you can maintain distance from adds.

**Headhunter Crit Chain (PvE/PvP)**
Use Headhunter as primary talent. Stack Critical Hit Chance (60%+) and Crit Damage (+140%). Build for magazine sustainability via Gunner specialization (armor per shot). Rotation: land crit headshot (Headhunter +20% activates for 10s), land second crit headshot (Headhunter stacks/refreshes), maintain uptime through consecutive crits. This playstyle enables exceptional sustained crit damage if accuracy remains consistent.

**Sniper-Support Group Role (PvE)**
Pair PSG-1 with a team focused on suppression/tanking. Build for consistent crit delivery rather than peak damage. Use Technician specialization for skill power. Your role becomes eliminating high-priority targets (Heavies, named enemies) while teammates handle adds. Stack Weapon Damage and Crit Chance to 55% for balanced output.

## PvE vs PvP Deployment

**PvE Dominance:**
The PSG-1 excels at all difficulty tiers. Against isolated elite targets (bosses, named enemies), the 2.5x headshot multiplier and 220,000 base damage enable efficient single-shot eliminations. In Legendary farming, the PSG-1 becomes the primary DPS weapon—let teammates suppress adds while you eliminate high-priority targets from range. A well-aimed headshot crit delivers 550,000 damage; two shots eliminate most bosses.

**PvE Sustainability:**
The 8-round magazine allows extended engagements without reloading. Against 3–4 elite targets, you can eliminate each with 2 headshots (6 of 8 rounds), then reload. This provides extended operational capability before resupplying ammunition.

**PvP Viability:**
In Conflict, the PSG-1 is a dedicated pick-tool. Against stationary targets or those in cover, a single crit headshot (550,000 damage) eliminates or severely wounds opponents. Dark Zone PvP favors the PSG-1 for sniper nest scenarios where distance and positioning are controllable.

**PvP Challenges:**
Against moving targets, the 100 RPM becomes a liability. You must lead targets accurately, anticipating movement. In CQB (close-quarters battles) or aggressive rushes, the PSG-1 is useless—swap to an AR or pistol. The weapon excels only when engagement distance exceeds 20m and targets are predictable.

## Pros & Cons

**Strengths:**
- 220,000 base damage is highest per-shot among marksman rifles
- 2.5x headshot multiplier is exceptional; 550,000 crit headshots unmatched
- 8-round magazine allows extended engagements without frequent reloads
- 100 RPM encourages deliberate, accurate shooting; spray prevention
- Ranger talent scaling is exceptional at 30m+ (up to +40% at 50m+)
- Exceptional burst damage for single-target elimination
- Weak-point hunting playstyle rewards precision and positioning
- Works effectively as primary in organized team compositions

**Weaknesses:**
- 100 RPM is extremely slow; cannot suppress multiple targets
- 8-round magazine, while extended for MMR, limits multi-target engagement
- 2.5x multiplier requires headshots; bodyshot damage is merely adequate (220,000)
- 2.5-second reload creates vulnerability in sustained firefights
- Requires exceptional aim discipline; missed shots are severely punished
- Terrible for crowd control; strictly single-target weak-point focused
- No unique passive; purely a talent platform
- Slow handling feels sluggish in fast-paced encounters

## FAQ

**Q: Should I use Ranger or Headhunter?**
Ranger for long-range (30m+) weak-point hunting. Headhunter for sustained crit chains (55%+ crit chance builds). Choose based on playstyle—precision placement (Ranger) vs consistent crit delivery (Headhunter).

**Q: Can PSG-1 solo Legendary?**
Yes, with discipline. The 550,000 crit headshot damage eliminates elite targets efficiently. However, you must maintain distance from adds; CQB overwhelms the PSG-1. Best used as secondary while teammates suppress adds.

**Q: What's the optimal range for PSG-1?**
20–50m. Below 20m, precision becomes difficult and adds pressure you. Above 50m, bullet travel becomes noticeable. The sweet spot is 30–40m where Ranger scaling is maximum and target visibility is excellent.

**Q: Does PSG-1 work in group Heroics?**
Excellent. Assign yourself weak-point elimination role. Focus on eliminating named enemies and Heavies while teammates suppress adds. This division of labor maximizes group efficiency.

**Q: Is PSG-1 better than other marksman rifles?**
Yes, for raw burst damage. PSG-1 has the highest per-shot output and headshot multiplier. Other MMRs suit sustained DPS roles; PSG-1 is strictly peak burst.

## Closing Thoughts

The PSG-1 is a *precision weak-point hunter for players who embrace slow, deliberate shooting*. Its 220,000 base damage and 2.5x headshot multiplier create exceptional crit potential unmatched by rifles. The 100 RPM is intentionally slow—this weapon punishes spray tactics and rewards accuracy. In Heroic/Legendary content with organized team support, the PSG-1 excels at eliminating high-priority targets. In solo CQB or PvP spray-downs, it falters. But for what it does—deliver devastating headshot crits at range—the PSG-1 stands unrivaled. If you value precision over speed and can maintain consistent headshot accuracy, the PSG-1 belongs in your loadout.
