## At a Glance

The Bighorn is the multi-target king of the Exotic AR roster. Where most assault rifles reward you for tunneling into a single yellow bar, the Bighorn punishes that habit and rewards spread-fire engagement instead. Its 3-round burst trigger, paired with a stacking damage buff that requires you to hit *unique* targets, turns every group of trash mobs into a chain reaction of escalating damage. By the time you reach the third stack you are pumping out roughly +60% Weapon Damage on top of an already healthy 39,000 base — and that buff lasts five full seconds, which in practice is enough time to delete an entire patrol.

This is not a sniper-substitute and it is not a single-target raid weapon. It is a horde-clear tool, a Strikers engine, and the Exotic that finally makes the often-overlooked "spray and pray" archetype competitive in TU22.1. If your build philosophy is "more red bars dead per second," the Bighorn is built for you.

### Handling Profile

The Bighorn fires in 3-round bursts at an effective sustained cadence of roughly 233 bursts per minute. Recoil per burst is moderate — the muzzle climbs vertically with very little horizontal sway, which means that once you settle the first round on chest height the second and third land naturally on neck and head. This makes the headshot multiplier easier to capitalize on than it looks on paper, especially at mid range.

The 30-round magazine gives you exactly 10 bursts per mag, and at 2.0 seconds the reload is on the faster end of the AR class. With a properly built Strikers chest piece (Bullet Hell) you may not need to reload between groups at all, since the magazine refill on missed shots keeps the gun fed during target switching.

### Damage Floor and Ceiling

At gear score 60 with a 4-piece Strikers core and properly rolled WD attributes, the per-bullet damage floor lands around 165k–185k on a body shot against a non-armored target at optimal range. With 3 stacks of Apex Predator active and a headshot multiplier of x1.65, individual rounds can crack 600k+ in chained engagements. Three rounds per burst means a single trigger pull at full stack can deal 1.8M+ to a single elite — and you are pulling that trigger ten times before you reload.

## Talent — Apex Predator

> Hitting 3 unique targets within 2 seconds grants +20% Weapon Damage for 5 seconds. Stacks up to 3 times (+60% WD).

Apex Predator is what defines the gun. The talent does not care about kills, headshots, or critical hits — only that you tag three different enemies within a two-second window. Each successful "tag" of three uniques refreshes and adds a stack, up to a cap of three (+60% total). The buff window is 5 seconds, which is generous, but in practice you will be spreading shots constantly to keep all three stacks alive.

### How to use it correctly

The instinctive way to play an AR is to dump the mag into one priority target. That is the exact wrong way to use the Bighorn. Instead, treat the first burst of each engagement as a "tagging" burst — fire one burst into target A, swap to target B, swap to target C, and watch the buff icon hit 3 stacks. From that moment on you have a five-second window to dump the rest of the magazine into whoever needs to die first, with a flat +60% damage multiplier active.

In group content this becomes trivial because targets are everywhere. In solo content, even 4 red bars in a normal-difficulty patrol is enough to keep stacks rolling. The talent shines hardest in clearing Underground, Summit floors with high enemy density, and Countdown rounds.

### What breaks the rotation

- Shooting the same target twice in a row does not progress the stack
- Hitting an already-tagged target inside the 2-second window does not refresh
- Killing a tagged target before tagging two more loses you the contribution
- Dropping below 3 stacks resets only the missing tier, not the whole buff

## Top Builds

### Bighorn × Strikers Battlegear (the meta pick)

This is the de-facto chain-clear build for solo and group PvE.

- Brand: 4-piece Strikers (Battlegear)
- Chest: Bullet Hell (Strikers high-end, magazine refill on missed shots)
- Backpack: Vigilance or Striker brand backpack with Spotter
- Talents: Vigilance (chest) + Spotter (backpack) — pure WD stacking on top of Strikers
- Specialization: Gunner for the +10% WD signature passive
- Skills: Striker Drone + Restorer Hive (or Bulwark shield for solo Legendary)
- Attributes: 6/6 weapon damage rolls on every gear piece
- Stat priority: WD > CHC > CHD > AR damage

The synergy here is dirty. Strikers gives up to +60% WD at full stack, Bighorn gives +60% WD at full Apex Predator stack, and missed shots from Bullet Hell continuously refill the magazine. Combined with Gunner spec you are running a permanent ~+150% WD multiplier in active engagements, with no mag management. Effective sustained DPS on multi-target packs sits in the 5–7M range, with peaks far higher when bursts crit.

### Bighorn × Hunter's Fury

A more aggressive, cover-to-cover style build. Hunter's Fury rewards you for vaulting cover, throwing grenades, and getting close — none of which the Bighorn has any trouble doing thanks to its 25–40m optimal range. Each cover-vault grants stacking buffs that pair beautifully with Apex Predator. This build is slightly less DPS than Strikers but significantly more survivable in solo Legendary, and the playstyle feels closer to a CQB rifle than a pure spray gun.

## PvE Performance

The Bighorn is currently a top-3 multi-target Exotic AR in the directive-heavy PvE meta. It outperforms most legendary AR options in any scenario where 3+ enemies are present at once, which is essentially every Stronghold, every Summit floor above 25, and every Legendary mission. In single-target boss fights it falls behind dedicated DPS choices like the St. Elmo's Engine, but even there a competent player can keep stacks alive against the boss + adds + drones, keeping the gun within 70–80% of dedicated single-target ARs.

### Where it dominates

- Underground operations on Heroic+
- Countdown rounds 1–4
- Summit floors with 4+ red bars per room
- Manhunt patrol cleanup
- Control point liberations on Challenging+

### Where it struggles

- Pure single-target boss DPS phases
- Legendary boss rooms with only one priority target
- Long-range engagements past 50m (falloff hits hard)

## PvP Performance

In Conflict and Skirmish, the Bighorn is a niche pick. Its TTK on a single agent is mediocre because Apex Predator cannot easily stack against just 1 visible enemy. In Conflict modes with active rotations and multiple line-of-sight contacts it can punch surprisingly hard, but most PvP players will be better served by a flat-damage AR like the Eagle Bearer or a high-recoil rifle like the LWM4.

In the Dark Zone the picture is brighter. Multiple rogue agents in a chain engagement, NPC packs, and sudden bracket fights all favor a stack-on-tag weapon. If you run DZ regularly and like the messier side of the mode, Bighorn is genuinely competitive there.

## FAQ

### Does Apex Predator work on environmental targets, drones, or pets?

Yes for hostile drones, hostile robotic dogs, turrets, and any tagged hostile entity. No for friendly skills, neutral animals, or destructibles like cars. NPC summons (chargers, named tank adds) count as unique targets as long as they have separate health bars.

### Does headshot multiplier apply to all three rounds in a burst?

Yes. Each round in the 3-round burst is calculated independently for headshot damage. If you land all three on the head you get x1.65 on every round, which is where the gun's nuclear potential really lives.

### Is the Bighorn worth crafting if I already have Eagle Bearer?

Yes — they fill different roles. Eagle Bearer is the universal flat-damage AR that works anywhere. Bighorn is the specialist multi-target weapon. Most endgame players run Eagle Bearer for bosses and Bighorn for clear phases, or even swap between them mid-mission.

### Can I keep all 3 stacks active during long boss fights?

Only if there are adds on the field. Pure boss-only encounters cap your stacks at zero, since you cannot tag three uniques. In raid-style encounters with constant add waves, full stacks are easy to maintain.

### What attributes should I prioritize on Bighorn itself?

Weapon Damage, Critical Hit Chance, Critical Hit Damage — in that order. Magazine size is irrelevant because Bullet Hell solves it. Reload speed is a luxury; the base 2.0s reload is already very fast.

### Does the burst-fire trigger interact with reload-on-empty perks?

Yes. Reload talents that proc on empty mag trigger normally with the Bighorn — the burst trigger does not change empty-mag detection.

### Will the Bighorn stay strong after TU22.1?

The talent has not been touched in the current PTS data and the multi-target meta is the dominant playstyle for Heroic+ and Legendary content. Expect it to remain top-tier for the foreseeable patch cycle.

## Closing

The Bighorn is the rare Exotic that genuinely changes the way you play. Pick it up if you want a weapon that rewards target awareness, if you live in group PvE, or if you have ever felt that single-target ARs are boring after the third hour of grinding the same boss room. Pair it with Strikers, drop into Heroic Underground, and watch entire rooms evaporate three bursts at a time.
