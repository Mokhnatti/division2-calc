## At a Glance

The Glock 19 is The Division 2's highest-RPM standard service pistol — a 9mm polymer-framed semi-automatic with the fastest cyclic rate (320 RPM), largest magazine (17 rounds), and shortest reload (1.4s) among the base pistol pool. The trade-off for all this volume is the lowest base damage of any standard pistol at 75,000. The Glock 19 is, in essence, the "machine pistol of pistols" — a sidearm that fires almost like an SMG when held down, sacrificing per-shot ceiling for a relentless stream of rounds.

In TU22.1, the Glock 19 fills the most extreme rotational pistol niche. If the PF45 is "high mag, moderate damage" and the P226 is "high RPM, moderate damage and capacity," the Glock 19 is "highest RPM, highest mag, lowest damage." This makes it the absolute best Memento stack-feeder, the best Pestilence tick-applier, and the best sustained-pressure pistol in the standard pool. It will never compete for per-shot damage, but for stack-management and continuous-fire builds, the Glock 19 is the gold standard.

Theoretical sustained DPS reaches approximately 400,000 before mods, achievable in real engagements thanks to the 17-round mag and 1.4s reload. The recoil profile is light — the 9mm chambering and Glock's polymer frame keep vertical kick minimal, and the 320 RPM rate doesn't outpace recoil recovery. This makes the Glock 19 one of the most controllable pistols in the game, even at sustained fire.

The 16 m optimal range is the shortest in the standard pistol pool, and falloff hits hard — at 45 m, damage drops to roughly 50% of base, which puts headshots in the 5-6M range on a moderately rolled build. The Glock 19 is firmly a close-quarters tool, and using it outside its optimal range surrenders most of its damage potential.

## Talent

The Glock 19 rolls the standard pistol talent pool with no fixed talent on standard rarity drops. **Perpetuation** is the dominant meta pick — its pistol damage stacks on primary reload synergize beautifully with the Glock's high cyclic rate and large mag.

**Pummel** is also strong, especially for rifle and marksman builds where the Glock 19's high crit-kill frequency means rapid rifle stack accumulation. **Vindictive** is solid for group play, granting allies bonus crit chance after pistol hits. Skip **Sadist** and **Last Stand** rolls.

The Glock 19's 320 RPM means it generates more pistol shots per second than any other standard pistol, which directly translates to more talent-trigger opportunities. Talents that proc on hit, kill, or stack accumulation all benefit disproportionately from the Glock's fire rate.

## Top Builds

The Glock 19 fits naturally into several builds:

**Maximum Memento Cycler:** Coyote's Mask, Memento backpack, Striker chest, and the Glock 19 as the dedicated stack-feeder. The 17-round mag and 320 RPM mean Memento stacks refresh faster than with any other pistol, and the 1.4s reload keeps cycle uptime maximized.

**Pestilence Spreader:** Pestilence rewards rapid tick application across multiple targets, and the Glock 19's mag size and RPM let you spread the parasite across a group faster than any other pistol. Pair with a high-DPS rifle primary for the actual damage application.

**Heartbreaker Sustained Pressure:** Heartbreaker chest piece thrives on continuous fire, and the Glock 19 is the perfect secondary slot to maintain Heartbreaker proc uptime during primary reloads.

**Hunter's Fury Skirmisher:** In Dark Zone and Conflict, Hunter's Fury with the Glock 19 lets you maintain pressure on multiple opponents through extended engagements. The mag size means you stay in the fight longer than enemies with smaller pistol mags.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE, the Glock 19 is a pure rotational pistol. It will never carry damage on Heroic or Legendary content as a finisher, but it serves as the best stack-feeder in the game for builds that need to maintain Memento, Pestilence, or Heartbreaker procs. Its role is supportive, and on stack-heavy builds it pulls genuine weight despite the modest damage numbers.

In PvP, the Glock 19 is a sneaky strong CQB sidearm. The 320 RPM is fast enough to compete with SMGs at point-blank range, and the 17-round mag means you outlast opponents in extended skirmishes. The fast reload lets you re-engage quickly after dropping behind cover. Where the Glock loses is at any range beyond 20 m — falloff hits hard, and the low per-shot damage means you lose trades against marksman pistols and rifles. Treat the Glock 19 in PvP as a CQB-only tool.

## FAQ

**Glock 19 vs P226 — which is better?**
The Glock 19 has higher RPM (320 vs 300), faster reload (1.4 vs 1.5), and larger mag (17 vs 15), but lower base damage (75k vs 80k). For pure rotational use, the Glock 19 wins. For slightly more per-shot punch, the P226 edges ahead.

**Is the Glock 19 viable in CQB PvP?**
Yes, surprisingly so. The 320 RPM and 17-round mag give it real teeth at point-blank range, and the fast reload supports aggressive play. It loses at range, but in CQB it competes with SMGs.

**Can the Glock 19 one-tap red-bars on Heroic?**
With a tuned build and a clean headshot, yes — but it's the least likely standard pistol to one-tap given the 75k base damage. Use it for sustained chip damage rather than per-shot finishing.

**What's the best talent for the Glock 19?**
Perpetuation for swap-cycle builds, Pummel for rifle primary builds. Both proc more frequently on the Glock 19 than slower pistols thanks to the high RPM.

## Closing

The Glock 19 is The Division 2's "machine pistol" — a sidearm built for sustained fire, stack management, and rotational pressure rather than per-shot damage. It is the best Memento feeder, the best Pestilence applier, and one of the best Heartbreaker sustainers in the standard pistol pool. It will not carry your damage chart, but it will keep your build's stack mechanics alive across long engagements, and that supportive role is essential to TU22.1's optimized DPS builds. If you run a stack-heavy build and you find a clean Perpetuation Glock 19, it deserves a serious look as your secondary — it does its niche better than any other standard pistol in the game.
