## At a Glance

The 686 Magnum is the heaviest hitter in The Division 2's pistol pool — a six-shot magnum revolver with the highest base damage of any sidearm in the game. Where the rest of the pistol category trades raw damage for sustained fire, the 686 sits firmly on the opposite end: each round drops a quarter-bar from a Black Tusk Heavy on Heroic, and a clean headshot can decapitate a red-bar in a single trigger pull all the way through Legendary on most weeks. It's the sidearm you reach for when you need stopping power, not volume.

In terms of role, the 686 functions as an "exotic-feel" baseline weapon: low capacity, slow cyclic, brutal per-shot output. Its 200 RPM ceiling and 6-round cylinder mean you cannot spray it — every press of the trigger has to count. The 2.5-second reload is the longest among standard pistols, which reinforces that pacing. Players who learn to treat the 686 as a precision tool rather than an emergency sidearm get enormous mileage out of it, especially on Sharpshooter and Survivalist specializations that synergize with marksman behaviour.

Theoretical sustained DPS sits around 733,000 before mods, which is misleading because the 686 will never realistically hit theoretical DPS — the cylinder empties in 1.8 seconds and the reload eats more time than the burst. What matters is per-shot damage and headshot value: a 1.0 weapon damage headshot from a moderately rolled build (≈ 4M weapon damage) lands around 16-18M against a Heroic Heavy, which is enough to one-tap most red and most purple targets in PvE.

Recoil on the 686 is sharp and vertical with a small horizontal kick on shots 3-5. Standard practice is a quick re-acquire between trigger pulls rather than fighting the cylinder rhythm, since the 200 RPM cap is comfortable for hand-paced shooting anyway. Compensator and short-barrel mods favour stability, while extended barrels favour optimal range — the 686 is one of the few pistols that genuinely benefits from a 30 m+ engagement window.

## Talent

The 686 Magnum rolls one of the standard pistol talent pools, with **Perpetuation** as the most common high-value roll. Perpetuation grants a stack of pistol damage when you reload your primary weapon, encouraging weapon-swap rotations. **Liberty's brother in spirit** without being a named exotic, the 686 also rolls Pummel, Sadist, and Vindictive on certain drops.

Talent best-in-slot for endgame is generally **Pummel** when paired with a high-velocity primary (since Pummel converts pistol crit kills into rifle damage stacks), though Perpetuation is the cleaner option for mag-dump SMG and AR builds where you reload constantly. Avoid Sadist on the 686: the bleed-trigger requirement clashes with the low fire rate, and Sadist's 35% damage stack is wasted when you're already one-tapping targets.

## Top Builds

The 686 Magnum slots best into three distinct build archetypes:

**Glass Cannon Sharpshooter:** 4-piece Striker, Memento backpack, Coyote's Mask, Sharpshooter spec. The 686 acts as a bonus headshot pistol when stacks need ramping or when you need to one-tap a sniper without breaking your rifle's stack progression. Multiplicative crit damage from Coyote and the Sharpshooter tree pushes 686 headshots into the 25M+ region on Heroic.

**Memento Stack Maintenance:** A pure utility build where the 686 is used to cash trinkets at extreme range. Pair with Perfectly Optimist on a primary marksman rifle or use as a pistol-only specialist run. Memento's stack mechanic favours per-shot damage, and the 686 turns each trigger pull into a guaranteed stack addition.

**Pestilence Reload Cycler:** Pestilence builds reward weapon swaps, and the 686 Magnum's 2.5-second reload is offset by the fact that you swap to it for one or two damaging shots, then swap back. Run with Heartbreaker chest piece or Ridgeway's Pride for resilience while you finish the kill.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE, the 686 Magnum is a specialist tool. It is not your damage primary; it is your "elite finisher." Heroic and Legendary content rewards per-shot precision, and the 686 lets you snipe veteran rushers without breaking position. Where it falls flat is in waves of red-bar trash — the 6-round cylinder runs dry quickly and the reload window leaves you exposed. Keep an SMG or AR primary for chip damage and use the 686 for priority targets.

In PvP (Dark Zone, Conflict), the 686 is a powerhouse in skilled hands. The 220,000 base damage scales aggressively with weapon damage rolls, and a clean headshot at mid-range can drop a glass-cannon agent in a single trigger pull. The drawback is the engagement window: in close-quarters Dark Zone fights the 200 RPM cap is too slow, and you'll lose to SMG users in face-to-face rolls. Treat the 686 in PvP as a counter-sniper sidearm rather than a CQB option.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Highest base damage of any pistol (220,000) — outclasses Custom 1911 and most named pistols
- Excellent headshot multiplier scaling — 1.5× on top of build crit modifiers
- Strong talent pool, especially Perpetuation and Pummel
- Visually iconic and satisfying — heavy recoil arc rewards aim discipline

**Cons:**
- Tiny 6-round cylinder limits sustained engagements
- Long 2.5s reload — longest among standard pistols
- 200 RPM is too slow for CQB or panic situations
- No native talent at standard rarity drops — relies on rolled brand bonuses

## FAQ

**Is the 686 Magnum better than the Custom 1911?**
For per-shot damage, yes — 220k vs 110k base. For DPS, no — the 1911's 280 RPM and 7-round mag make it the better sustained option. Pick the 686 for finishers, the Custom 1911 for general-purpose pistol slots.

**Does the 686 benefit from extended magazine mods?**
There is no extended mag option for the 686 cylinder — the 6-round capacity is fixed. Focus on damage, optimal range, and reload speed mods instead.

**Can I one-shot Heroic enemies with the 686?**
Red-bar enemies, yes, with a clean headshot on a moderately rolled build. Purples and yellows require a build with significant amplified weapon damage and crit damage, but it is achievable on a tuned Sharpshooter setup.

**Should I use the 686 in solo Legendary?**
Not as your primary — the reload window is too punishing. As a Memento stack pistol or Pestilence-cycle tool, however, it earns its slot.

## Closing

The 686 Magnum is one of those rare weapons that defines a playstyle rather than fitting into one. It rewards patience, headshots, and weapon-swap rotations, and it punishes spray-and-pray panic shooting. If you build around its strengths — long range, single targets, surgical removal — it becomes one of the most satisfying sidearms in The Division 2. If you treat it like a backup pistol and pull it out under pressure, you'll get killed mid-reload. Learn its rhythm, pair it with the right talent, and the 686 will carry you through Heroic and Legendary content with style.
