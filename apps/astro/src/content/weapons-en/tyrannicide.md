## At a Glance

The Tyrannicide is a Named Marksman Rifle that lives at the intersection of raw single-shot lethality and aggressive tempo. Where most MMRs in The Division 2 force you to choose between damage per shot and rate of fire, Tyrannicide leans into both — a generous 100 RPM ceiling combined with a 200000 base damage chassis means it punches harder than rifles while keeping more of the tactical pace MMR fans expect.

Players gravitate to Tyrannicide for one reason: it makes headshots feel meaningful again. The 2.5x headshot multiplier is the standard MMR figure, but on a 200K base hit it translates into the kind of numbers that delete yellow-bar elites in two well-placed body-and-head combos. In TU22.1 it remains a top-tier pick for solo Heroic and Legendary content, mid-range Countdown anchors, and any build that wants reliable single-target deletion without committing to the slower pacing of a heavy sniper.

## Stats

### Core numbers

- **Base damage:** 200000 per shot
- **Rate of fire:** 100 RPM
- **Magazine:** 6 rounds
- **Reload:** 2.5 seconds (full)
- **Headshot multiplier:** 2.5x
- **Optimal range:** 40-80m
- **Damage falloff start:** ~50m
- **Aim down sights time:** ~0.35s

### Damage output

- **Body DPS (sustained):** ~333K
- **Headshot DPS (sustained):** ~833K
- **Burst (mag dump, headshots):** 6 shots x 500K = 3M in roughly 3.6s including the trigger pull cadence
- **Time-to-kill on a 1.2M HP red bar:** under one second with a clean head

### Handling

Tyrannicide is forgiving for an MMR. The recoil pattern is mostly vertical with a slight pull right on shot 4 onward, which makes it easier to walk back onto target than rifles like the Mk17 family. The 6-round mag is the only soft cap on its damage output — most players solve it with Extra magazines (Determined, Optimist) or a Big Game Hunter brand bonus.

## Talent

### Tyrannicide-specific talent

The exotic-tier talent on Tyrannicide rewards landing consecutive headshots. Each headshot stacks a damage buff up to a cap (typically 5 stacks at 10% each), and a body shot or a missed shot resets the stack to zero. At max stacks the rifle is doing roughly 50% more weapon damage per round, which on a 200K base translates to enormous one-shot potential against elites.

The design philosophy mirrors classic precision-rifle gameplay: punish the lazy, reward the patient. Pair it with stability mods and a Cool Headed/Determined frame and the talent practically pays for itself within the first magazine of any engagement.

### Practical tips

- Open every fight on a head — never burn the first stack on a body shot
- Reloads do not reset stacks, but missed shots do
- The buff persists across targets, so chain executes across a clump of reds before pivoting to a yellow

## Top Builds

### Glass Cannon Headhunter

Four-piece Striker plus Coyote's Mask plus Walker, Harris and Co. holster. Drop a Damage to Targets Out of Cover gear attribute, stack Headshot Damage on the chest and backpack, and let Tyrannicide carry the single-target while a Bullet King or 200% mag SMG handles ad clear. Expect 1.2-1.4M crits on a clean head against a red.

### Solo Heroic Anchor

Three-piece Striker plus three-piece Hunter's Fury plus Determined as the secondary AR. Tyrannicide is your headshot deletion tool, while Hunter's Fury feeds you bonuses every time you melt a target at close range. This works particularly well in narrow Countdown maps where you can cycle from CQB to a 50m sightline in seconds.

### PvP Sniper Lite

Empress Plate Carrier plus four-piece Picaro's Holster plus Sokolov chest. Push optimal range and damage to elites, then use Tyrannicide as a Conflict mid-range one-shot. The faster RPM gives it a real edge over Saber and Watchman in DZ duels where you need a quick follow-up.

## PvE-PvP

### PvE

In PvE, Tyrannicide sits in the upper tier of MMRs alongside Saber, Mantis and Seven Six Two. Its strength is consistency — the high RPM means you do not lose tempo when an elite dodges your first shot, and the magazine refreshes fast enough that you rarely get caught reloading mid-fight. It is a near-perfect pick for Summit floors 90-100, Legendary directives and any open-world bounty where you want elites gone before they ever reach cover.

The weakness is range. Beyond 80m it starts losing meaningful damage to falloff, which is why Watchman and Mantis still hold their place on long-sightline maps like Roosevelt Island. Inside 80m, however, Tyrannicide is the better tool nine times out of ten.

### PvP

Conflict and the Dark Zone reward Tyrannicide users who play patient. The 200K base damage tag-team with armor-on-kill timing means a single solid headshot plus a body follow-up is often a kill against Status Effect or Hard Wired loadouts. The 6-round magazine is more painful in PvP than PvE — once it is empty you are committed to the reload — so Extra magazines are basically required.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

## FAQ

### Is Tyrannicide better than Saber?

In raw per-shot damage, no — Saber's 300K base hits harder. But Tyrannicide fires at 100 RPM versus Saber's 80, so for sustained DPS and chasing moving targets, Tyrannicide pulls ahead. Pick Saber if you want one-shot guaranteed kills on yellow bars, pick Tyrannicide if you want to delete waves of reds and purples.

### What is the best brand set for Tyrannicide?

Striker remains the king for raw output. If you want a more sustainable build, Hunter's Fury or Hard Wired both pair well with the headshot stacking talent.

### Should I run a scope or a marksman scope?

A 12x marksman scope works for static engagements, but most Tyrannicide players prefer an 8x or 9x because the rifle is fast enough to function inside 50m and a heavier scope hurts ADS speed.

### Is the talent worth chasing max stacks?

Yes, but do not panic if you are stuck at three or four stacks. The damage curve is generous — even at half stacks you are doing more than a comparable non-named MMR.

### How does it perform against the Tank archetype?

Reasonably well. Yellow-bar tanks take 3-4 clean headshots at full stacks. If you swap in a Crit chance build instead of pure headshot damage, you can bring that down to two against most archetypes.

## Closing

Tyrannicide is the MMR that finally feels like it belongs in the post-TU22 sandbox. It is fast enough to keep up with the tempo of modern Heroic content, hits hard enough to compete with Saber and Mantis, and has a talent that genuinely rewards good aim instead of artificially gating power behind RNG. If you have one in your stash, it deserves a serious build slot — and if you do not, it is worth grinding for.
