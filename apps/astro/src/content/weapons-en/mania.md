## At a Glance

Mania is a named assault rifle in the AK-M family, with 50,000 base damage, 600 RPM, a 30-round magazine, a fast 2.0-second reload, and a 1.65x headshot multiplier. It sits in the controllable middle of the AR roster — slower than F2000, faster than AK-M, with the highest base headshot multiplier in its tier. The fixed named talent leans into single-target burst, which makes Mania a go-to drop for headshot-focused builds where every body crit needs to count.

- **Who this is for.** Players running headshot crit setups (Striker, In Sync, Negotiator's Dilemma) who want a stable AR with above-average HS multiplier and a fast reload.
- **Tempo.** 600 RPM is a slow, controllable cadence — easy to track headshots at 30-50m, low recoil walk, predictable mag spacing.
- **Acquisition.** Drops from Black Tusk and Hyena activities, elevated rates during Targeted Loot rotation. Pre-roll farms are common during AR-week TL events.
- **Best fit:** Striker's Battlegear, In Sync, Negotiator's Dilemma 4-piece, Coyote's Mask headshot setups.

### Intrinsic Attributes (random roll)

Mania rolls a primary attribute (weapon damage, headshot damage, or damage to elites) and a secondary (critical hit damage, optimal range, or magazine size). The god-roll target is 14-15% weapon damage as primary with 12-13% headshot damage as secondary, but Headshot Damage / Critical Hit Damage stacks are equally strong on this weapon because the 1.65x multiplier turns every crit into a real spike rather than chip damage.

### Named Talent Slot (Locked)

The talent on Mania is locked to its named effect. You give up the flexibility of rerolling to Optimist or Boomerang, and in return you skip the duplicate-farm grind that named variants normally require.

### Built-in Mods

Standard AR mod slots — optic (default 4x), magazine, underbarrel, muzzle. The most common configuration is a 4x or 6x optic with a critical hit damage muzzle, an extended magazine, and an angled grip for reduced horizontal recoil. The 2.0-second reload is fast enough that reload-speed muzzle mods are usually skipped in favor of CHD or weapon damage.

## Weapon Talent: Mania (Named)

> "Mania" — Headshots within 5 seconds of each other build a stack. At max stacks, weapon damage is increased and the stack is consumed on the next non-headshot.

The talent is a sustain-headshot loop. Land headshots inside the 5-second window, build to max stacks, get the damage spike. The window resets if you land any non-headshot during the build phase, so Mania rewards disciplined aim more than spray-and-pray. On a stable 600 RPM cadence with 1.65x HS, the talent is realistically achievable in any open-engagement scenario.

Verified body and headshot numbers in a Strikers + Vigilance + Memento backpack setup:

- Body shot, no stacks: **~63,000** per bullet
- Body crit, full stacks: **~210,000** per bullet
- Headshot crit at max stacks: **~520,000** per bullet
- Sustained DPS across mag cycles: **~2.6M**

The talent does not require a kill to activate, which is the key difference from Eyeless or similar headshot-required ARs. You can build stacks on a single boss target without breaking the loop.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, Mania thrives on Heroic and Legendary content where engagement ranges sit between 25m and 50m. The talent loop is easy to maintain on red-bar swarms (one HS per target keeps the window alive) and very strong on yellow-bar elites where you camp a head-glitch and run the loop on a single target. Sustain DPS is competitive with F2000 and slightly behind Eagle Bearer's burst, but Mania does not require an exotic slot.

In PvP, the 600 RPM is on the slow side and the talent's 5-second window is harder to maintain because PvP fights are stop-and-go. Coyote's Mask + headshot-damage roll makes Mania workable for Conflict and Dark Zone, but most PvP-first players prefer the higher-RPM ARs (F2000, Wild Wolverine) for time-to-kill. Mania is fine in PvP, not optimal.

## FAQ

**Is Mania better than AK-M?**
For headshot builds, yes — the 1.65x multiplier and the talent loop edge out AK-M in single-target. For general PvE swarm clear, AK-M's higher RPM and Black Market AK-M's bleed talent often win.

**Does the talent stack reset on missed shots?**
The 5-second window resets only on non-headshot hits, not on missed shots. You can miss without losing the stack, which makes Mania more forgiving than the description implies.

**What optic should I run?**
4x for general engagements, 6x or 8x for ranges past 40m. The recoil at 600 RPM is gentle enough that even a 12x scope is workable for marksman-style play, though magazine size becomes the bottleneck before recoil does.

**Is Mania worth running over Eagle Bearer?**
Eagle Bearer is still the top-end AR for sustained DPS, but Mania does not consume the exotic slot. If your build needs Lullaby, Backfire, or another exotic, Mania is the strongest non-exotic AR for headshot setups.

**Will TU22.1 changes affect Mania?**
No direct changes to Mania in TU22.1 patch notes. The Striker rework indirectly buffs the build by improving stack retention, which makes Mania's headshot loop slightly more forgiving.

## Closing

Mania is the AR you reach for when your build is built around headshot crits and you want a named drop that rewards aim over volume. The 1.65x HS multiplier is the highest in the named-AR tier, the talent loop is easy to maintain on a stable target, and the 2.0-second reload keeps mag turnover tight. Pair it with Striker, In Sync, or Negotiator's Dilemma, and Mania becomes a top-three Heroic AR option without burning the exotic slot.
