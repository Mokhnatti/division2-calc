## At a Glance

The Stoner LMG is the workhorse of the base light machine gun roster in The Division 2. With a 100-round drum, a controllable 600 RPM cadence, and a forgiving recoil pattern, it sits in the comfortable middle ground between burst damage and sustained suppression. It is the first LMG most agents reach for when they want to learn the archetype without committing to the heavier M60 or the spray-friendly HK121. As a base weapon, drops appear at any World Tier, and rolls scale with watch level, which means a fresh Stoner you pull at SHD 1000+ is fully competitive with named LMGs in raw stat budget.

The defining feature is balance. The Stoner does not lead any single category, but it never embarrasses itself either. Time-to-kill on red elites at typical Heroic ranges is solid, the recoil curve flattens out after a few attachments, and the magazine size means you almost never reload mid-engagement. If you are building around an LMG-centric brand combination such as Providence and Sokolov, the Stoner is the safest pick.

### Damage math

Sustained DPS at 600 RPM equals 600 / 60 multiplied by 60,000, giving a raw 600,000 DPS before any modifiers. Add a typical Heroic critical-hit profile of 50 percent crit chance with 80 percent crit damage and the Stoner pushes well past 800,000 DPS without amplified damage stacking. Headshots at 1.55x are modest by LMG standards, so this rifle prefers crit and amplified damage over headshot stacking.

## Talent slot

The Stoner LMG carries a single weapon-mod talent slot. Recommended choices in TU22.1:

- **Optimist** - up to 35 percent weapon damage as the magazine empties. Excellent fit because the 100-round drum gives long uptime to climb the stack.
- **Steady Handed** - 25 percent recoil reduction makes the Stoner laser-flat. Useful for new players or when you stack damage on the build itself.
- **Boomerang** - 10 percent of damage taken refunds health on a successful crit. Pairs nicely with high crit chance.

For full-on DPS Heartbreaker or Striker setups, Optimist is the default. For headhunter rifle hybrids, Steady Handed is rare but valuable.

## Top Builds

### 1. Heartbreaker Stoner (PvE Heroic)

The classic. Heartbreaker grants stacking weapon damage on body shots, which the Stoner provides in spades. Run all Critical Hit Chance and Damage on the chest and backpack, plus Vigilance on a one-piece. Total uptime on Heartbreaker stacks sits near 100 percent because the 100-round magazine never lets you drop below the threshold. Expected output: roughly 1.6 to 1.9 million DPS sustained on bosses.

### 2. Sokolov Concern + Providence

A two-and-two brand split that delivers Weapon Damage, Weapon Handling, Critical Hit Chance, and Critical Hit Damage natively. Add a Cool Headed chest, Bloodsucker backpack, and Gunner specialization for accuracy bonuses. Optimist on the Stoner caps your stacks during long boss fights.

### 3. Hard Wired LMG support

Hard Wired's cooldown reset on damage taken pairs with skill builds, but the Stoner can run as a backup damage tool. Use it as a sustained pressure weapon while skills do the heavy lifting on bosses. Optimist still wins the talent slot.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, the Stoner is a top-three LMG for Heroic and Legendary content. The wide hitbox of high-armor enemies makes the moderate headshot multiplier irrelevant, and Optimist plus stacking talents shred armored elites. Suppression value is high because of the magazine size, so you can pin a flanker while teammates reposition.

In PvP, the Stoner is rarely seen and for good reason. Time-to-kill at PvP scaling is too slow to compete with rifles or marksman rifles, the slow strafe speed while aiming makes you an easy target, and the headshot multiplier punishes anyone who tries to play it like a precision tool. If you must bring an LMG to the Dark Zone, the M60 gives a better burst window. The Stoner is a PvE specialist.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- 100-round magazine erases reload pressure
- Balanced recoil that beginners can manage
- Generous accuracy budget when paired with Determined or Vigilance
- Synergizes with every major LMG brand set
- Reload speed is fast for an LMG at 4.5 seconds

### Cons

- Headshot multiplier of 1.55x is mediocre in a meta that rewards 2.0x+
- Base damage of 60,000 trails the M60 by a clear margin
- No exotic-tier talent in the base form
- Mediocre PvP performance
- Drum reload animation can feel sluggish under panic

## FAQ

### Is the Stoner LMG still worth using in TU22.1?

Yes. Patch notes from late 2025 increased base LMG damage by 4 percent across the family, which lifted the Stoner back into competitive territory. It is now within five percent of the M60 in sustained DPS while keeping its larger magazine.

### Should I roll Optimist or Steady Handed?

Optimist for damage builds, Steady Handed only if your gear gives you no recoil control and you cannot stomach the climb. On most builds Optimist wins by a wide margin because it scales multiplicatively with weapon damage.

### What perks should I prioritize on attachments?

Critical Hit Chance on the optic, Critical Hit Damage on the muzzle, and Damage to Targets Out of Cover on the magazine. Skip stability rolls if you have any handling on the build.

### How does the Stoner compare to the Negotiator's Dilemma?

Negotiator's Dilemma is an exotic that places marks on enemies; it functions as a marksman tool, not a pure LMG. The Stoner outperforms Negotiator's Dilemma for sustained group fights but loses in single-target burst windows.

## Closing

The Stoner LMG is a quiet workhorse that rewards patience, positioning, and stacking. It does not produce highlight-reel one-shots, but it is the LMG most likely to clear a Heroic Countdown or carry a fresh group through Legendary missions. If you are new to the archetype, learn the Stoner before reaching for the M60. If you are experienced, keep one rolled with Optimist in your stash for the brand sets that prefer sustained pressure over burst spikes. In TU22.1 the Stoner sits firmly in the A tier of LMGs, and that is exactly where it has lived since the launch of Warlords of New York.
