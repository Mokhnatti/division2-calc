## At a Glance

Lady Death is the SMG that rewards patience. Its talent doesn't fire on the first shot, the second shot, or even the first magazine. It builds quietly across 75 rounds without a reload, and only when you make it to that count does the next magazine come out swinging at +60% weapon damage. For SMG players who treat their build as a long fight rather than a panic burst, Lady Death is the queen of the slot.

In TU22.1 the talent has held up beautifully because the meta still rewards builds that stack damage modifiers across long engagements rather than burning a single magazine and reloading. Lady Death belongs in any SMG locker where the player has the discipline to delay the first reload.

A 50-round magazine and 1100 RPM means a single mag delivers about 2.7 seconds of fire. To trigger the talent you need to make it through one and a half mags without reloading, which means stretching that to roughly four seconds of continuous shooting before the buff actually arrives.

### Damage shape

Without the talent active, Lady Death plays as an above-average SMG with very high RPM and serviceable damage per round. Once the talent kicks in, the same rifle hits roughly 1.6x harder per round for one full magazine. That's the difference between an average SMG and a top-tier room clearer.

## Talent: Premeditation

The mechanic reads cleanly:

1. Land 75 rounds without reloading.
2. The next full magazine deals +60% weapon damage.

The buff doesn't transfer if you swap weapons. The buff doesn't carry across deaths. The buff doesn't refresh until you reload again and stack another 75 rounds.

In practice the loop becomes: open with the loaded magazine and stack hits, transition into the second magazine while continuing to fire (no reload), at the 75-round mark the screen indicator confirms the buff is queued, you reload one final time, and the next mag is the one that does the heavy lifting.

This is the only SMG in the game with this kind of "delayed payoff" mechanic and it pays players who don't panic-reload at half magazine.

## PvE vs PvP

### PvE

Lady Death thrives in PvE encounters where you can stay aggressive without retreating to reload. Countdown wave clears, Summit dense floors, and any room with concentrated targets are textbook. The talent's buff window pairs perfectly with brand sets that reward continuous output.

In raids the rifle is competitive when boss phases stay long enough for the buffed magazine to land in a meaningful damage window. Less effective on phases that force you to swap weapons mid-fight.

### PvP

In PvP, Lady Death is dangerous in the right hands. The talent activation requires a long uninterrupted firing window, which in DZ play is rare but possible during ambushes. When the buffed magazine lands on an opponent, it produces some of the fastest TTKs in the SMG slot.

## Pros and Cons

Pros:

- 50-round mag and 1100 RPM for one of the highest sustained DPS bases in the slot
- +60% weapon damage on the buffed mag is one of the largest single buffs available
- Synergizes with every brand set that rewards sustained fire
- Hand-feel of the rifle is responsive and satisfying

Cons:

- Talent requires patience and discipline; panic-reloaders get nothing
- Buff doesn't carry between deaths or weapon swaps
- Less effective in fights that force frequent reloads
- Buff window is one mag long, then you start over

## FAQ

**Does the buff carry over if I take a hit and lose half my health?**
Yes, the buff is tied to firing patterns, not to your survival state. As long as you didn't reload, the buff queues at 75 rounds.

**Does the buff apply to crit shots?**
The +60% is weapon damage, which is multiplicative with crit damage. So your crits during the buffed mag scale with both modifiers.

**Can I trigger it twice in one fight?**
Yes, if you fire 75 rounds, reload, fire the buffed mag, then fire another 75 rounds without reloading, the buff queues again. This is rare but possible in long-form Summit floors.

**Does it work with In Sync or Combined Arms?**
The buff is purely weapon damage, so In Sync interactions are limited. Combined Arms boosts skills, not weapons. Striker and Sokolov are the natural pairings.

**Is the buff visible on screen?**
Yes, you'll see a clear UI indicator when the talent is queued. The icon stays active until you fire the buffed magazine empty.

**Should I avoid reloading mid-magazine?**
Yes. Reloading at any point before the 75-round mark resets the count. The whole point of Lady Death is to fight through your magazine even when instinct says reload.

## Closing

Lady Death is the SMG for players who think a few seconds ahead. The talent doesn't reward reflexes or aim; it rewards trigger discipline and the willingness to ride out a magazine that you'd normally swap. When the buffed mag arrives, every round in it carries the weight of a long, deliberate setup. In TU22.1 the rifle remains a top-tier exotic in the SMG slot because the meta still rewards sustained damage profiles, and few weapons sustain like Lady Death does. Slot it, learn to ignore the urge to reload, and watch your room clears go up.
