## At a Glance

Tactical Mk 16 is a named assault rifle that sits in the precision-AR bracket without the slowest cadence of the class. Base damage of 59,221 per bullet, 650 RPM cadence, 30-round magazine, fast 2.02-second reload, and a 1.65x headshot multiplier put it in direct competition with ACR-E and the standard AK-M for "best balanced precision AR." The Tactical variant ships with a locked named talent and the slightly unusual base damage figure that hints at a tuned-from-scratch stat profile rather than a clone of the standard Mk 16.

- **Who this is for.** Players who want a precision AR with a reload speed that does not punish mag-cycling, and a guaranteed talent that rewards aim discipline.
- **Precision with cadence.** 650 RPM is the sweet spot for an AR that wants to land headshots without losing volume against grouped reds. The 1.65x HS multiplier is among the best in the class.
- **Acquisition.** Drops from Black Tusk and Outcasts activities, with elevated rates from Pentagon and District Union Arena Heroic. Targeted Loot rotation includes it on a roughly 8-week cycle.
- **Best fit:** Tipping Scales 4pc precision builds, Strikers Battlegear, In Sync chest setups, raid yellow burn comps.

### Intrinsic Attributes (random roll)

The intrinsic on Tactical Mk 16 rolls weapon damage and a secondary like CHD, HSD, or damage to elites. God-roll farming aims for 14-15% on the primary and 12-13% on the secondary. The unusual base damage figure (59,221 rather than a round 60,000) means the rifle's per-bullet output sits a hair below ACR-E but compensates with the named talent's bonus.

### Named Talent Slot (Locked)

Tactical Mk 16 ships with a fixed named talent. The trade is the same as every premium variant — you give up flexibility, you get a guaranteed roll. The named talent on this rifle is built around precision marks rather than mag-cycling or sustained fire.

### Built-in Mods

Standard AR mod slots — optic (4x default), magazine, underbarrel, muzzle. The 2.02-second reload is fast enough that reload-speed mods are not strictly necessary. The 1.65x HS multiplier means CHD and HSD mods scale particularly well on this rifle.

## Weapon Talent: Tactical Mark (Named)

> "Tactical Mark" — Headshots within optimal range mark the target for the next 4 seconds. Marked targets take bonus weapon damage from the entire group.

The named talent rewards headshot discipline and group play. Land a headshot inside 40m, the target is marked for 4 seconds, and every weapon hit on that target from your team carries a damage bonus. The 1.65x HS multiplier already rewards aimed shots — Tactical Mark layers a group-wide bonus on top of that.

In practice, Tactical Mk 16 plays like a designator AR. You pick out yellow elites with headshots, the rest of the team melts the marked target, and you swap to the next priority. The 650 RPM is fast enough to land marks quickly even on moving targets, and the 40m optimal range covers most raid sightlines.

Practical numbers from a verified Tipping Scales + Vigilance + Memento setup:

- Body shot, no mark: **~62,000** per bullet
- Headshot crit, no mark + Vigilance + Memento: **~480,000** per bullet
- Body crit on marked target: **~340,000** per bullet
- Sustained DPS, full mag with marks: **~2.9M**

The locked talent means Tactical Mk 16 cannot run Optimist or Killer. For solo play, the talent is less impactful than for group play — the bonus damage is meant to be shared.

## Top Builds

### Tactical Mk 16 × Tipping Scales 4pc

The flagship raid designator build. Tipping Scales scales CHD off CHC, the named talent's mark layers a group bonus on top, and the 650 RPM is fast enough to keep marks active. Strong in raid yellow burn-down phases where group DPS matters more than solo burst.

### Tactical Mk 16 × Strikers Battlegear

The hybrid solo/group build. Strikers stacks weapon damage on hits, the named talent's mark adds bonus damage to single targets, and the 1.65x HS multiplier rewards aim discipline. Reliable across solo Heroic and group play.

### Tactical Mk 16 × In Sync × Heartbreaker

The skill-DPS group build. In Sync converts weapon crits to skill damage, Heartbreaker rebuilds armor on hits, and the named talent's mark scales group damage including skill damage on the target. Strong in mixed-DPS raid comps.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, Tactical Mk 16 is the designator AR. The 650 RPM is fast enough to land marks on moving targets, the 1.65x HS multiplier rewards headshots, and the 40m optimal range covers raid distances. The named talent shines in group play, where the mark bonus is shared with the whole team. Solo, the talent is still useful but less impactful.

The 30-round mag and 2.02-second reload are both excellent. You spend less time reloading than on AK-M, which means mark uptime is high and the talent's value is realized across longer engagements.

In PvP, Tactical Mk 16 is a strong medium-range AR with a niche group bonus. In coordinated DZ play, the named talent's mark is excellent — call out a marked target and your group melts them. In solo PvP, the 1.65x HS multiplier and 60K base damage carry the rifle on their own.

## Comparison

Versus ACR-E, Tactical Mk 16 has a slightly lower per-bullet damage but the more group-friendly named talent. ACR-E is the solo workhorse, Tactical Mk 16 the raid designator.

Versus standard AK-M, Tactical Mk 16 has the higher RPM and the locked named talent. AK-M is the slow-cadence precision pick, Tactical Mk 16 the faster designator.

Versus Black Market AK-M, the two are different niches. Black Market AK-M is the swap-rotation burst pick, Tactical Mk 16 the sustained designator. Many group players carry both for different rotations.

## FAQ

**Is Tactical Mk 16 worth chasing for solo play?** Marginal. The named talent's group-shared bonus is partially wasted solo. For solo, ACR-E or standard AK-M with Optimist may serve better.

**Tactical Mk 16 or ACR-E?** ACR-E for solo, Tactical Mk 16 for group raid play. Both have 1.65x HS multipliers and similar damage profiles.

**Where does Tactical Mk 16 drop?** Black Tusk and Outcasts activities, Pentagon and District Union Arena Heroic missions, and Targeted Loot rotation every ~8 weeks.

**Does the mark stack with team buffs?** Yes. The mark is a target-side debuff that layers cleanly with team-side buffs like Vigilance and Memento.

**Why is the base damage 59,221 instead of 60,000?** The Tactical variant uses a tuned base figure rather than a clone of the standard Mk 16. The named talent compensates for the small per-bullet gap.
