## At a Glance

The Mk20 SSR bridges the gap between full-auto ARs and bolt-action snipers. At 200,000 base damage and 130 RPM (semi-automatic), this weapon permits rapid-fire precision without the spray randomness of rifles. The 2.5x headshot multiplier produces 500,000 damage per critical hit. The 10-round magazine is generous for an MMR, allowing extended engagement without reloading. The Mk20 rewards consistent accuracy—pull the trigger as fast as your aim allows, and each shot carries meaningful damage. This is the rifleman's choice: precision-based volume rather than spray-based or bolt-action-based elimination.

## Stats & Performance

**Base Configuration:**
- **Damage:** 200,000 per shot
- **Fire Rate:** 130 RPM (semi-automatic; actual rate depends on input trigger speed)
- **Magazine Capacity:** 10 rounds
- **Reload Time:** 2.4 seconds
- **Headshot Multiplier:** 2.5x
- **Optimal Range:** 18–45 meters

**Damage Output Breakdown:**

At 130 RPM (theoretical maximum with perfect trigger discipline), the Mk20 SSR generates 433 damage per second. Total magazine damage is 2,000,000 (pure bodyshots), or 5,000,000 with full headshots. Magazine depletion takes 4.62 seconds at maximum trigger speed, with reload cycles lasting 7.02 seconds.

The 2.5x headshot multiplier produces 500,000 damage per critical hit. Against single targets, two crit headshots deliver 1,000,000 total damage. The 10-round magazine allows 5 eliminations (2 shots each) before reloading—remarkable magazine sustainability.

## Talent Slot

The Mk20 SSR accepts **Marksman Rifle talents**. Recommended configurations include:

- **Ranger:** +1% damage per meter beyond 10m. At 30m, +20%; at 40m, +30%; at 45m, +35%. The damage scaling compounds with 200,000 base damage for substantial range advantage.
- **Headhunter:** +20% damage for 10 seconds after landing a headshot. Achievable with semi-auto trigger discipline; consecutive headshots maintain uptime indefinitely.
- **Ace:** +20% damage to the last shot in magazine. The 10th round deals 240,000 damage, incentivizing full magazine utilization.
- **Preservation:** +15% incoming healing; resets on weapon swap. Useful for solo players lacking dedicated healer builds.

Headhunter is the meta choice for sustained precision builds; Ranger for long-range weak-point hunting.

## Top Builds

**Rapid-Fire Precision (PvE/PvP)**
Stack Critical Hit Chance to 60% and Critical Hit Damage to +140%. Use Headhunter talent. Build for semi-auto trigger speed optimization (practice rapid, controlled clicking). Rotation: land crit headshot (Headhunter +20% activates), land second crit headshot (Headhunter refreshes), maintain uptime through rapid consecutive crits. This playstyle enables exceptional sustained DPS if trigger discipline remains tight.

**Ranger Long-Range Hunter (PvE)**
Use Ranger talent. Engage at 30–45m distance, targeting headshots exclusively. Build for Weapon Damage rolls and stack Critical Hit Chance to 55%. Ranger's +30–35% damage scaling at range transforms 200,000 base damage into 260,000–270,000 effective output. Pair with Marksman specialization for weapon handling.

**Magazine Sustainability Extended (Legendary)**
Stack Ammo Capacity and use Ace talent. Build for consistent crit delivery (55% Crit Chance). Strategy: utilize full 10-round magazine capacity with Ace's final-round damage boost, reload, repeat. Against 3–4 elite targets, eliminate each with 2–3 crit headshots (7–8 of 10 rounds), then reload. This provides extended operational capability without external ammo supply.

## PvE vs PvP Deployment

**PvE Strengths:**
The Mk20 SSR excels at all difficulty tiers. Its semi-auto nature permits rapid-fire weak-point focus without spray penalty. Against isolated elite targets, the 500,000 crit headshot and 10-round magazine enable efficient elimination chains. In Legendary farming, the Mk20 becomes an effective alternative to PSG-1—slightly lower damage (200,000 vs 220,000) offset by higher magazine capacity (10 vs 8) and RPM (130 vs 100).

**PvE Versatility:**
The 10-round magazine allows engaging 5 targets (2 shots each) before reloading—superior to PSG-1's 8 rounds. This extended magazine capacity suits multi-target Heroic scenarios where you're eliminating multiple elite enemies across a room.

**PvP Viability:**
In Conflict, the Mk20 SSR is competitive against other precision weapons. A single crit headshot (500,000 damage) eliminates or severely wounds opponents. The semi-auto nature permits reactive fire—if a target moves, adjust and fire again quickly (unlike bolt-action snipers).

**PvP Advantage:**
Against rushing opponents, the Mk20's 130 RPM permits defensive rapid fire. While not matching AR spray output, the semi-auto capability provides medium-range (15–25m) self-defense superior to slower marksman rifles.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I use Mk20 or PSG-1 for Legendary farming?**
PSG-1 for peak burst (220,000 vs 200,000 base damage). Mk20 for extended magazine sustainability (10 vs 8 rounds). If you eliminate 4+ targets per room, Mk20 is superior due to magazine capacity. If you focus on single elite targets, PSG-1 is superior due to base damage.

**Q: Does trigger speed affect Mk20 DPS?**
Yes, significantly. A player clicking 130 times per minute is maximum theoretical DPS. Realistic trigger speed is 80–100 clicks per minute, reducing effective RPM. Practice rapid, controlled clicking to maximize output.

**Q: Can Mk20 solo Legendary?**
Yes, with discipline. The 500,000 crit headshot damage eliminates elite targets efficiently. However, you must maintain positioning; CQB adds overwhelm the Mk20. Best used as primary DPS with teamwork suppressing adds.

**Q: What range should I engage at?**
18–45m. Below 18m, precision becomes difficult and adds pressure you. Above 45m, bullet travel becomes noticeable. The sweet spot is 30–40m where precision is consistent and Ranger scaling is maximum.

**Q: Does Headhunter or Ranger work better?**
Headhunter for rapid-fire crit chains (60% Crit Chance builds). Ranger for long-range weak-point hunting (30m+). Choose based on playstyle—sustained crit delivery (Headhunter) vs precision placement (Ranger).

## Closing Thoughts

The Mk20 SSR is a *precision-based semi-auto rifle for players who value trigger discipline and sustained crit delivery*. Its 200,000 base damage and 2.5x headshot multiplier create exceptional crit potential when paired with optimized builds. The 130 RPM semi-automatic nature bridges the gap between bolt-action precision and full-auto volume—it rewards accurate trigger speed without spray randomness. In Heroic/Legendary content, the Mk20 excels as a primary DPS weapon when weak-point focus is achievable. In CQB scenarios or reactive PvP, it struggles. But for what it does—enable rapid-fire precision—the Mk20 SSR is genuinely excellent. If you value precision-based volume and can maintain consistent headshot accuracy, the Mk20 belongs in your primary slot.
