## At a Glance

The Watchman is a Named M44 sniper-class Marksman Rifle that has built a reputation as one of the most reliable long-range single-target weapons in The Division 2. With a 280000 base damage profile, 80 RPM and the standard MMR 2.5x headshot multiplier, it sits comfortably in the upper bracket of precision rifles, just below Saber for raw per-shot damage but with a more forgiving talent design.

Where Saber demands you stay at long range to keep its talent active, Watchman's named ability is more flexible and rewards a wider range of playstyles. The result is an MMR that performs well from 50m to 120m, that scales with both Crit and headshot builds, and that has been a fixture in build guides since launch.

## Stats

### Core numbers

- **Base damage:** 280000 per shot
- **Rate of fire:** 80 RPM
- **Magazine:** 5 rounds
- **Reload:** 2.7 seconds (full)
- **Headshot multiplier:** 2.5x
- **Optimal range:** 50-120m
- **Damage falloff start:** ~80m
- **Aim down sights time:** ~0.4s

### Damage output

- **Body DPS (sustained):** ~373K
- **Headshot DPS (sustained):** ~933K
- **Burst (mag dump, headshots):** 5 shots x 700K = 3.5M in roughly 3.75 seconds
- **Time-to-kill on a 1.4M HP yellow bar:** one or two clean headshots

### Handling

Watchman uses the M44 chassis, which means heavy single-shot recoil with a strong vertical kick. The pattern is predictable and forgiving with a stability mod, but it punishes spamming the trigger. ADS speed is on the slower side for an MMR — pair it with a brand bonus or attachment that improves swap speed if you want to use it as a hybrid weapon.

## Talent

### Watchman-specific talent

The Watchman talent rewards consecutive critical hits. Each crit landed in succession stacks a buff to weapon damage and crit damage; missing a shot or hitting a body target without a crit clears the stack. At max stacks the buff is significant — typically 30-50% combined damage and crit damage uplift — turning every shot into a serious threat.

The talent design encourages a specific playstyle: build for high crit chance, take measured shots, and ride the stack into late-fight executions. It pairs especially well with brand sets that boost crit chance across all gear pieces.

### Practical tips

- Build for at least 50% crit chance to keep stacks reliable
- Reloads do not clear the buff
- Body shots without a crit clear the stack — be selective with what you fire at
- Yellow bars are ideal because their longer health pools let you ride the stack to maximum

## Top Builds

### Striker Crit MMR

Four-piece Striker plus Coyote's Mask plus Walker, Harris and Co. holster. Stack Crit Chance until you hit ~60%, then push Crit Damage and Headshot Damage. Watchman crits with this build hit 1.5-2.0M against Heroic yellows.

### Pure Sniper

Three-piece Sokolov plus Empress Plate Carrier plus Picaro's Holster. Damage to elites and damage to targets out of cover make Watchman an absolute deletion tool against high-HP single targets. This is the build for raid sniper roles.

### Negotiator's Dilemma Marker

Four-piece Negotiator's Dilemma plus Lengmo gloves. Watchman tags the priority target, the Negotiator effect spreads damage to nearby reds. This makes the rifle a full team-clear tool instead of pure single-target.

## PvE-PvP

### PvE

Watchman is a top-tier PvE MMR in TU22.1. It outperforms most of the rifle class on long-sightline content, dominates Countdown when you need to delete elites from across the map, and is one of the best raid sniper picks for fights with single high-HP bosses. The talent rewards good aim more than positioning, which gives it a slight edge over Saber for players who do not always get to choose their engagement distance.

In open-world Heroic and Legendary, Watchman handles bounty bosses easily and clears purple-bar elites in single shots with a Crit-heavy build. It is one of the most reliable picks for solo high-difficulty content.

### PvP

In Conflict, Watchman is a sniper that respects no build. A clean headshot on most setups is a kill, and the 50-120m optimal range covers nearly every PvP map. The downsides are the long reload and slow ADS — if you whiff a shot, the followup is slow enough that an aware opponent can close the distance and punish you.

In the Dark Zone, Watchman is a contested-loot favorite. The range advantage lets you lock down rooftops and hold sightlines that other weapons cannot reach.

## FAQ

### Watchman or Saber?

Watchman is more flexible — its talent works at any range as long as you crit, while Saber demands distance. Saber has slightly higher per-shot damage. Most players keep both and pick based on the engagement.

### What scope works best?

A 12x marksman scope is the natural choice. Some players go 9x for a slight ADS bonus, but the rifle is built for long range and a heavier scope leans into its strengths.

### Does the talent stack with regular crit damage?

Yes. The Watchman talent buff is multiplicative with character-sheet crit damage, which is why the rifle scales so well into Crit-focused builds.

### Can Watchman one-shot Heroic yellows?

With a Striker or Sokolov build, a clean crit headshot at full stacks regularly one-shots non-tank yellow bars. Tanks usually take two clean shots.

### Is the slow reload a dealbreaker?

It is the rifle's biggest weakness. A reload speed mod or a brand bonus that shaves it down to 2.0 seconds noticeably improves performance. Determined or Optimist on a backup magazine can also help.

## Closing

Watchman is the M44-chassis sniper that deserves the build slot. It hits hard, plays well at any range past 30m, and has a talent that scales naturally with the kind of Crit-heavy builds most serious players already run. The slow reload and small magazine are real weaknesses, but they are also the price of one of the highest single-shot ceilings in the game. If you want a precision rifle that can headline any PvE encounter and hold its own in Conflict and the Dark Zone, Watchman is one of the best choices in The Division 2's TU22.1 sandbox.
