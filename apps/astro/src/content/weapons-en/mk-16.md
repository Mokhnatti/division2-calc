## At a Glance

The Mk 16 is a lightweight assault rifle designed for rapid, accurate volume fire. With 55,000 base damage, 700 RPM, and a 30-round magazine, the Mk 16 splits the difference between precision rifles and spray-and-pray SMG tactics. The 700 RPM rate sits in an ideal sweet spot for most players — fast enough to suppress targets without wasting ammunition, slow enough to maintain accuracy without excessive muzzle climb. The 2.0-second reload is standard for its class, and the 1.55× headshot multiplier rewards precision over spray.

In TU22.1, the Mk 16 is the foundational AR for builds that prioritize sustained fire and consistent DPS. Where specialized ARs like the ACR fill niche roles, the Mk 16 delivers middle-ground performance across every encounter type. Strikeforce operators and Striker builds gravitate toward the Mk 16 because the 700 RPM allows you to land consistent headshots while ramping stacks, unlike higher-RPM ARs that require more aim discipline.

This makes the Mk 16 one of the easiest ARs to chain headshots with, especially at the 35-meter optimal range. Beyond 40 meters, falloff begins degrading damage output, but the reduction is gradual — at 70 meters you're still dealing roughly 60% of base damage.

## Talent

The Mk 16 rolls the standard AR talent pool: Strained, Perfectly Handling, Close and Personal, and occasionally Allegro. Strained remains the meta pick for high-skill builds — each shot at lower than 50% health grants bonus weapon damage, scaling infinitely with aggressive play. Perfectly Handling is the defensive alternative, reducing recoil and improving accuracy.

For endgame optimization, Strained synergizes perfectly with glass-cannon builds that deliberately maintain low armor. The bonus damage stack compounds exponentially during Striker ramp phases, and the Mk 16's moderate recoil allows you to land consistent headshots even while maintaining health pressure. Avoid Close and Personal unless running aggressive CQB-focused builds.

The 700 RPM allows you to ramp Striker stacks quickly while maintaining crit consistency. Strained's bonus damage compounds as you farm, turning each successive kill into higher DPS output. **Divine Wrath Precision:** Run Divine Wrath chest with Mk 16 and focus on crit chance/damage stacking. The Mk 16's 700 RPM and 1.55× headshot multiplier synergize well with Divine Wrath's crit-focused scaling. Pair with a secondary precision rifle for tactical flexibility.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE, the Mk 16 is the reliable workhorse across all difficulties. It doesn't specialize in any single role — it doesn't have the precision of a marksman rifle, the volume of an LMG, the burst of an AK-M — but it excels at turning consistent headshots into steady, predictable damage output. On Heroic and Legendary content, the Mk 16's ease-of-use makes it ideal for players who want to focus on positioning and tactics rather than fighting recoil mechanics.

In PvP (Conflict, Last Stand, Dark Zone), the Mk 16 punches above its weight in mid-range engagements. The 700 RPM is fast enough to suppress opponents without losing accuracy, and the 1.55× headshot multiplier means a 3-4 headshot kill is achievable against moderately armored agents. The 2.0-second reload is standard across the AR class.

## FAQ

**Is the Mk 16 or ACR better?**
The Mk 16 is better for sustained headshot damage; the ACR is better for precision at distance. The Mk 16's 700 RPM keeps you ramping stacks faster, while the ACR's lower RPM allows tighter grouping at extended ranges. Choose Mk 16 for Striker/glass-cannon builds, ACR for pure precision.

**Can I solo Legendary with the Mk 16?**
Yes, especially when paired with Striker, Coyote's Mask, and Memento. The consistent damage output and moderate recoil allow you to maintain damage while managing cover and healing windows. It's not the fastest option, but it's reliable.

**Should I use Strained or Perfectly Handling on the Mk 16?**
Strained if you're running glass-cannon (below 50% armor). Perfectly Handling if you're running moderate-to-high armor. Strained has higher damage ceiling but requires discipline; Perfectly Handling is more forgiving.

**Does the Mk 16 work in team Legendary runs?**
Yes, especially if someone on your team is running Perpetuation or Memento. The Mk 16's consistent damage output scales well with team compositions, and the 700 RPM keeps you feeding stacks to your team's primary damage dealer.
