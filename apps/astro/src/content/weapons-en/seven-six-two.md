## At a Glance

The Seven Six Two is a Named Marksman Rifle that quietly stakes a claim as one of the most balanced precision weapons in The Division 2. With a 250000 base damage value, 100 RPM, and an 8-round magazine — generous for the MMR class — it bridges the gap between heavy single-shot snipers like Saber and Watchman and the faster-paced precision rifles like Tyrannicide.

The 2.5x headshot multiplier is the standard MMR figure, but Seven Six Two's identity is built around magazine size and tempo. Where most MMRs force you to manage 5- or 6-round magazines with long reloads, Seven Six Two gives you 8 rounds and a 2.4 second reload, which means you spend more time shooting and less time vulnerable behind cover.

## Stats

### Core numbers

- **Base damage:** 250000 per shot
- **Rate of fire:** 100 RPM
- **Magazine:** 8 rounds
- **Reload:** 2.4 seconds (full)
- **Headshot multiplier:** 2.5x
- **Optimal range:** 40-90m
- **Damage falloff start:** ~70m
- **Aim down sights time:** ~0.35s

### Damage output

- **Body DPS (sustained):** ~416K
- **Headshot DPS (sustained):** ~1.04M
- **Burst (mag dump, headshots):** 8 shots x 625K = 5M in roughly 4.8 seconds
- **Time-to-kill on a 1.4M HP yellow bar:** one or two clean headshots

### Handling

Seven Six Two has predictable vertical recoil with minor horizontal drift after the third shot. The pattern is forgiving enough that most players can land 6-7 of an 8-round magazine on target without difficulty. ADS speed is moderate — slightly faster than Saber, slightly slower than Tyrannicide — and the 2.4 second reload is on the faster end for the MMR class.

## Talent

### Seven Six Two talent

The named talent on Seven Six Two rewards consistent fire on the same target. Each consecutive hit on a single target stacks a damage buff, and switching targets resets the stack. The talent design encourages focus over flexibility, which fits the rifle's role as a single-target deletion tool.

Compared to Watchman's crit-stacking talent or Saber's range-locked buff, Seven Six Two's talent is one of the most accessible — it activates simply through landing shots, with no positional or crit requirements. That accessibility makes it a strong pick for players who want an MMR that performs without demanding specific build conditions.

### Practical tips

- Pick a target and stay on it — the talent rewards focus, not target swaps
- Yellow bars are ideal because their longer health pools let stacks ramp to maximum
- The 8-round magazine specifically helps maintain the talent — fewer reloads means more stacks
- Pairs well with Determined or Optimist on a backup magazine for sustained pressure

## Top Builds

### Striker Sustained Marksman

Four-piece Striker plus Coyote's Mask plus Walker, Harris and Co. holster. Seven Six Two layers Striker stacks with the named talent stacks for huge sustained damage. Crit headshots in this loadout regularly hit 1.5-2.0M against Heroic yellows.

### Hunter's Fury Hybrid

Three-piece Hunter's Fury plus Empress Plate Carrier plus Picaro's Holster. Hunter's Fury rewards close-range play, and Seven Six Two covers the longer engagements where Hunter's Fury cannot reach. The hybrid is one of the most versatile setups for solo Heroic.

### Negotiator's Dilemma Spread

Four-piece Negotiator's Dilemma plus Lengmo gloves. Seven Six Two tags the priority elite, and Negotiator spreads damage to surrounding adds. This makes the rifle a full team-clear tool instead of pure single-target.

## PvE-PvP

### PvE

In PvE, Seven Six Two is a top-tier MMR that performs across most content types. It dominates Countdown when you need to delete elites from across the map, shines in Summit on long-sightline floors, and earns a build slot in any team that needs a flexible precision damage dealer. The 8-round magazine specifically gives it an edge in encounters where you want to chain damage without constant reloads.

For solo Heroic and Legendary, Seven Six Two carries beautifully because the talent ramps up naturally as you focus targets. The accessible talent design means you do not need a specialized build to extract value, which makes it one of the most beginner-friendly precision rifles in the game.

### PvP

In Conflict, Seven Six Two is a balanced precision pick. The 250K base damage with 2.5x headshot multiplier delivers reliable kills, and the 8-round magazine gives you genuine follow-up potential when an opponent breaks cover. It is not the highest per-shot damage MMR — Saber takes that crown — but it is one of the most consistent.

In the Dark Zone, Seven Six Two is a strong contested-loot weapon. The range coverage from 40-90m matches typical engagement distances, and the larger magazine means you can pressure multiple opponents without committing to a long reload window.

## FAQ

### Seven Six Two or Saber?

Saber has higher per-shot damage and a range-locked talent. Seven Six Two has a larger magazine, faster reload, and more accessible talent. For pure burst on a single yellow bar, Saber. For sustained precision pressure across multiple targets, Seven Six Two.

### Seven Six Two or Tyrannicide?

Both are 100 RPM precision rifles. Seven Six Two has higher base damage (250K vs 200K) and an 8-round magazine. Tyrannicide has a different talent design that rewards consecutive headshots specifically. For most players, Seven Six Two is the more forgiving pick.

### What scope works best?

A 9x or 12x marksman scope. Some players prefer 8x for hybrid play, but the rifle's optimal range pushes it toward heavier magnification.

### Is the talent worth chasing max stacks?

Yes. The damage uplift at full stacks is significant, and the 8-round magazine specifically helps you maintain stacks without breaking for reloads.

### Can Seven Six Two carry solo Heroic?

Easily. It is one of the most reliable solo Heroic MMRs in the game because the talent ramps without needing specific build conditions, and the 8-round magazine gives it more sustained pressure than the heavy snipers.

## Closing

Seven Six Two is the MMR for the player who wants reliability without sacrificing damage. The high base damage, generous magazine and accessible talent design produce a rifle that performs across nearly every content type in The Division 2. It does not have the absolute highest per-shot ceiling like Saber, or the longest optimal range like Watchman — but it has the most balanced damage profile in the MMR class, and it earns its slot in serious builds for that consistency. If you have one in your stash, it deserves a permanent build slot, and if you do not, it is one of the highest-value MMRs to chase in TU22.1.
