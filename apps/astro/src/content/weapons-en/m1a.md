## At a Glance

The M1A is a semi-automatic rifle in the Rifle family, sitting in the comfortable middle of the rifle roster. With 95,000 base damage, 130 RPM, and an 8-round magazine, the M1A trades AR cadence for per-shot punch. It is the rifle most agents reach for when they want a precision tool that still has rapid follow-up potential. Reload is a brisk 2.4 seconds, and the 2.0x headshot multiplier rewards careful shot placement.

In TU22.1 the M1A is one of the strongest rifles in the game thanks to a long history of community love and consistent balance attention. It is the rifle of choice for Strained, Rifleman, and Centered builds, and it is the foundation of every "click each enemy in the head" playstyle. The 8-round magazine is small for a primary weapon, but the 2.4-second reload keeps downtime manageable. If you have ever wanted a weapon that feels like a marksman rifle but plays at AR ranges, the M1A is the answer.

### Damage math

Sustained body-shot DPS equals 130 / 60 multiplied by 95,000, giving 205,833 raw DPS. Apply the 2.0x headshot multiplier with a typical 50 percent crit chance and 80 percent crit damage profile, and the M1A reaches roughly 700,000 to 800,000 DPS on headshot-only fire. With Strained or Rifleman talents, the M1A can climb above 1.3 million sustained DPS, putting it in line with top-tier ARs.

## Talent slot

The M1A carries a single mod talent slot. The strongest TU22.1 picks:

- **Strained** - up to 50 percent critical hit damage in exchange for armor, scaling with armor depleted. The M1A's high per-shot damage makes Strained one of the highest-multiplier talents in the game.
- **Rifleman** - up to 65 percent weapon damage stacking on consecutive headshots. The 2.0x headshot multiplier makes stacking realistic.
- **Boomerang** - heal on critical hit. Niche but viable for solo play.

Strained is the default damage roll for high-skill players, Rifleman for beginner-friendly setups.

## Top Builds

### 1. Strained M1A (PvE Heroic)

The classic. Stack Critical Hit Chance and Critical Hit Damage on the chest and backpack, run a Picaro's Holster, and use Glass Cannon for the chest. Strained on the M1A produces some of the highest crit damage numbers in the game. Output: 1.4 to 1.7 million DPS sustained.

### 2. Rifleman Stacking

Rifleman rewards consecutive headshots, and the M1A's 2.0x multiplier and 8-round magazine align perfectly. Use a Coyote's Mask, Memento backpack, and Determined chest. Output: 1.3 to 1.5 million DPS sustained, with strong sustain because Rifleman stacks survive reloads.

### 3. Centered Aim Stack

Centered chest grants weapon damage based on accuracy stat. Pair with Sokolov Concern and Providence brand sets for high accuracy and weapon damage. Strained on the M1A. Output: 1.3 to 1.5 million DPS sustained.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, the M1A is a top-tier rifle for Heroic and Legendary content. Its high per-shot damage and 2.0x headshot multiplier shred yellow elites, and Strained or Rifleman procs turn it into a marksman-class weapon at AR ranges. The 8-round magazine demands accuracy but does not cripple sustain because the 2.4-second reload is fast.

In PvP, the M1A is one of the strongest rifles in the game. Time-to-kill on a 6-million-armor opponent is roughly 0.5 seconds with two headshots, and the 130 RPM cadence allows for rapid follow-up shots. The 8-round magazine is small but adequate for most duels, and the 2.4-second reload keeps you in the fight. The M1A is a staple PvP weapon for Conflict and Dark Zone duels.

## FAQ

### Is the M1A still meta in TU22.1?

Yes. The M1A has been a meta rifle since the launch of The Division 2 and remains so in TU22.1. Recent balance patches have not changed its position significantly, and it is still the rifle of choice for Strained and Rifleman builds.

### Strained or Rifleman?

Strained for damage builds, Rifleman for forgiveness and beginner-friendly setups. Strained produces the highest single-shot crits, Rifleman the highest sustained DPS. Most players keep both rolls in the stash.

### What attachments should I prioritize?

Critical-hit-chance optic, critical-hit-damage muzzle, extended magazine, and a stability grip. The M1A's standard 8-round magazine can be swapped for a 10-round magazine on the SOCOM M1A CQB preset.

### How does the M1A compare to the Police M4?

Police M4 is the named M1A variant with a guaranteed Perfectly Allegro talent (rate of fire on weapon kill). Police M4 outdamages the M1A on add-heavy content but loses on burst windows. The base M1A is more flexible because you can roll any talent.

## Closing

The M1A is the rifle for players who want a precision weapon that still plays at AR ranges. Per-shot damage is high, headshot multiplier rewards skill, and the 2.4-second reload keeps downtime manageable. In TU22.1 the M1A sits firmly in the S tier of legendary rifles, with only the Police M4 and exotic Lullaby ahead in narrow scenarios. Pick the M1A if you want a rifle that rewards accuracy, and pair it with Strained or Rifleman for the strongest results.
