## At a Glance

The HK121 is the rate-of-fire specialist of the base LMG family. Where the M60 thumps and the Stoner sustains, the HK121 sprays. Eight hundred rounds per minute is the highest cadence in the LMG class, comparable to many assault rifles, and the 50-round magazine is small enough that you actually empty it during a single sustained engagement. Per-shot damage of 70,000 sits comfortably between the Stoner and the M60, but the real story is bullet density: more shots in the air per second translates to more crit rolls, more talent procs, and more pressure on enemy hitboxes.

In TU22.1, the HK121 has emerged as the LMG of choice for crit-stacked builds. Its high RPM rolls more critical hits per second than any other belt-fed weapon, which means crit-chance and crit-damage talents pay out faster. The trade-off is reload uptime: the 50-round magazine empties in under four seconds, and the 4.0-second reload eats into your damage window if you miss too often.

### Damage math

Sustained DPS at 800 RPM equals 800 / 60 multiplied by 70,000, which gives 933,333 raw DPS - the highest sustained value of any base LMG. Apply a typical 50 percent crit chance and 80 percent crit damage profile and the HK121 climbs to roughly 1.3 million DPS without talents. The catch is the reload: factor in 4.0 seconds of downtime every 3.75 seconds of firing and your real DPS settles around 670,000.

## Talent slot

The HK121 carries one mod talent slot. The best picks for TU22.1:

- **Killer** - 50 percent critical hit damage for 10 seconds after a critical kill. The HK121's high fire rate produces frequent crits, so chaining Killer is realistic on Heroic content.
- **Optimist** - up to 35 percent weapon damage as the magazine empties. Works because the 50-round magazine empties fast, hitting peak Optimist value within a couple of seconds.
- **Steady Handed** - 25 percent recoil reduction. The HK121's recoil pattern is tighter than the M60 but still climbs sharply on the third or fourth shot.

Killer wins for crit-stacked builds and Optimist wins for Heartbreaker setups.

## Top Builds

### 1. Striker HK121 (PvE Heroic)

Striker rewards consistent hits, and 800 RPM dumps Striker stacks faster than any other LMG. With a Coyote's Mask, Memento backpack, and a four-piece Striker brand, the HK121 hits roughly 1.8 to 2.0 million DPS in sustained fights. Killer talent on the gun chains crit damage between waves.

### 2. Heartbreaker Spray

Heartbreaker rewards body shots, and the HK121's hitrate makes it the second-best Heartbreaker LMG behind the Stoner. Optimist on the gun, Vigilance on the chest, and a Picaro's Holster create a clean rotation. Expect 1.5 to 1.7 million DPS sustained.

### 3. Future Initiative Crit Support

For group play, Future Initiative's skill-tier and weapon-damage bonuses synergize with the HK121's bullet density. Pair with a Catharsis or Reformation gear set running Empress International. The HK121 acts as suppression and stack generator while teammates run skills.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, the HK121 is a top-tier crit-stacking LMG. The high RPM is ideal for chunking through massed yellow bars on Heroic and Legendary, and the smaller magazine pushes you to play in cover bursts rather than continuous suppression. Boss DPS is competitive with the M60 if you can keep Killer stacks live, and Striker uptime is the best in the LMG class.

In PvP, the HK121 is a glass cannon. Its time-to-kill on a 6-million-armor opponent is among the fastest of any LMG when bullets connect, but the small magazine punishes whiffed engagements. SMG and rifle users who close the gap will out-trade you because of the LMG's slow strafe speed. Use the HK121 in PvP only with a teammate to cover reloads.

## FAQ

### Is the HK121 the best LMG for Striker?

For uptime, yes. The HK121 generates Striker stacks faster than any other LMG and recovers stacks quickly after misses. The M60 does more damage per stack but stacks slower, so the HK121 has the edge on adds-heavy content while the M60 wins on bosses with wide armor segments.

### Killer or Optimist on the HK121?

Killer scales harder when you can chain crit kills, which is realistic on Heroic add waves. Optimist is the safer pick for boss fights where there are no kills to refresh the buff. Most players keep one of each in their stash.

### What attachments should I prioritize?

Extended magazine (62 rounds), critical-hit-chance optic, critical-hit-damage muzzle, and a heavy grip for stability. Skip the standard 50-round magazine if you can roll the extended.

### Does the HK121 outperform an assault rifle for DPS?

Yes, sustained DPS is higher than any non-exotic AR thanks to the bigger per-shot damage and equal RPM. The trade-off is mobility - LMG handling is slower than AR handling, so the AR feels better in flowing fights.

## Closing

The HK121 is the LMG for players who want the suppression and magazine size of belt-fed without giving up the bullet density of an assault rifle. It is the easiest LMG to stack Striker on, the second-best for Heartbreaker, and a credible choice for any crit-focused build. Recoil management is its main learning curve, but a tight grip and good optic flatten it into a near-laser. In TU22.1, the HK121 sits firmly in the S tier of LMGs for adds-heavy Heroic content, with the M60 still ahead on bosses. Pick the HK121 if you like fast feedback loops and quick stack refreshes.
