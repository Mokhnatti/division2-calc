## At a Glance

Brilliance is a Named Rifle that makes a strong claim to being the most flexible mid-range weapon in The Division 2's TU22.1 sandbox. It hits hard for a rifle, fires fast enough to keep tempo with elite waves, and benefits from a talent that rewards aggressive positioning rather than the usual passive damage stacking.

With a 95000 base damage value, 120 RPM and an 8-round magazine, Brilliance occupies a sweet spot between the slow heavy hitters like the M870 family and the spammy semi-autos that flood the rifle category. The 2.0x headshot multiplier is on the lower end for the class, but Brilliance does not depend on headshot damage to carry its weight — it leans into consistent body damage and aggressive Crit scaling.

## Stats

### Core numbers

- **Base damage:** 95000 per shot
- **Rate of fire:** 120 RPM
- **Magazine:** 8 rounds
- **Reload:** 2.4 seconds (full)
- **Headshot multiplier:** 2.0x
- **Optimal range:** 25-55m
- **Damage falloff start:** ~40m
- **Aim down sights time:** ~0.3s

### Damage output

- **Body DPS (sustained):** ~190K
- **Headshot DPS (sustained):** ~380K
- **Burst (mag dump, body):** 8 shots x 95K = 760K in roughly 4 seconds
- **Time-to-kill on a 1M HP red bar:** under three seconds with consistent body shots

### Handling

Brilliance is one of the smoother rifles to handle. The recoil is light, the muzzle climb is manageable without a compensator, and the ADS speed is fast enough to swap between targets without losing tempo. The 8-round magazine sits in the middle of the rifle class — large enough that you do not panic-reload between targets, small enough that you should still bring Determined or Extra Mags for sustained engagements.

## Talent

### Brilliance-specific talent

The named talent on Brilliance triggers when you take damage and rewards counter-aggression. After taking damage, the rifle gets a temporary buff to weapon damage — typically 25-30% for a window of 5-7 seconds. Crucially, the buff resets every time you take more damage, which means it stays active during sustained firefights.

The design encourages a specific play pattern: lean into the fight, take some chip damage, return fire with the buff active. This makes Brilliance an excellent rifle for Hunter's Fury, Striker, or any brand set that already wants you to be aggressive.

### Practical tips

- Do not hide behind cover the entire fight — Brilliance wants you to absorb a little damage
- The buff window is generous, so trade shots confidently
- Pair with armor regen for sustained mid-range pressure
- Works well in solo Heroic where you are constantly getting tagged

## Top Builds

### Hunter's Fury Aggressor

Four-piece Hunter's Fury plus Lengmo backpack plus Brazos de Arcabuz holster. The rifle is your bread and butter from 25-50m. Hunter's Fury feeds you damage and survivability the moment you push close, and Brilliance's talent activates naturally as you trade with reds.

### Striker Crit Build

Four-piece Striker plus Coyote's Mask plus Walker, Harris and Co. holster. Stack Crit Chance and Crit Damage with Damage to Health on the backpack. Brilliance crits scale aggressively — expect 350-450K critical hits at full Striker stacks.

### Aces and Eights Marksman Hybrid

Four-piece Aces and Eights for the bonus damage on three-of-a-kind, paired with a marksman scope and rifle damage gear attributes. Brilliance is fast enough to hit the Aces and Eights conditions consistently, turning every fourth shot into a serious hammer.

## PvE-PvP

### PvE

Brilliance is a top-tier mid-range PvE rifle. The 120 RPM keeps it competitive for sustained DPS, the 95K base hits hard enough to make body shots worthwhile, and the talent rewards the aggressive trading style most Heroic and Legendary content asks for. It pairs especially well with brands that want you in the open — Hunter's Fury, Striker, Negotiator's Dilemma — and it earns its slot in any team that needs a flexible mid-range damage dealer.

In specific content it shines in Countdown mid-range objectives, Heroic open-world combat, and any Summit floor with mixed enemy types. It is less effective in long-sightline raids or sniper-heavy maps, where the falloff at 55m starts to hurt.

### PvP

In Conflict, Brilliance is a solid mid-range pick that gets stronger the more aggressive you are. The talent activates the moment your opponent tags you, which makes it forgiving for players who like to push instead of camp. In the Dark Zone its strengths are similar — push the contested loot, take the trade damage, return fire with the buff up.

The weakness is the lower headshot multiplier compared to other rifles. PvP often comes down to clean head shots, and a 2.0x multiplier means Brilliance trades worse than rifles in the 2.5x bracket against equally-built opponents.

## FAQ

### Is Brilliance better than the Custom P416 G3?

For sustained mid-range DPS with a build that wants you in the fight, Brilliance is the stronger pick. The Custom P416 has a slightly different talent profile that scales with consecutive hits — both are top-shelf rifles and either is a good keep.

### What is the optimal sight?

A 4x or 6x scope is the sweet spot for Brilliance. Anything heavier hurts ADS speed without unlocking real benefit, since the rifle is not built for distances past 55m.

### Does Brilliance work for solo Heroic?

Yes. The talent activates naturally as you take chip damage, and the 8-round mag plus 2.4 second reload keep you in the fight. Pair it with a sustainable brand set like Hunter's Fury or Hard Wired and it carries solo content well.

### How does the talent interact with overheal?

Overheal counts as armor for talent purposes, but the Brilliance buff specifically procs when you take damage to actual armor or health, not when overheal absorbs a hit. Account for that with builds that lean heavily on overheal regen.

### Can Brilliance one-shot reds with headshots?

Sometimes, but not reliably. Body shots are usually two to three rounds depending on the enemy archetype, and clean headshots can drop reds in one with a Crit-heavy build.

## Closing

Brilliance is the rifle for the player who wants to live in the fight, not behind it. It does not have the absolute highest damage per shot, the longest range, or the deadliest headshot multiplier — but it has a talent that rewards aggressive engagement, a fire rate that keeps tempo with modern endgame content, and a body damage profile that does not need perfect aim to perform. For mid-range roles in Heroic, Legendary, Conflict and DZ runs, Brilliance is one of the most versatile weapons in your stash.
