## At a Glance

Bottom Feeder is a named Skorpion EVO — a compact, fully automatic submachine gun with aggressive damage scaling and one of the fastest reload cycles in the SMG category. Where the Sleipnir favors mag capacity and sustained fire, the Bottom Feeder prioritizes speed: 950 RPM, only 30-round magazine, and a lightning-fast 1.8-second reload. The Skorpion platform is known for tight recoil and snappy handling, making Bottom Feeder the go-to SMG for players who value accuracy over volume.

In TU22.1, the Bottom Feeder's named talent (Perpetual) makes it exceptional for reload-cycling builds. Every reload grants stacks that translate directly into damage and reload speed, turning the SMG's naturally quick reload into a scaling tool that rewards weapon-swap rotations. The 30-round magazine is smaller than competing SMGs, but the reload speed is so fast that downtime between magazines becomes negligible on optimized builds.

Sustained DPS calculates to approximately 380,000 before mods, placing the Bottom Feeder in line with other 950 RPM options. Where it distinguishes itself is in burst DPS and reload cycles: the 30-round magazine empties in 1.89 seconds, creating a mag-dump-to-reload cycle of only 3.69 seconds total. This is faster than most AR weapons cycle full magazines and reloads, making the Bottom Feeder ideal for targets that demand rapid fire-and-reset rhythms.

Recoil on the Skorpion is notoriously tight — minimal vertical climb and predictable horizontal drift. This makes the Bottom Feeder one of the easiest SMGs to chain headshots with at close range, even during sustained fire. The 20-meter optimal range keeps it firmly in CQB territory, with falloff degrading damage to roughly 50% by 55 meters.

## Talent

The Bottom Feeder rolls Perpetual as its named talent — every reload grants a stack of weapon damage and reload speed for 30 seconds, stacking infinitely. The talent encourages aggressive reload cycling: fire 15-20 rounds, reload for stacks, fire another burst, reload again. With a 1.8-second reload, you can ramp stacks faster than almost any other weapon.

Perpetual scales exceptionally well with Pestilence, Heartbreaker, and reload-focused builds where weapon swaps happen frequently. Each swap back to the Bottom Feeder can stack Perpetual higher, creating a damage multiplier that compounds through encounters. The talent is wasted on static, cover-based playstyles where you're not reloading frequently — but for mobile, aggressive DPS rotations, Perpetual is top-tier.

## Top Builds

**Reload Optimization Operator:** Pair Bottom Feeder with Pestilence or Lady Death and run 4-piece Striker with Memento backpack. The 1.8-second reload cycles Perpetual stacks rapidly, feeding damage into your primary while procs feed back into SMG damage. The result is a tight, reload-centric rotation with minimal downtime.

**Heartbreaker Stack Machine:** Run Heartbreaker chest with Bottom Feeder as the stack generator. The fast reload means you proc Heartbreaker consistently, maintaining survivability while ramping Perpetual damage stacks. Pair with an AR for follow-up damage between SMG rotations.

**Hunter's Fury Aggressive:** Bottom Feeder's tight recoil makes it exceptional for Hunter's Fury PvP builds where armor damage application matters more than raw burst. The reload speed lets you cycle between armor-damaged targets faster, effectively sustaining engagement pressure.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE, the Bottom Feeder is a specialist tool for reload-rotation builds. It doesn't compete with Sleipnir for raw magazine capacity, but the reload speed and Perpetual talent make it superior for coordinated encounters where you're swapping weapons frequently. On Heroic and Legendary content, Bottom Feeder shines when paired with Pestilence or other reload-scaling effects.

In PvP (Conflict, Last Stand, Dark Zone), the Bottom Feeder is one of the most underrated SMGs. The tight Skorpion recoil makes it accurate even during panic spray, and the 30-round magazine is sufficient for most 1v1 scenarios. The 1.8-second reload means you can peek-fire, reload, and peek again with minimal exposure. Against LMG users or players holding static positions, the Bottom Feeder's speed advantage is decisive.

## FAQ

**Bottom Feeder or Sleipnir — which is better?**
Sleipnir for sustained magazine output; Bottom Feeder for reload cycles. Sleipnir's 50-round mag lets you fire continuously through Striker stacks. Bottom Feeder's Perpetual talent amplifies reload-based builds. Choose based on your primary weapon swap frequency.

**Does Bottom Feeder work solo?**
Yes, especially on Heroic difficulty. Perpetual stacking through reload cycles keeps your damage ramping independently of team composition. Solo players value consistency, and Bottom Feeder delivers that.

**Can Bottom Feeder one-mag a yellow-bar on Legendary?**
No. The 30-round magazine at 950 RPM doesn't output enough damage for elite-level one-mags without significant build support like Coyote, Striker stacks, or Perpetual procs.

**Is Perpetual better than Frenzy?**
They scale differently. Frenzy rewards full-mag commitment and adds damage/RPM. Perpetual rewards reload frequency and adds damage/reload speed. For reload-heavy builds, Perpetual wins; for full-mag-dump builds, Frenzy wins.

## Closing

Bottom Feeder is the SMG for players who value precision and speed over volume.
