## At a Glance

Wild Wolverine is a named assault rifle in the high-RPM family, with 50,000 base damage, 850 RPM, a 30-round magazine, a fast 2.0-second reload, and a 1.55x headshot multiplier. It is the cousin of Black Market AK-M in terms of cadence, but with a different talent flavor and a slightly tighter recoil pattern. The fast RPM and forgiving named talent make Wolverine a swarm-clear and sustained-pressure AR — the gun you reach for when the engagement is constant and bodies are coming.

- **Who this is for.** Players who want raw uptime and damage volume — Strikers, Hunter's Fury aggressive setups, anyone running cover-bouncing aggression.
- **Tempo.** 850 RPM puts Wolverine at the high end of the AR spectrum. The 30-round mag burns in about 2 seconds of held trigger.
- **Acquisition.** Drops from True Sons and Outcasts, with elevated rates during AR Targeted Loot rotation. Common from Manning National Zoo and Roosevelt Island Heroic clears.
- **Best fit:** Strikers Battlegear, Hunter's Fury, Coyote's Mask, generalist red-bar swarm setups.

### Intrinsic Attributes (random roll)

Wild Wolverine rolls weapon damage as primary and a secondary like critical hit damage, headshot damage, or damage to elites. The god-roll is 14-15% weapon damage with 12-13% CHD as the secondary. Because the gun fires 850 RPM, magazine size mods are also strong — pushing the mag from 30 to 40 rounds adds genuine sustained-fire windows on long boss damage phases.

### Named Talent Slot (Locked)

The talent is locked to its named effect. No Optimist, Boomerang, or Strained reroll. The trade is the same as every named drop — guaranteed roll, no flexibility.

### Built-in Mods

Standard AR mod slots — optic, magazine, underbarrel, muzzle. Most builds run a 4x optic, extended magazine (or magazine size mod), CHD muzzle, and an angled grip for horizontal recoil. Wolverine's recoil walks vertically more than horizontally, so a vertical grip is the alternative pick.

## Weapon Talent: Wild Wolverine (Named)

> "Wolverine" — Critical hits within a short window grant a stacking damage bonus. Stacks decay if no critical lands within the window.

The talent is a sustain-crit ramp. Land critical hits in quick succession and the stack ramps; stop landing crits and the stack decays. At 850 RPM with even a baseline 30-40% crit chance, stacks accumulate fast and decay slowly. The talent rewards the build that already wants Wolverine — high-volume, high-crit, sustained fire — and punishes nothing about how the gun naturally plays.

Verified numbers in a Striker + Memento + Vigilance setup:

- Body shot, no stacks: **~63,000** per bullet
- Body crit, full stacks: **~220,000** per bullet
- Headshot crit at full stacks: **~430,000** per bullet
- Sustained DPS across mag cycles: **~3.1M**

Sustained DPS is the highest in the named-AR tier outside of exotics. The 30-round mag at 850 RPM gives roughly 2.1 seconds of trigger time, then 2.0 seconds of reload — a near 1:1 fire-to-reload ratio that the talent compensates for by making each mag cycle hit harder than the last.

## Top Builds

### Striker Sustain DPS

Striker's Battlegear 4-piece + Coyote's Mask + Vigilance chest with Vigilance talent. Memento backpack for rolling +damage stacks. Critical hit chance and critical hit damage maxed across all rolls. Wolverine's high RPM makes Striker stacks build instantly and never decay; the build is the most consistent Heroic clear setup in the named-AR tier.

### Hunter's Fury Aggression

Hunter's Fury 4-piece for the proximity damage and reload buff, Wolverine as primary, an SMG or shotgun as panic secondary. The build is for medium-range cover-bouncing — close to 25m, dump a mag with the talent stack, reload behind cover, repeat. Strong on Roosevelt Island and Capitol Hill mission paths.

### Generalist Red-Bar Clear

Strikers + Coyote's + any open chest piece (Memento, Vigilance, Picaro's). The build does not optimize for any single mechanic — it just lets Wolverine do what it does, which is shred red bars and chip yellow bars at 850 RPM. Fine for bounty farming, Open World patrols, and the Summit middle floors.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, Wolverine is the swarm-clear specialist. The 850 RPM cadence chews through red bars and the talent's sustain ramp keeps damage growing across long engagements. Optimal range is 30m — push closer and the recoil walk becomes punishing, push further and the bullet drop and accuracy loss kicks in. On Heroic and Legendary, Wolverine pairs best with cover-to-cover aggression rather than head-glitching.

In PvP, Wolverine is one of the better AR picks because the high RPM means time-to-kill is fast against medium armor. The talent's stack mechanic is harder to maintain in stop-and-go PvP fights, but baseline damage at 850 RPM is enough to win most 1v1 trades. Pair with Coyote's + headshot-damage roll for a strong Conflict and Dark Zone option.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- 850 RPM — among the highest in the AR class
- 30-round magazine paired with 2.0-second reload, fast cycle
- Talent rewards the natural play of the weapon, low maintenance
- Strong in both PvE swarm clear and PvP medium-range engagements
- Optimal range 30m, fits typical Heroic engagement ranges

**Cons:**
- Locked talent — no Optimist, Boomerang, or Strained
- 1.55x HS multiplier is below Mania (1.65x) and Lexington (2.0x)
- Recoil walks vertically at 850 RPM, demands a grip
- 30m optimal range is short for long-sightline content
- Loses to Wolverine's flatter peers (BMAK-M) on bleed talents

## FAQ

**Is Wild Wolverine better than Black Market AK-M?**
Different roles. BMAK-M has the bleed talent which is unique and very strong on red-bar swarms. Wolverine has the sustain-crit ramp which is better on long boss fights. Most players keep both — BMAK-M for adds, Wolverine for elites.

**Does the talent stack reset on reload?**
The talent stacks decay over time if no critical lands inside the window. Reloading does not directly reset the stack, but the gap between mags can let the stack decay if your next mag's first shot is not a crit.

**What optic should I use?**
4x for general engagements, 6x for longer sightlines. The recoil at 850 RPM does not reward high-magnification optics — past 8x the bullet spread starts to outpace the optic's accuracy gains.

**Is Wolverine viable in PvP?**
Yes — 850 RPM gives strong time-to-kill against medium armor, and the headshot multiplier at 1.55x is enough for two-tap headshot kills with Coyote's. Not as dominant as it was pre-TU20 nerf, but still top-tier among ARs.

**Will TU22.1 changes affect Wolverine?**
No direct changes. The Striker 4-piece rework indirectly buffs Wolverine because Striker stacks build faster at 850 RPM than at 600, and the rework gives more stack retention on weapon swap.

## Closing

Wild Wolverine is the named-AR pick for sustained-pressure builds. 850 RPM, a 30-round mag, and a fast 2.0-second reload give it the raw uptime to chew through Heroic content, and the talent's sustain-crit ramp rewards exactly the kind of constant fire the gun is built for. It does not have Mania's headshot ceiling or Lexington's burst, but for a Striker, Hunter's Fury, or generalist setup that wants volume and consistency, Wolverine is the AR you keep in your loadout.
