## At a Glance

The Custom Tactical M1A is the named version of the base M1A semi-auto rifle and one of the most popular rifle pickups in The Division 2. Firing at 140 RPM with a ten-round magazine and a 95k base damage value, the Custom Tactical M1A produces the strongest sustained-DPS rifle profile in the game outside of the marksman category. The x2.0 headshot multiplier means consistent head hits scale catastrophically.

In TU22.1 the Custom Tactical M1A is firmly in the meta tier. It is the rifle that Vigilance and Memento builds default to when they want a balance of per-shot damage and sustained fire, and it is the rifle that Striker builds run when they want headshot scaling without giving up magazine size.

The Custom Tactical M1A is the rifle for agents who want the headshot scaling of a lever-action with the sustained fire of a semi-auto. It is more forgiving than the 1886, more flexible than the SR-1, and produces more consistent damage than any other named rifle in the game.

The 95k base damage figure is lower than the 1886 but the 140 RPM trigger and ten-round magazine produce significantly higher sustained DPS. With the x2.0 headshot multiplier, a clean head hit lands at 190k base, which scales past 1.5 million on a fully buffed Vigilance Glass Cannon build.

The 140 RPM trigger is a comfortable speed for a semi-auto rifle. Faster than the 1886, slower than the Police Mk16, and well within the range where consistent headshot accuracy is realistic.

The ten-round magazine is generous and produces about 4.3 seconds of sustained fire before reload. The 2.4-second reload is acceptable. Headshot multiplier of x2.0 is the highest of any non-lever-action rifle.

## Talent

The Custom Tactical M1A rolls a fixed talent: **Boomerang**. Boomerang grants the weapon a chance on critical hit to refund a round to the magazine. The talent is consistent and produces the kind of magazine extension that pairs cleanly with sustained-DPS rifle builds.

In PvE, Boomerang is a steady damage upgrade because the magazine refund means you spend less time reloading and more time shooting. In PvP, the talent is less impactful because Conflict and DZ engagements end before the magazine refund matters.

The Custom Tactical M1A cannot be re-rolled to a different talent. If you want a different rifle talent profile, you are looking at the base M1A or another named rifle.

## Top Builds

### Vigilance Glass Cannon Custom Tactical M1A

The signature headshot-rifle build. Vigilance triples damage out as long as you are not taking damage, and Glass Cannon doubles damage in and out. The Custom Tactical M1A's x2.0 headshot multiplier and ten-round magazine combine into a sustained-headshot package that no other rifle can match. Run Picaro's Holster, Fox's Prayer, and a Memento backpack for the stack synergy.

### Memento Rifle Build

Memento backpack stacks weapon damage as you pick up trophies dropped by kills. The Custom Tactical M1A's 140 RPM trigger and ten-round magazine mean you produce kills faster than most rifles, which builds Memento stacks faster. This is the most flexible build for the Custom Tactical M1A because Memento works in every game mode.

### Striker Custom Tactical M1A

Six-piece Striker with the Custom Tactical M1A is a strong solo Heroic build. Striker stacks build per hit and the M1A produces consistent hits on its 140 RPM trigger. The ten-round magazine means stacks survive between targets without needing Pummel or Boomerang to refresh them.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE the Custom Tactical M1A is a top-three rifle. It does not produce the per-shot kills of the 1886 and it does not have the magazine of a Police Mk16, but its sustained DPS is the highest of any named rifle in the game. Heroic Hunter HVTs, named bosses, and red elites all melt under a Vigilance Glass Cannon Custom Tactical M1A on consistent headshot accuracy.

In PvP the Custom Tactical M1A is a respected pick. The x2.0 headshot multiplier scales cleanly through PvP normalization and the 140 RPM trigger gives you enough fire rate to chase moving targets. Aim for the head. Body shots produce disappointing damage relative to the headshot ceiling.

## FAQ

**Is the Custom Tactical M1A better than the 1886?**
For sustained DPS, yes. For per-shot kill potential, no. The Custom Tactical M1A wins extended engagements and the 1886 wins single-shot one-tap scenarios.

**What is the best talent for the Custom Tactical M1A?**
Boomerang is fixed on the weapon. If you want a different talent, you are looking at the base M1A, which can roll Lucky Shot, Optimist, or Pummel.

**Can the Custom Tactical M1A one-shot reds on Heroic?**
Yes, on a clean headshot with Vigilance Glass Cannon, a fully buffed Memento stack, or a top-tier Striker build. Body shots will not produce one-shot kills on Heroic reds.

**Is the Custom Tactical M1A worth using over the base M1A?**
Yes, in most cases. Boomerang is a respectable damage scaler and the magazine refund means you spend less time reloading. If you specifically want a different talent, the base M1A is the alternative.

## Closing

The Custom Tactical M1A is the meta sustained-DPS rifle in The Division 2. It does not produce the per-shot kills of the 1886 and it does not have the room-clear potential of an LMG, but its combination of fire rate, magazine, and headshot multiplier produces the strongest rifle DPS profile in the game.

In 2026 the Custom Tactical M1A sits at the top of the named-rifle tier. It is the default pick for Vigilance, Memento, and Striker builds and it remains one of the most consistent weapons in the entire game. On a clean headshot accuracy run, it outperforms every other rifle in sustained content.
