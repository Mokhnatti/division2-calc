## At a Glance

The Bighorn is The Division 2's three-mode exotic assault rifle, and the semi-automatic mode is the one most agents end up living in. Where the full-auto mode shines on close-range pressure and the three-round-burst mode rewards precise mid-range engagement, semi-auto is the Bighorn's marksman-rifle persona — single, deliberate, hard-hitting shots that lean into the rifle's exotic per-shot damage and its signature **Apex Predator** talent.

This page is a reference for the semi-auto mode specifically. The other two modes are covered separately. If you have already farmed the Bighorn and want to know which mode to anchor your build around, the answer for most PvE applications is right here.

## Stats

The Bighorn has different per-shot values for each firing mode. In semi-auto, the rifle scales each shot to a higher damage value than the full-auto mode — roughly comparable to a battle-rifle profile. The headshot multiplier inherits the exotic baseline.

| Stat | Value | Notes |
|------|------:|-------|
| Damage per shot (semi) | High exotic-tier | Among the hardest-hitting AR shots in PvE |
| Effective RPM (semi) | Trigger-limited | Capped by player input rate |
| Magazine | 30 rounds | Shared across modes |
| Reload time | ~2.4 s | Standard exotic reload |
| Headshot multiplier | x1.55 | Standard AR weakpoint |
| Optimal range | 40 m | Longest of the three Bighorn modes |
| Mode switch | Instant | Y/Triangle (configurable) |

The semi-auto mode is the best of the three for sustained marksman play, ammo efficiency, and per-shot scaling. If you are running Memento, Coyote's, or any per-shot-damage stacking build, semi-auto is the mode where the Bighorn shines hardest.

### Mod Slots

## Talent

The Bighorn's exotic talent is **Apex Predator**, which scales weapon damage based on consecutive landed shots. Apex Predator stacks faster on semi-auto than on full-auto in practice, because every trigger pull is a deliberate landed shot rather than a sprayed round that may miss. The talent's ramp-up means semi-auto Bighorn is a sustained-DPS weapon: it gets stronger the longer you stay engaged.

The talent's ceiling on semi-auto is not as high in raw DPS as full-auto can produce in ideal conditions, but the consistency of stack maintenance is much better. For most players in most content, semi-auto Apex Predator is the mode that produces the best landed-DPS over the course of an engagement.

## Top Builds

### Memento Coyote's Semi-Auto Bighorn (raid DPS)

Memento backpack, Coyote's mask, four red cores, glass-cannon chest, named Bighorn. Use semi-auto exclusively. Memento trophies stack into the per-shot damage, Coyote's flat headshot multiplier compounds the 1.55x base, and Apex Predator scales the entire engagement. This is the Bighorn loadout that pushes the rifle into top-tier raid DPS territory.

### Striker Bighorn (sustained PvE)

Four-piece Striker, Sokolov chest, Walker holster. Semi-auto Bighorn keeps Striker stacks healthy without burning ammunition, and the Apex Predator stacks ride alongside Striker stacks for compound scaling. A safer build than the glass cannon, with most of the practical ceiling.

### Headhunter Bighorn (mode-switching)

Four-piece Headhunter, named Bighorn. Use semi-auto for the headshot-priority shots that load Headhunter's bonus, then switch to full-auto or burst to dump the loaded shot at maximum effect. The Bighorn is the only rifle in the game that lets you mode-switch this fluently, and Headhunter rewards it.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, semi-auto Bighorn is one of the strongest single-target rifles in the game. The per-shot damage is high enough to one-tap most red bars with a head shot and to chunk yellow bars in three-to-four rounds. The Apex Predator stacks naturally on the deliberate trigger pulls, and the 40 m optimal range means the rifle stays effective in raid and Summit content where positioning matters.

In PvP, semi-auto Bighorn is a hit-and-miss pick. The per-shot damage is brutal on landed head shots, but the trigger-limited RPM means a missed shot costs more than on a full-auto AR. Skilled marksman PvP players love the Bighorn semi-auto for its precision and TTK potential; spray-and-pray PvP players will be happier in full-auto mode.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros**

- Highest per-shot damage of the three Bighorn modes
- Apex Predator stacks reliably on landed shots
- 40 m effective range — best of the three modes
- Pairs perfectly with Memento, Coyote's, and Headhunter
- Ammo-efficient sustained DPS

**Cons**

- Trigger-limited RPM caps peak DPS below full-auto theoretical
- Semi-auto demands rhythm; mashing the trigger drops the stack ramp
- Less forgiving than full-auto for missed shots
- Not the right mode for crowd control

## FAQ

**Is semi-auto the strongest Bighorn mode in TU22.1?** For sustained PvE single-target damage, yes. Full-auto produces higher peak DPS in ideal short windows, and burst mode is the most precise at range, but semi-auto is the most reliable mode for raid, Summit, and Heroic Countdown.

**Do I lose Apex Predator stacks when switching modes?** No. Apex Predator stacks persist across firing-mode switches, which makes the Bighorn one of the best mode-switching rifles for hybrid playstyles.

**What attributes should I roll on the Bighorn?** Weapon damage on the major slot is non-negotiable. Headshot damage on the minor is the standard pick; damage to elites is a strong alternative for raid play.

**Semi-auto or full-auto for general PvE?** Semi-auto. Full-auto has its moments, but semi-auto is the mode you should default to and only switch out of for specific situations.

## Closing

The Bighorn's semi-auto mode is the precision-marksman face of one of the most flexible exotics in the game. If you have been alternating between modes without committing, give semi-auto a dedicated week of play. The rhythm is rewarding, the per-shot damage is exceptional, and the Apex Predator stacks ride cleanly across the entire engagement. For most PvE content, semi-auto Bighorn is the mode that earns the rifle's exotic-tier reputation.
