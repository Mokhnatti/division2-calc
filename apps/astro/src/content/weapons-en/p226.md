## At a Glance

The P226 is The Division 2's mid-tier service pistol — a 9mm semi-automatic with a 15-round magazine, 300 RPM cyclic rate, and 80,000 base damage. It sits between the heavy-hitting 1911-platform pistols (Custom 1911, M1911) and the smaller, faster Glock 19 in terms of role. The P226 trades per-shot damage for capacity and rate of fire, making it a strong sustained-fire sidearm without sacrificing too much accuracy or recoil control.

In TU22.1, the P226 occupies a niche similar to the PF45 but with slightly different trade-offs: less per-shot damage, but a higher RPM ceiling. Where the PF45 fires at 280 RPM with 90k base, the P226 fires at 300 RPM with 80k base, meaning their sustained DPS lands roughly equivalent. The choice between them comes down to feel and talent rolls — the P226 has a slightly snappier cyclic rate, while the PF45 has the fractional damage edge per shot. Both are excellent rotational pistols for stack-based builds.

Theoretical sustained DPS reaches approximately 400,000 before mods, achievable in practice thanks to the 15-round mag and consistent fire rate. Recoil is light and predictable — the 9mm chambering and modern frame design mean shots stay grouped tightly through a full magazine. The P226 is one of the easier pistols to track headshots on, which partially offsets its lower per-shot damage.

The 300 RPM rate is the highest among the standard pistol category before you reach the Glock 19's 320 RPM. This makes the P226 viable for short-burst close-quarters engagements where rate of fire matters more than per-shot ceiling. The 18 m optimal range is short, and falloff drops damage to roughly 60% by 50 m, so the P226 is firmly a close-to-mid-range tool.

## Talent

The P226 rolls the standard pistol talent pool. **Perpetuation** is the meta pick — pistol damage stacks on primary weapon reload, ideal for rotational builds that swap between primary and pistol frequently.

**Pummel** is strong for rifle and marksman builds where the P226 acts as a stack-generator for the primary. **Vindictive** offers utility in group play. Avoid **Sadist** rolls unless you specifically build around bleed-application, and skip **Last Stand**.

The P226's high RPM and large mag mean it generates pistol crit kills more frequently than slower pistols, which makes Pummel particularly effective — you'll proc the talent more often, building rifle stacks faster.

## Top Builds

The P226 works in several archetypes:

**Memento Stack-Feeder:** Coyote's Mask, Memento backpack, Striker chest. The P226's 15-round mag and 300 RPM make it one of the fastest Memento stack-feeders in the game, second only to the PF45 with extended mods. The high RPM means stacks tick over quickly, and the 1.5s reload keeps pressure steady.

**Heartbreaker Pressure Build:** Heartbreaker chest synergizes with sustained fire weapons, and the P226's mag size and RPM make it a good secondary slot. Use it during primary reloads to maintain Heartbreaker proc uptime without dropping your damage rotation.

**Pestilence Cycler:** Pestilence rewards swaps, and the P226 is well-suited as a swap target — its high RPM applies Pestilence ticks rapidly, and the mag size lets you stay in pistol mode longer to build damage before swapping back.

**Hunter's Fury PvP Pressure:** In Dark Zone and Conflict, Hunter's Fury 4-piece with the P226 as a sidearm gives you strong sustained-fire pressure. The 15-round mag is excellent for skirmisher rotations where you need to stay engaged longer than a 7-round mag allows.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE, the P226 is a competent rotational pistol. It doesn't hit hard enough to act as a finisher on Heroic and Legendary elites, but it cycles fast enough to feed Memento stacks, apply Pestilence ticks, and provide chip damage during primary reloads. Its role is supportive rather than headline, and on tuned stack-management builds it carries genuine value.

In PvP, the P226 is a strong skirmisher pistol. The 300 RPM and 15-round mag make it competitive in CQB engagements, and the predictable recoil delivers consistent headshot tracking at mid-range. Where it falls short is in dueling against high-damage pistols like the 686 or Custom 1911 — the lower per-shot damage means it loses straight trades. Use the P226 in PvP for sustained pressure and follow-up rather than burst engagement.

## FAQ

**P226 vs PF45 — which is better?**
The PF45 has slightly higher per-shot damage (90k vs 80k); the P226 has slightly higher RPM (300 vs 280). DPS is roughly equivalent. Pick based on talent roll quality and personal preference — both are excellent rotational pistols.

**Can the P226 one-tap headshot at endgame?**
On red-bars, yes, with a tuned build and clean headshot. Purples and yellows are very unlikely to one-tap given the 80k base damage ceiling.

**Should I roll Perpetuation or Pummel?**
Perpetuation for swap-rotation builds, Pummel for rifle primary loadouts that want pistol-driven damage stacks. The P226's high crit-kill frequency makes Pummel slightly stronger than on slower pistols.

**Is the P226 worth using over an SMG sidearm?**
Pistols and SMGs occupy different slots — pistols are secondaries, SMGs are primaries. The P226 doesn't replace an SMG; it complements one in builds that want both sustained primary fire and a rotational pistol.

## Closing

The P226 is The Division 2's "high-RPM service pistol" pick — a sidearm that prioritizes volume of fire and stack-management over per-shot damage. It won't carry your DPS chart against the 686 Magnum or Custom 1911, but it will keep your Memento stacks alive, your Pestilence ticks landing, and your Heartbreaker procs uptime steady. In TU22.1's stack-heavy meta, that supportive role is genuinely valuable, and the P226's combination of mag size, RPM, and predictable recoil makes it one of the better rotational pistols in the loot pool. If you find a clean Perpetuation or Pummel roll, it deserves a serious look in your sidearm slot.
