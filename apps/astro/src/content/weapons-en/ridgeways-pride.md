## At a Glance

Ridgeway's Pride is an Exotic Assault Rifle that flips the standard cover-shooter playbook on its head. With a base damage of 50,000 per bullet, a 750 RPM cadence, a 40-round magazine, and a 2.3-second reload, this rifle is built for players who refuse to hide behind a wall. The 1.55x headshot multiplier is class-standard, but the talent — Make It Count — is anything but, rewarding you for staying out in the open and punishing you for ducking back into safety.

- **Who this is for.** Aggressive players who push the room, run buff-stack builds, and use cover for movement rather than shelter. The talent is designed around that playstyle and falls apart for turret-style builds.
- **No-cover playstyle.** The 30% out-of-cover damage bonus is permanent so long as you do not enter cover. Going to cover resets the stacks, full stop. This is a discipline weapon.
- **Acquisition.** Exotic field research from the Camp White Oak side mission, plus weekly project rotation. RNG free once you complete the steps.
- **Best fit:** Striker's Battlegear, Hunter's Fury 4pc, Memento backpack, Glass Cannon chest, or any aggressive front-line build.

## Weapon Talent: Make It Count

> "Make It Count" — While out of cover, gain +30% Total Weapon Damage. Stacks reset upon entering cover.

The mechanic is simple in description and brutal in execution. Anywhere outside the cover system gives you a flat 30% weapon damage bonus, and the moment you press the cover button (or the auto-cover-on-touch system snaps you in) the stack zeroes out. Coming out of cover does not refund the stack — you have to play the entire fight on foot.

In practice this turns Ridgeway's Pride into a build commitment, not just a weapon swap. You cannot run it on a Glass Cannon turret build because the moment you hide from a grenade you lose the talent. You cannot run it on a passive Heroic strategy because the talent specifically punishes the duck-and-shoot pattern that most Heroic content rewards. What you can do is run it on a Hunter's Fury or Strikers aggressive frame where the buff stack is permanent so long as you are alive and moving.

Practical numbers from a verified Strikers + Memento setup at full Strikers stacks and out-of-cover talent active:

- Body shot, base: **~50,000** per bullet
- Body shot, +30% talent: **~65,000** per bullet
- Body crit, +30% talent + Strikers max: **~210,000** per bullet
- Headshot crit at full stack: **~325,000** per bullet
- Sustained DPS, full mag: **~3.5M**

The 30% is total weapon damage, which means it applies on top of all other damage modifiers — Strikers stacks, brand bonuses, expert holster, mod damage. That stacking is what makes the talent strong in spite of the cover restriction.

## Top Builds

### Ridgeway's × Strikers Battlegear × Memento

The flagship aggressive-front-line build. Strikers stacks weapon damage on every hit, Memento adds armor and weapon damage trophies that drop on kills, and Ridgeway's permanent +30% glues the whole thing together. The build wants you to be moving constantly between adds, never stopping behind a wall, and the rifle's per-bullet damage with Strikers caps in the 200K+ range.

### Ridgeway's × Hunter's Fury 4pc

The CQC variant. Hunter's Fury rewards close-range kills with weapon damage and armor regen stacks, which slots perfectly into a no-cover playstyle. The 35m optimal range on the AR sits right where Hunter's Fury wants you, and the talent stack means you are running through doorways at +30% damage rather than peeking from behind one.

### Ridgeway's × Glass Cannon × Coyote's Mask

The all-in glass cannon variant. Glass Cannon doubles incoming damage but boosts outgoing by 25%, Coyote's Mask gives you swap-procced CHC, and Ridgeway's permanent +30% layers on top. This is a Countdown and pre-cleared raid build only — one Black Tusk grenade and you are downed without armor regen to save you.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, Ridgeway's Pride is the AR for players who run aggressive frames and never use cover anyway. The 30% damage bonus is competitive with most exotic-tier talents on AR, and the 35m optimal range works for most Heroic indoor arenas. Where it falls off is content that forces you into cover — Black Tusk hunters in legendary missions, raid mechanics that demand positioning, any fight where standing in the open is a death sentence regardless of buff.

The talent does not care about kills, hits, or reloads. As long as you are not in cover, the +30% is up. That makes the rifle uniquely consistent for builds that sustain through movement rather than through ramped stacks.

In PvP, Ridgeway's Pride is a contested pick. The +30% out-of-cover damage is enormous in DZ skirmishes where most fights happen on foot, and the 750 RPM with 40-round mag is competitive with most ARs in the slot. The drawback is that the cover system in DZ is more reflexive than in PvE — you snap to cover by accident and your buff drops, which means PvP players need to manually unbind cover or play very carefully.

## Comparison

Versus Eagle Bearer, Ridgeway's Pride trades the headshot-kill talent for a permanent passive bonus that does not require kills at all. Eagle Bearer is the better headshot weapon, Ridgeway's is the better volume weapon. Versus Chatterbox, Ridgeway's hits harder per bullet but lacks the on-reload CHC stack that makes Chatterbox a sustained-fire monster.

Versus a god-roll Police M4 or AK-M with Optimist or Boomerang, Ridgeway's wins on flat damage but loses talent flexibility. The base ARs let you mix and match brand and chest talents that Ridgeway's locks you out of by demanding the no-cover playstyle.

## FAQ

**Is Ridgeway's Pride still good after TU22?** Yes. The talent was tuned briefly in TU20 to specify "while out of cover" rather than "out of cover stacks," which removed an exploit but did not change the practical playstyle. Still a top-tier exotic AR.

**How do I get it?** Camp White Oak has a side mission that, once completed weekly for several rotations, drops the exotic component. Combine with the named cache rewards from the project tab.

**Does cover-on-touch reset the talent?** Yes. Brushing a low wall and snapping in counts as entering cover. Many players unbind the cover system entirely when running the rifle.

**Ridgeway's or Pestilence?** Different tools. Pestilence is a sustained-DoT rifle that wants you behind cover, Ridgeway's is the opposite — aggressive front-line damage. They are not direct competitors.

**Can I run Ridgeway's with a defensive build?** Technically yes, but the talent will not be active most of the time. It is designed for offensive frames.

## Closing

Ridgeway's Pride is the rifle for players who already refuse to hide. The +30% damage bonus is permanent, stacks with everything, and asks only that you commit to moving instead of camping. If your playstyle already lives outside the cover system, this rifle pays you for what you were doing anyway.
