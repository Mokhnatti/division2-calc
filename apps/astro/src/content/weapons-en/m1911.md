## At a Glance

The M1911 is the workhorse of The Division 2 sidearm pool — a classic single-action .45 ACP semi-automatic that has been a staple of the loot tables since launch. It is not the flashiest pistol in the game, nor the most powerful, but it occupies a comfortable middle ground between the cylinder-based revolvers (686 Magnum, Diceros) and the higher-capacity service pistols (P226, Glock 19). With 100,000 base damage, 250 RPM, and a 7-round magazine, the M1911 is what most players reach for when they want a reliable backup that punches above its weight in headshot trades.

The role of the M1911 in TU22.1 is essentially "default well-rounded pistol." It is the weapon you pick up off a fallen enemy, slot into a quick-swap build, or use to round out a Memento or Pestilence rotation when you don't need the per-shot ceiling of a magnum. Its 1.5-second reload is fast, its recoil pattern is gentle, and the 7-round capacity is just enough to deal with a single elite or two reds before you need to swap or reload. The M1911 isn't dazzling on paper, but it is endlessly useful in practice.

Theoretical sustained DPS is approximately 416,000 before mods. In practice, with brand bonuses and a Memento or Striker stack, an M1911 in TU22.1 routinely lands headshots in the 6-10M range, which is competitive against unarmored red-bars and well into purple-bar territory on Heroic. The recoil pattern is mild and predictable: a slight upward kick that reseeds quickly between shots, making the M1911 one of the easiest pistols to track at distance.

The 18 m optimal range is short — beyond that, falloff begins immediately, dropping to roughly 60% of base damage by 50 m. This means the M1911 is designed for close-to-mid-range work and shouldn't be your answer to a sniper across the map. Reload speed and a small mag are the trade-offs you accept for a fast, flat-shooting sidearm.

## Talent

Standard rarity drops do not roll a fixed talent on the M1911 — instead, talent pool inheritance applies based on rarity tier. **Perpetuation** is the most common high-value talent and the one most players seek out. It adds pistol damage stacks on primary weapon reload, which slots naturally into rotation builds.

Other useful rolls include **Pummel** (pistol crit kills convert into rifle damage stacks for your primary), **Sadist** (bonus damage to bleeding targets — synergizes with Bloodsucker's or Coyote builds), and **Vindictive** (allies gain crit chance after you hit a target). Avoid **Last Stand** rolls; the talent activates only at low health and rarely matters when the M1911 is in your hand.

## Top Builds

Three archetypes use the M1911 well:

**Memento Cycler:** The M1911 is one of the best Memento stack-feeders in the game. Its 1.5-second reload and 7-round mag mean you cycle through stacks fast, and Memento's pickup mechanic doesn't care about per-shot damage as much as kill volume. Pair with Coyote's Mask, a Striker chest, and a high-RPM SMG primary.

**Sharpshooter Backup:** Run the M1911 as a finisher in a Sharpshooter build. The pistol damage tree on Sharpshooter doubles as a headshot specialist, and the M1911's predictable recoil makes it one of the most accurate finisher pistols available. Useful for picking off snipers or fleeing red-bars after your rifle stack runs dry.

**Pestilence Swap:** Pestilence rewards weapon swaps, and the M1911's 1.5s reload combined with its modest 100k base damage means you swap to it, fire 2-3 shots into a target to apply Pestilence ticks, then swap back. The M1911 is excellent for this rotation because it isn't trying to one-tap — it is just delivering enough damage to keep the parasite pressure alive.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE, the M1911 is a reliable secondary slot. It handles red-bars at close range, finishes wounded purples, and feeds Memento stacks faster than any other pistol in the game thanks to its mag size and reload speed. On Heroic and Legendary it falls behind harder-hitting sidearms (686, Custom 1911) for elite damage but stays useful as a stack-management tool. If your build doesn't care about pistol per-shot damage, the M1911 is genuinely one of the best picks in the category.

In PvP, the M1911 is a competent skirmisher but not a duel-winner. The 250 RPM is too slow to out-trade an SMG in CQB and too low-damage to one-tap headshots from range. Where the M1911 shines in Conflict and Dark Zone is in finishing fleeing opponents — its short reload means you can swap to it from a depleted primary and continue applying pressure without dropping out of the fight. Treat it as a continuation tool, not a primary engagement weapon.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Fast 1.5-second reload — among the fastest in the pistol category
- Predictable recoil, easy to track headshots
- Solid 7-round magazine — better than revolvers, comparable to Custom 1911
- Wide loot availability — drops everywhere, easy to roll for talent

**Cons:**
- 100,000 base damage is unimpressive at endgame
- Short 18 m optimal range — falloff hits hard
- No native named-pistol exotic effects
- 250 RPM is below most service pistols (P226, Glock 19)

## FAQ

**Is the M1911 worth using at endgame?**
As a secondary slot for Memento or Pestilence rotations, yes. As a primary damage source, no — the per-shot damage caps too low for Heroic and Legendary elite TTK.

**M1911 vs Custom 1911 — which is better?**
Custom 1911 is strictly better in terms of damage (110k vs 100k), RPM (280 vs 250), and reload (1.4s vs 1.5s). Use the Custom 1911 if you want a 1911-platform pistol; the standard M1911 is mostly a stepping stone or a Memento feeder.

**What talent should I roll on the M1911?**
Perpetuation if you swap weapons frequently, Pummel if you run a rifle primary that wants damage stacks. Pummel slightly edges out Perpetuation for AR/MMR builds.

**Does the M1911 work with shotgun specialist builds?**
It can, but most shotgun specialists prefer the Custom 1911 or a P226 for the bigger mag. The M1911 is fine as a budget option.

## Closing

The M1911 is one of those weapons that you either love or forget about entirely. It is not flashy, not exotic, not the highest damage in its category — but it is reliable, it cycles well, and it does its job in the secondary slot without complaint. In a TU22.1 meta where Memento and Pestilence rotations dominate, the M1911's combination of fast reload, predictable recoil, and consistent damage makes it a genuinely good pick for swap-heavy builds. If you're chasing per-shot ceiling, look at the 686 Magnum or Custom 1911. If you're chasing reliability and stack maintenance, the M1911 is still one of the best tools in the pool.
