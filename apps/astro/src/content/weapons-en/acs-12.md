## At a Glance

The ACS-12 is the introductory full-auto shotgun in The Division 2. It fires at 250 RPM with an eight-round magazine and a 30k per-shot damage value, putting it firmly between the semi-auto Super 90 and the full-auto AA-12 on the DPS curve. For agents who want the feel of a full-auto shotgun without committing to the AA-12's harder-to-control fire rate, the ACS-12 is the natural starting point.

In TU22.1 the ACS-12 is a respected mid-tier full-auto. It does not produce the room-clearing burst of the AA-12 and it does not have the per-shot punch of any pump, but the 250 RPM trigger and eight-round magazine combine into a weapon that handles continuous engagements more comfortably than anything in the pump bracket.

The ACS-12 is also the more forgiving full-auto shotgun for newer players. Recoil is manageable, the spread cone holds together better than the AA-12's, and the lower fire rate means you actually have time to react during a magazine.

The 30k base damage figure is a per-shell number — each trigger pull fires twelve pellets and the per-pellet damage scales as you would expect. Sustained DPS at 250 RPM puts the ACS-12 at roughly 75% of the AA-12's output but with significantly tighter shot-to-shot accuracy.

The eight-round magazine is the same size as the Super 90's and gives you about 1.9 seconds of continuous fire before reload. The 2.0-second reload is acceptable for the family, neither punishing nor remarkable.

Headshot multiplier of x1.5 is standard. The full-auto trigger means consistent headshot hits are harder to maintain than on a pump, but the ACS-12 holds enough of its spread under sustained fire that you can land most of your pellets in the same general area as your reticle.

## Talent

The ACS-12 rolls from the full-auto shotgun talent pool. Best-in-slot picks are:

- **Pummel** — kills refresh the magazine and grant +20% weapon damage for 10 seconds.
- **Optimist** — weapon damage increases by 1% for every missing round in the magazine.
- **Lucky Shot** — magazine capacity increased by 50%, weapon handling improved.

Pummel is the meta talent on every shotgun in the game and the ACS-12 is no exception. Optimist plays well on the eight-round magazine because you can stack meaningful damage before the reload kicks in. Lucky Shot is the niche pick for builds that want a twelve-round ACS-12 to maximize sustained DPS without reload interruptions.

## Top Builds

### Striker ACS-12

Six-piece Striker with the ACS-12 is the textbook solo Heroic build for agents who want full-auto shotgun feel. Striker stacks build per pellet, so a single 250 RPM magazine can produce more stacks than any other primary in the game. Pummel keeps the stacks alive forever, and the ACS-12's tight spread cone means you actually land most of those pellets on target.

### Heartbreaker ACS-12

Heartbreaker scales damage with hits taken while ammo is in the magazine. The ACS-12 is the second-best full-auto shotgun for Heartbreaker (after the AA-12) because the slightly slower fire rate means you eat through the magazine more slowly, which keeps stacks alive longer. Run Picaro's Holster and Fox's Prayer for the standard talent stack.

### Hunter's Fury ACS-12

Hunter's Fury is the close-range PvE-and-PvP brand-set that scales with hits in close range. The ACS-12's full-auto trigger and 10-meter optimal range make it the perfect Hunter's Fury primary for solo Conflict and Dark Zone work. Pair with The Catharsis or a Memento backpack for the talent-stack synergy.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE the ACS-12 is a solid all-rounder. It does not produce the highlight-reel boss kills of an AA-12 or the one-shot reds of an M870, but it handles continuous engagements better than any pump and more comfortably than any other full-auto shotgun. The eight-round magazine and manageable recoil mean you can chain three or four kills without ever stopping to think.

In PvP the ACS-12 is a respected mid-tier pick. It is not the meta — that is the Boomstick or AA-12 — but it has the best controllability of any full-auto shotgun in 1v1 Conflict, and the 10-meter optimal range gives it more flexibility than a sawed-off. Aim for the upper chest. PvP normalization caps shotgun headshot scaling, so consistent body shots are more valuable than risky headshot attempts.

## FAQ

**Is the ACS-12 better than the AA-12?**
For controllability and forgiveness, yes. For raw DPS, no. The AA-12 produces higher sustained damage but the ACS-12 is easier to land consistently, especially at the upper end of its optimal range.

**What is the best talent for the ACS-12?**
Pummel for almost every build. Optimist works well on the eight-round magazine if you are running Strega or another build that benefits from delayed reloads.

**Can the ACS-12 one-shot reds?**
Not in a single trigger pull, no. The per-shell damage is too low. However, a two- or three-pellet burst will kill any red on Challenging difficulty and most reds on Heroic with a buffed build.

**Is the ACS-12 good for solo Heroic?**
Yes, especially on Striker or Heartbreaker. The eight-round magazine and continuous fire profile mean you handle multi-target rooms more comfortably than any pump shotgun build.

## Closing

The ACS-12 is the gateway full-auto shotgun for The Division 2. It does not produce the meta-defining damage of an AA-12 or the pure one-shot kills of an M870, but it is more forgiving than either of them, and on a Striker or Heartbreaker build it can outperform anything in the family on continuous-engagement scenarios.

In 2026 the ACS-12 sits in the upper-middle tier of shotguns. It is not the strongest pickup but it is the most controllable full-auto, and that controllability is what makes it the right pick for agents who are transitioning from pumps into full-auto play.
