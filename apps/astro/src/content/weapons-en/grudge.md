## At a Glance

Grudge is an Exotic Rifle that doubled down on the headshot-chain mechanic and turned it into one of the strongest single-target damage profiles in the game. With a base damage of 80,000 per bullet, a 100 RPM cadence, an 8-round magazine, and a 2.5-second reload, this rifle rewards methodical headshot stacking against priority targets. The 2.0x headshot multiplier is on the upper end of the rifle class and pairs cleanly with the talent's ramping damage stacks.

- **Who this is for.** Players who play long-range single-target roles in raid and Heroic content and want a rifle that scales with sustained accuracy. The talent is designed for fights that last longer than 5 seconds against high-HP enemies.
- **Headshot chain dependency.** Every link in the chain matters. A single body shot or missed shot resets the build, so this is a discipline weapon for confident snipers.
- **Acquisition.** Exotic field research from the Manning National Zoo expedition, plus weekly project rotation. Once completed it is in your stash forever.
- **Best fit:** Tipping Scales 4pc, Headhunter chest, Strikers Battlegear, or any single-target ramp build.

## Weapon Talent: Headshot Chain Damage Stacks

> "HS Chain Build Damage Stacks" — Each consecutive headshot increases your weapon damage by 8%, stacking up to 10 times. Stacks reset on body shot, miss, or reload.

The mechanic is the most demanding ramp talent in the rifle class. Every consecutive headshot adds 8% weapon damage, stacking up to 80% at 10 hits. The 8-round magazine means you can hit 8 stacks per mag if every shot lands on the head, and reloading drops you back to zero — but unlike Mantis, switching targets does not reset the stack, which means you can chain through a wave of yellow elites in a single mag if your aim is steady.

In practice, Grudge plays like a long-fight ramp weapon. The first 1-2 shots are always at baseline damage, the middle 4-5 shots ramp into the meaningful range, and the last 2 shots of the mag are doing 70-80% more damage than the first. On a Tipping Scales 4pc + Headhunter setup, that closing shot is one-shot territory for most yellow elites and named bosses lose 25-30% of their HP per mag.

Practical numbers from a verified Tipping Scales + Headhunter build at max CHD stacks:

- Body crit, no stack: **~210,000** per bullet
- Headshot crit, 1 stack: **~720,000** per bullet
- Headshot crit, 5 stacks (40% bonus): **~1,000,000** per bullet
- Headshot crit, 10 stacks (80% bonus): **~1,300,000** per bullet
- Headhunter cap on cash-in: **3,000,000+** with full stacks rolled into Headhunter chest
- Burst DPS in optimal mag: **6-8M** sustained, **15M** burst on cap-cash

The crucial thing is the headshot chain does not require kills. You can stack 10 deep on a single boss target, which is what makes Grudge so strong in raid encounters where one named boss is the priority.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, Grudge is a top-tier raid and Heroic single-target rifle. The talent ramp pairs perfectly with the kind of long fights against named bosses and yellow elites that Heroic missions throw at you, and the 50m optimal range works for most indoor arena sightlines. Where it falls off is mob-clear in tight rooms — the 100 RPM is too slow for grouped reds, and the talent reset on body shot punishes any spray.

The 8-round mag is generous for a rifle and gives you enough headroom to actually reach 8 stacks before reload, which is the threshold where the talent starts paying real dividends.

In PvP, Grudge is a heavy-hitter precision rifle but rarely the meta pick. The slow rate of fire and ramp dependency make it slow to start a fight, which is a problem in DZ skirmishes where the first 0.5 seconds determine the engagement. Where it shines is sniping perches in Conflict where you can pre-stack on an unaware target and one-shot them. Most ranked PvP players prefer Mantis or a god-roll Diceros for the precision role.

## Comparison

Versus Mantis, Grudge has a longer ramp but a higher ceiling and a larger magazine. Mantis caps at 5 stacks per mag for 25% bonus, Grudge can reach 10 stacks for 80%. Grudge is the better long-fight rifle, Mantis the better short-engagement one.

Versus Eagle Bearer, Grudge trades the on-kill reload buff for a damage ramp. Eagle Bearer is more forgiving for chain-kill scenarios, Grudge is stronger against single high-HP targets.

Versus a god-roll Model 700 with Boomerang, Grudge wins on raw per-bullet damage but loses the on-miss return mechanic that makes Boomerang so forgiving.

## FAQ

**Is Grudge still good after TU22?** Yes. The talent has not been touched in any recent balance pass, and the rifle remains a top-tier raid pick. The exotic field research is straightforward once you complete the Manning National Zoo prerequisites.

**Does target switching reset the stacks?** No. Only body shots, missed shots, and reloads reset the chain. This is what makes Grudge so strong against waves of yellow elites — you can carry 10 stacks across multiple targets.

**What is the practical stack count?** 5-7 stacks is what you live at most of the time in a normal Heroic mission. 10 stacks happens on boss fights where you have a stationary high-HP target and uninterrupted aim.

**Grudge or Mantis?** Grudge for long single-target raid fights. Mantis for short bursts against yellow elites and PvP. They cover different roles in the same loadout.

**Can I run Grudge without Tipping Scales?** You can, but you are leaving most of the build's ceiling on the table. Strikers is the closest non-Tipping alternative and gives up about 30% ceiling DPS.

## Closing

Grudge is the rifle that rewards patience and punishes spray. If you can land 8 consecutive headshots on a boss without missing, this gun is the ceiling of the rifle class. Pair it with Tipping Scales and a Headhunter chest and you have one of the strongest single-target burst profiles in the game.
