## At a Glance

The Mk 17 is a semi-automatic rifle in the Rifle family, occupying the heavy-hitter slot of the legendary rifle roster. With 100,000 base damage, 130 RPM, and a 10-round magazine, the Mk 17 trades a sliver of the SOCOM's cadence for the highest per-shot damage of any legendary rifle. Reload is a tidy 2.5 seconds, and the 2.0x headshot multiplier rewards careful shot placement.

In TU22.1 the Mk 17 has emerged as the rifle of choice for players who want maximum per-shot damage without dropping into marksman-class cadence. It is the rifle that pairs best with Strained because the higher base damage means each crit hits harder, and it is the rifle that performs best on bosses and yellow elites because per-shot damage matters more than sustained output. The 10-round magazine matches the SOCOM, and the 130 RPM cadence matches the M1A, so the Mk 17 sits in a comfortable middle ground that combines the strengths of both.

### Damage math

Sustained body-shot DPS equals 130 / 60 multiplied by 100,000, giving 216,666 raw DPS - the highest sustained body-shot DPS of any base rifle. Apply the 2.0x headshot multiplier with typical 50 percent crit chance and 80 percent crit damage, and the Mk 17 reaches roughly 750,000 to 850,000 DPS on headshot-only fire. With Strained or Rifleman, the Mk 17 can climb above 1.5 million sustained DPS, leading the rifle category in raw output.

## Talent slot

The Mk 17 carries a single mod talent slot. The strongest TU22.1 picks:

- **Strained** - up to 50 percent critical hit damage in exchange for armor. The Mk 17's high per-shot damage makes Strained one of the highest-multiplier talents in the game.
- **Rifleman** - up to 65 percent weapon damage stacking on consecutive headshots. The 10-round magazine helps maintain stacks across longer fights.
- **Perfectly Optimist** - up to 35 percent weapon damage as the magazine empties. The 10-round magazine ramps Optimist quickly enough to be useful.

Strained for damage builds, Rifleman for sustained content.

## Top Builds

### 1. Strained Mk 17 (PvE Heroic)

The classic. Stack Critical Hit Chance and Critical Hit Damage on chest and backpack, Picaro's Holster, Glass Cannon chest. Strained on the Mk 17 produces the highest crit damage numbers in the rifle category. Output: 1.5 to 1.8 million DPS sustained.

### 2. Rifleman Stacking

Rifleman rewards consecutive headshots, and the Mk 17's 10-round magazine and 2.0x headshot multiplier align perfectly. Use a Coyote's Mask, Memento backpack, and Determined chest. Output: 1.4 to 1.6 million DPS sustained, with strong stack sustain.

### 3. Tip of the Spear Mk 17

Tip of the Spear scales grenade and signature damage with weapon damage. The Mk 17's high per-shot damage feeds the set efficiently. Strained on the gun, Vigilance on the chest, and a Coyote's Mask round out the build. Output: 1.4 to 1.6 million DPS plus skill and grenade damage spikes.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, the Mk 17 is the top-tier rifle for Heroic and Legendary boss fights. Its highest-in-class per-shot damage shreds yellow elites in fewer shots than any other rifle, and Strained procs deliver crits comparable to marksman-rifle headshots. On adds-heavy content, the SOCOM has a slight edge because of higher cadence, but the Mk 17 stays competitive thanks to its larger per-shot value.

In PvP, the Mk 17 is one of the strongest rifles for Conflict and Dark Zone. Time-to-kill on a 6-million-armor opponent is roughly 0.5 seconds with two headshots, matching the SOCOM but with higher per-shot damage. The 10-round magazine and 2.5-second reload keep you in the fight, and the 130 RPM cadence is fast enough for follow-up shots.

## FAQ

### Is the Mk 17 better than the M1A?

For per-shot damage and Strained synergy, yes. For long-range engagements and traditional Rifleman setups, the M1A is comparable. The Mk 17 is the rifle to bring for boss fights and high-damage burst windows; the M1A is the rifle to bring for general-purpose precision play.

### Strained or Rifleman?

Strained for damage builds, Rifleman for stacking sustain. Strained produces the highest single-shot crits in the rifle category, Rifleman the highest sustained DPS over long fights. Most players keep both rolls in the stash.

### What attachments should I prioritize?

Critical-hit-chance optic, critical-hit-damage muzzle, extended magazine, and a stability grip. The Mk 17's standard 10-round magazine can be swapped for a 13-round magazine for boss fights.

### How does the Mk 17 compare to the Lullaby exotic?

Lullaby is an exotic rifle with a unique reload-on-melee mechanic and a damage-stacking talent. Lullaby outdamages the Mk 17 on melee-friendly setups but loses on pure ranged play. The Mk 17 is more flexible because you can roll any talent and play at full range.
