## At a Glance

The Saber is one of the heaviest-hitting Marksman Rifles in The Division 2, a Named precision weapon built around a single ruthless premise: every shot should matter, and every shot should kill. With a 300000 base damage value and a 2.5x headshot multiplier it sits at the top end of the per-shot damage curve, only beaten by a handful of true exotics and the heaviest sniper-class weapons.

Saber is the answer when you do not want to chase moving targets at speed — you want to put a clean round through a yellow bar's helmet and watch them fall. The 80 RPM cadence is deliberate, the 5-round magazine is unforgiving, and the 2.7 second reload is long enough that a missed shot in the wrong moment costs lives. In return you get a rifle that turns Heroic and Legendary elites into single-shot problems and that headlines almost every "best MMR" tier list since it dropped.

## Stats

### Core numbers

- **Base damage:** 300000 per shot
- **Rate of fire:** 80 RPM
- **Magazine:** 5 rounds
- **Reload:** 2.7 seconds (full)
- **Headshot multiplier:** 2.5x
- **Optimal range:** 50-100m
- **Damage falloff start:** ~70m
- **Aim down sights time:** ~0.4s

### Damage output

- **Body DPS (sustained):** ~400K
- **Headshot DPS (sustained):** ~1M
- **Burst (mag dump, headshots):** 5 shots x 750K = 3.75M in roughly 3.75s
- **Time-to-kill on a 1.5M HP yellow bar:** one clean head, maybe two

### Handling

Saber's recoil is heavy but predictable — a strong vertical kick with very little horizontal drift, which makes it ideal for marksman scopes and longer engagement distances. The 5-round magazine combined with the slow reload pushes you toward Determined or Optimist for builds that want sustained pressure, but most Saber loadouts treat it as a sniper-style executioner: take three shots, reload behind cover, repeat.

## Talent

### Saber-specific talent

The named talent on Saber rewards landing critical hits at range. When you hit a critical headshot beyond a threshold distance — typically 30m or so — the rifle gets a stacking buff to weapon damage, and a body shot or a close-range hit clears it.

The intent is obvious: Saber wants to be played at range. The talent encourages you to hold your ground, take measured shots, and refuse to chase enemies into close combat. Paired with Crit Chance and Crit Damage gear it produces some of the largest single-shot numbers in the game outside of an exotic.

### Practical tips

- Always engage from the longest sightline available
- Pair with marksman gloves and chest with Crit-focused secondaries
- Reloads do not clear the buff, but missing or hitting close range does
- The talent is generous enough that even moderate Crit chance builds see a real benefit

## Top Builds

### Pure Marksman Crit

Four-piece Striker plus Coyote's Mask plus Walker, Harris and Co. holster. Crit Chance on the chest, Crit Damage on the backpack, Damage to Targets Out of Cover everywhere else. With this loadout Saber crits regularly hit 1.6-2.0M against Heroic yellows.

### Hunter's Fury Marksman

Three-piece Hunter's Fury plus Empress Plate Carrier plus Picaro's Holster. Push damage to elites and headshot damage. This setup turns Saber into a finisher for the moments when an elite breaks cover at range.

### Negotiator Crit Hybrid

Four-piece Negotiator's Dilemma plus Lengmo gloves. Saber places the marker, the Negotiator effect mops up the surrounding adds. This loadout turns the rifle into a full team-clear tool instead of a single-target deletion piece.

## PvE-PvP

### PvE

Saber is one of the strongest single-target MMRs in TU22.1 PvE. Heroic and Legendary content rewards burst damage on hard targets, and Saber's 750K headshot ceiling per round is exactly what those modes ask for. It excels in Countdown when you can hold an angle on a yellow bar from across the map, in Summit on long-sightline floors, and in raids where the team needs a precision damage anchor.

Where it struggles is movement-heavy fights. The 5-round magazine and 2.7 second reload make it punishing if you are forced to relocate constantly. Pairing it with a fast skill like a Striker drone or a Sniper turret offsets that weakness by giving you a way to keep pressure on while you reposition.

### PvP

In Conflict and DZ, Saber is the sniper that other snipers respect. A clean headshot is almost always a kill on a non-bulwark build, and the 50-100m optimal range happens to overlap perfectly with most PvP engagement distances. The downsides are also amplified — the long reload window is a death sentence if you whiff a shot in a duel, and the slow ADS time makes it weak inside 30m.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- 300K base damage, one of the highest per-shot values in the MMR class
- 2.5x headshot multiplier translates to genuine one-shot kills on most reds and purples
- Predictable recoil pattern, easy to learn
- Range-focused talent that rewards positioning and aim
- Versatile across PvE and PvP

### Cons

- 5-round magazine, very punishing for missed shots
- 2.7 second reload is long enough to lose engagements
- Slow ADS, weak inside 30m
- Talent requires distance, hurting CQB performance
- High build investment to unlock its full potential

## FAQ

### Saber or Tyrannicide?

Saber is the higher per-shot ceiling, Tyrannicide is the higher sustained DPS. If you play patient and pick targets, Saber wins. If you play aggressive and chase tempo, Tyrannicide wins. Both deserve a build.

### What scope should I use?

A 12x marksman scope is the natural choice for Saber. Some players prefer a 9x for a slight ADS speed bonus, but the rifle is built for distance and a heavier scope plays into its strengths.

### Does the talent stack with regular Crit damage?

Yes. The Saber talent buff is multiplicative with Crit Damage, which is why the rifle scales so aggressively into Crit-focused builds.

### How important is reload speed?

Very. The 2.7 second base reload is one of the slowest in the MMR class. A reload speed mod or a brand bonus that shaves it down to 2.0 seconds noticeably improves field performance.

### Is Saber worth farming if I already have Mantis?

Yes. Mantis is a generalist precision rifle, Saber is a single-target deletion specialist. They cover different roles and most serious players keep both.

## Closing

Saber is the rifle for the player who believes the first shot should be the only shot. It rewards patience, position and precision, and in the hands of a competent marksman it produces some of the highest single-shot damage numbers in the game. It is not the easiest weapon to pick up — the small magazine, slow reload and range-locked talent demand discipline — but for those willing to play to its strengths, Saber sits firmly in the top tier of Division 2 weapons.
