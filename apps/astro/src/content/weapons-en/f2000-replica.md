## At a Glance

F2000 Replica is the named variant of the F2000 assault rifle, a bullpup AR that sits firmly in the high-RPM bracket of the class. Base damage of 55,000 per bullet, 750 RPM cadence, 30-round magazine, 2.2-second reload, and a 1.55x headshot multiplier define a weapon built for volume rather than per-bullet output. The Replica adds a locked named talent to the standard F2000 profile, turning it from a competent fast-firing AR into a build cornerstone for sustained-damage setups.

- **Who this is for.** Players who want a high-cadence AR that puts rounds on target faster than the precision-AR class, with a guaranteed talent that does not require farming dozens of duplicates.
- **Volume DPS.** 750 RPM is among the faster figures in the AR class. The 55K per-bullet damage is lower than precision ARs, but the bullets-per-second rate makes up the gap and pulls ahead in sustained engagements.
- **Acquisition.** Drops from named bosses across DC and NY, with elevated rates from Cleaners-aligned activities. Targeted Loot rotation includes it on a roughly 7-week cycle.
- **Best fit:** Strikers Battlegear, Hard Wired skill builds with weapon-talent triggers, In Sync chest setups, generalist sustain loadouts.

### Intrinsic Attributes (random roll)

The intrinsic on F2000 Replica rolls weapon damage and a secondary like CHD, HSD, or damage to elites. God-roll farming aims for 14-15% on the primary and 12-13% on the secondary. Targeted Loot rolls average higher than open-world drops. The bullpup configuration means recoil control is less demanding than it would be on an equivalent rifle-stock AR.

### Named Talent Slot (Locked)

F2000 Replica ships with a fixed named talent. The locked slot is the trade — you give up flexibility, you get a guaranteed roll. With the Replica, the named talent is built around the high RPM, which means the value is harder to replicate by rolling a generic talent like Optimist on the standard F2000.

### Built-in Mods

Standard AR mod slots — optic (4x default), magazine, underbarrel, muzzle. The 750 RPM means F2000 Replica burns through a 30-round mag in about 2.4 seconds of constant fire, so magazine extension mods are a popular pick. Reload-speed mods compete with damage-bias mods for the same slots.

## Weapon Talent: Replica Edition (Named)

> "F2000 Replica" — Sustained fire builds a stacking rate-of-fire and damage bonus that resets on stop-fire. Caps inside one mag.

The named talent is built for the gun. Every shot fired in an unbroken burst feeds the stack, and the stacks scale both rate of fire and damage simultaneously. By the back half of the mag, F2000 Replica is firing closer to 850 RPM with damage that approaches precision-AR per-bullet figures. The catch is that stop-fire resets the stacks, so the talent rewards committing to the engagement rather than tapping individual targets.

In practice, F2000 Replica plays like a volume AR with a back-loaded damage curve. You burn through the first half of the mag at base stats, the back half rides the stack bonus into burst-DPS territory, and the reload puts you back at zero stacks for the next engagement. On a Strikers Battlegear build, the named talent's stacks layer with Strikers stacks for an aggressive ramp.

Practical numbers from a verified Strikers + Vigilance + Memento setup:

- Body shot, no stacks: **~58,000** per bullet
- Body crit, full stacks + Vigilance + Memento: **~280,000** per bullet
- Headshot crit at full stack: **~430,000** per bullet
- Sustained DPS, full mag with stacks: **~3.2M**

The locked talent means F2000 Replica cannot run Optimist or Killer. The named talent is the entire reason to pick this gun over the standard F2000.

## Top Builds

### F2000 Replica × Strikers Battlegear

The flagship sustain build. Strikers stacks weapon damage on hits, the named talent stacks rate of fire and damage, and the 750 RPM is the right cadence to ramp both stack engines simultaneously. The strongest sustained-DPS AR build outside the precision archetype.

### F2000 Replica × Hard Wired × In Sync

The hybrid skill-DPS build. Hard Wired keeps skills topped up, In Sync converts weapon crits to skill damage bonus, and the 750 RPM puts crits out fast enough to keep In Sync's bonus active. Strong in mixed-DPS Heroic comps.

### F2000 Replica × Heartbreaker × Memento

The aggressive armor-on-hit build. Heartbreaker rebuilds armor through sustained fire, Memento adds trophy stacks, and the named talent's ramp keeps the engagement going long enough to fully restore armor. Forgiving build for solo Heroic.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, F2000 Replica is the AR for players who want volume. The 750 RPM clears grouped reds faster than any precision AR, the named talent's back-loaded ramp makes the second half of the mag hit harder than the first, and the 35m optimal range covers most engagement distances. Where it falls off is long sightlines — the 35m cap is shorter than the precision-AR class.

The 30-round mag burns fast at 750 RPM, but the 2.2-second reload is short enough that you do not lose much DPS to swaps. Magazine extension mods are popular for keeping the stack alive longer.

In PvP, F2000 Replica is a strong CQC and medium-range AR. The 750 RPM wins most duels against slower ARs, and the named talent's ramp means extended fights favor F2000 Replica. The 1.55x HS multiplier is lower than precision ARs, so PvP value is less about headshots and more about volume of fire.

## Comparison

Versus standard F2000, the Replica trades the open talent slot for the locked named. The standard F2000 with a god-roll Optimist is competitive but lacks the rate-of-fire ramp that defines the Replica.

Versus Police M4, F2000 Replica is faster RPM and lower per-bullet damage. Police M4 is the more balanced pick, F2000 Replica the volume specialist.

Versus Black Market AK-M, the two are opposites. Black Market AK-M is the slow-cadence precision opener, F2000 Replica is the high-cadence sustain workhorse. Both are valid in different builds.

## FAQ

**Is F2000 Replica worth chasing in TU22.1?** Yes for sustain-DPS builds. The named talent is unique to the gun and remains uncontested in the high-RPM AR niche.

**F2000 Replica or ACR-E?** ACR-E for the higher per-bullet damage and 1.65x HS multiplier, F2000 Replica for the higher RPM and ramp talent. Different roles.

**Where does F2000 Replica drop?** Named bosses across DC and NY, Cleaners-aligned activities, and Targeted Loot rotation every ~7 weeks.

**Does the ramp talent stack with Strikers?** Yes. The named talent's stacks and Strikers stacks are separate engines and layer cleanly.

**Does stop-fire really reset the stacks?** Yes. The stack decays the moment you stop firing. Magazine extension mods and reload-on-the-move are the two main coping strategies.

## Closing

F2000 Replica is the volume-DPS AR with a unique back-loaded ramp. The 750 RPM cadence, 55K per-bullet damage, and named talent that stacks both rate of fire and damage make it the sustain-AR answer to the precision-AR ceiling. Pair it with Strikers Battlegear for the most aggressive sustain rotation in the AR class, and use it in any engagement where you can commit to a full mag without stop-fire.
