## At a Glance

The FAL is a battle rifle masquerading as an assault rifle. With a 70,000 base damage per shot — the highest in its class — a deliberate 650 RPM, a stingy 20-round magazine, and an elevated 1.65x headshot multiplier, the FAL is a specialist tool for agents who would rather make every round count than spray for safety. It is the AR you build around when you want a rifle that punches like a marksman gun but cycles fast enough to handle a group.

This rifle rewards precision. Every shot has weight, every reload feels long, and every headshot lands with a satisfying crack of platform damage. Players who came up on the original FAL in Division 1 will recognize the rhythm immediately: aim, breathe, two taps, look for the next target.

The 70k base damage and 1.65x headshot multiplier give the FAL the highest landed-headshot DPS in the AR class. The catch is that you have to land them — the 20-round magazine punishes missed shots harder than any other AR, and the 2.5-second reload makes a dropped fight a dropped life.

## Talent

The FAL's named-roll talent is **Strained**, the legacy crit-damage scaling talent that has defined burst-DPS builds since Year 1. Strained increases critical hit damage as your armor depletes, scaling up dramatically once you are below half health. On a high-damage, low-RPM platform like the FAL, Strained is devastating — every round is a candidate for a Strained-scaled crit.

Strained is the talent that put the FAL on the meta map and keeps it there. The interaction with low-armor builds is one of the most rewarding mechanics in the game: you intentionally let your armor stay broken, you stack repair-on-kill or Patience to cycle back when needed, and you ride the 60% armor-broken Strained bonus through the entire engagement.

The talent demands a specific build philosophy. You cannot run Strained on a tanky chassis without losing most of its value. You cannot run it on Patience or a self-repair vest without intentionally throttling your armor. But for agents who commit to the bleed-down playstyle, Strained on the FAL is one of the highest ceilings in the AR class.

## Top Builds

### Glass Cannon Strained FAL (raid DPS)

Coyote's Mask, Memento backpack, Hunter's Fury or Striker chest with Glass Cannon, Walker holster, four red cores, named FAL. Glass Cannon adds 25% damage at the cost of 25% incoming damage, which keeps your armor sliding into the Strained sweet spot. Memento trophies amplify the per-shot output, Coyote's flat headshot multiplier compounds the 1.65x base. This is one of the highest single-target DPS loadouts in the game when piloted correctly.

### Striker FAL (sustained PvE)

Four-piece Striker, Sokolov chest, Walker holster, three reds and a yellow. The 650 RPM is slow for Striker stack maintenance, but the high per-shot damage makes each stack-extending hit count for more. A safer build than the glass cannon, with most of the ceiling.

### Foundry FAL (defensive raid)

Four-piece Foundry Bulwark, named FAL. The set's armor regeneration covers Strained's intentional armor sliding, giving you a forgiving version of the bleed-down playstyle. Lower ceiling, much higher floor.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, the FAL is a top-three AR for organized DPS play. The base damage and headshot multiplier mean it punches in the marksman-rifle weight class while cycling faster than any DMR, and Strained scales the crits into territory that few rifles can match. The 20-round magazine and 2.5-second reload mean the FAL is unforgiving — you cannot panic-spray, you cannot tactical-reload aggressively, and you cannot afford to miss. Played correctly, the FAL is one of the most rewarding rifles in the game.

In PvP, the FAL is a niche pick. The high per-shot damage and 1.65x headshot multiplier produce a fast TTK on landed shots, but the slow RPM and small magazine punish missed shots more than any other AR. Skilled PvP players who can tag heads consistently rate the FAL highly; everyone else is better off with a higher-RPM rifle.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros**

- Highest base damage in the AR class
- 1.65x headshot multiplier — premium over the standard 1.55x
- Strained is one of the strongest crit-damage talents in the game
- Strong at 35 m, longer than most ARs

**Cons**

- 20-round magazine is the smallest in the AR class
- 2.5 s reload is the slowest in the AR class
- Punishing for newer or imprecise players
- Strained requires a specific armor-management playstyle to peak

## FAQ

**Is the FAL meta in TU22.1?** Yes, for raid and Heroic Countdown DPS specifically. Strained was nudged in the last balance pass but remains a top-tier crit talent, and the FAL's per-shot damage is unrivalled. For casual content, the rifle is overkill and the magazine size is annoying.

**FAL or Lefty?** The Lefty is a marksman rifle with a similar damage profile. The FAL fires faster and has a larger magazine; the Lefty hits harder per shot and reaches further. For 25-to-40 meter engagements, the FAL wins. For 50-meter-plus, the Lefty wins.

**How do I survive the small magazine?** Extended Mag is mandatory, and you should pair the FAL with a fast-handling secondary (SMG or shotgun) to cover reloads. Striker or Foundry sets ease the reload pressure with their armor-regen behavior.

**Is the FAL worth grinding for if I don't have a Strained build?** It is still a strong AR, but Optimist on the SIG 556 will out-DPS a non-Strained FAL in most scenarios. Build the chassis first, farm the named roll second.

## Closing

The FAL is the AR for agents who want their rifle to feel heavy. It is not forgiving, it is not flexible, and it is not the gun you hand to a new agent. But for players who have built a Strained-friendly chassis and want a rifle that rewards precision with raid-tier numbers, nothing in the AR class scales like the FAL. Roll one with weapon damage and crit damage attributes, learn the recoil pattern, and watch the damage numbers stop making sense in the best way.
