## At a Glance

The Mechanical Animal is a Named assault rifle in The Division 2 that hits the sweet spot of fire rate, per-shot damage, and handling. With a base damage of 50,000 per round, an 800 RPM cyclic rate, a 30-round magazine, a 2.0 second reload, and a x1.55 headshot multiplier, the Mechanical Animal sits comfortably in the mid-upper bracket of the AR class. It is fast enough to feed stack-based builds, hard-hitting enough to feel impactful, and stable enough to actually land its shots.

This is a weapon that earns its name through reliability rather than gimmicks. It feels mechanical in the best sense, where every trigger pull produces predictable, measurable results, and animal in the sense that it hits with real weight and aggression. For agents who want a versatile AR that scales across content types and build templates, this is one of the strongest picks in the current TU22.1 loot pool.

## Stats

### Base numbers

- Base damage: 50,000 per round
- Rate of fire: 800 RPM
- Magazine: 30 rounds
- Reload: 2.0 seconds
- Headshot multiplier: x1.55
- Optimal range: 12 to 38 meters
- Falloff: gentle past 40 meters
- Accuracy: high while aimed
- Stability: above-average for the RPM bracket

### Damage per second math

At 800 RPM the Mechanical Animal fires 13.33 rounds per second. With a base of 50,000 that calculates to roughly 666,667 baseline DPS before any modifiers. The x1.55 headshot multiplier sits at the AR class average, and once layered with red cores, weapon damage rolls and brand bonuses, real-world DPS climbs comfortably into multi-million territory. The 800 RPM is fast enough to feed Striker stacks and Memento ramps without losing the per-shot weight that defines a good AR.

### Time-to-kill in PvE

In Heroic content the Mechanical Animal drops standard reds in roughly 0.3 to 0.5 seconds, veterans in 0.8 to 1.2 seconds, and elites in 1.5 to 2.2 seconds. The faster fire rate consistently produces TTKs at the lower end of these ranges, and the stable handling means you actually realize the weapon's potential in practice.

## Talent

The Mechanical Animal's talent rewards sustained fire with a damage uplift that scales as you stay engaged. The effect layers cleanly with weapon damage rolls, red core attribute, and stack-based gear talents, which means it slots smoothly into existing build templates without demanding rework.

### How the talent layers

The buff builds as you fire, providing meaningful damage progression that rewards staying engaged with the fight. The effect does not require kills, reloads, or proximity triggers, which means it stays online during extended boss engagements as well as fast-paced room clears. This makes the Mechanical Animal one of the most flexible AR talents in the current loot pool.

## Top Builds

### Striker red core

The Mechanical Animal and Striker's Battlegear are an obvious pairing. The 800 RPM ramps Striker stacks quickly, the 30-round mag provides enough sustained fire to maintain peak stacks, and the 2.0 second reload limits decay windows. Pair with Providence headshot pieces, Fenris kneepads, and a Picaro's Holster.

### Memento balance

Memento backpack rewards consistent kills with damage and headshot bonuses. The Mechanical Animal's faster fire rate means trash mob clearing happens efficiently, which keeps Memento trophies stacking during sustained engagements. Run a balanced red-yellow split with Memento, Picaro and Providence brand pieces.

### Hard Wired hybrid

For control-leaning skill builds, the Mechanical Animal contributes meaningful damage even on lower red core counts. The talent's flat uplift means your AR pulls weight while skills handle the heaviest targets, and the stable handling makes it easy to land shots while juggling skill cooldowns.

## PvE vs PvP

### PvE feel

In PvE the Mechanical Animal is a smooth, dependable AR that handles every type of content without complaint. Trash mob clears feel responsive, veterans and elites fall in reasonable TTKs, and the weapon's range bracket covers most engagement distances inside missions and Strongholds. It is one of the most consistent AR picks for general endgame content.

### PvP feel

In conflict modes and the Dark Zone the Mechanical Animal is a strong all-rounder. The faster fire rate gives it an edge in close-mid range duels, and the per-shot damage holds up at longer ranges. The talent's flat uplift means you do not need to win the build mind-game to win the gunfight, and the stable handling translates directly to performance.

## FAQ

### How does Mechanical Animal compare to Glory Daze?

They are very similar weapons. Both fire at 800 RPM with 30-round magazines and 2.0 second reloads. The main difference is base damage, with Glory Daze at 55,000 and Mechanical Animal at 50,000. The choice often comes down to talent fit and which one you have a better-rolled version of, since the raw stat differences are minor.

### Where does it drop?

It is a named AR following standard TU22.1 named loot rules. Targeted loot weeks for ARs improve odds, and Heroic Countdown sessions remain among the most efficient farms. Dark Zone supply drops and Legendary mission rewards also count.

### Is the talent worth building around?

The talent provides a clean uplift without demanding specific triggers, so building around it is unnecessary. Slot the Mechanical Animal into an existing build template and let the talent contribute alongside your gear and brand bonuses. This versatility is what makes the weapon so widely used.

### Can I run this on Legendary?

Yes. The Mechanical Animal holds up in Legendary content with appropriate build tuning. The 800 RPM and 50,000 base damage produce sustained DPS that scales well into higher difficulty content, and the stable handling makes it easier to land headshots against bullet-sponge elites.

### Should I prioritize crit chance or weapon damage?

A balanced approach works best. The Mechanical Animal's stable handling makes crit builds practical to execute, and the talent layers cleanly with weapon damage rolls. Aim for a balanced spread of crit chance, crit damage and weapon damage rather than min-maxing one stat.

## Closing Thoughts

The Mechanical Animal is a quietly excellent AR that earns its slot through versatility and reliability rather than specialization. It hits a sweet spot between fire rate and per-shot damage, fits seamlessly into most build templates, and rewards sustained engagement without demanding specific triggers. It is not the flashiest named AR in the loot pool, but it is one of the most consistent.

For agents building Striker or Memento setups, this gun is a strong pick that scales with gear improvements. For players still developing their endgame loadout, the Mechanical Animal is a forgiving on-ramp that grows with your build rather than locking you into a specific play pattern. It is the kind of weapon you reach for when you want to stop thinking about your loadout and start thinking about the fight, and that practical reliability is exactly what makes a named AR worth keeping in the rotation across hundreds of hours of endgame play.
