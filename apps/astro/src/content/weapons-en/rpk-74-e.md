## At a Glance

The RPK-74 E is the lightest LMG in the base roster, and its profile reflects that heritage as a converted squad automatic weapon. With 50,000 base damage, 700 RPM, and a 75-round magazine, it sits between an assault rifle and a true heavy LMG. Reload is a brisk 4.0 seconds. Handling is the best of any LMG in the game, with strafe speed and ADS time noticeably faster than the M60 or HK121. The trade-off is per-shot damage, which is the lowest in the LMG class.

What the RPK-74 E loses in raw output it makes up for in feel and flexibility. It is the LMG to pick if you also play assault rifles and want a belt-fed option that does not force you to overhaul your playstyle. Recoil is mild, the optic mounts cleanly, and the magazine size is large enough that you do not panic-reload in the middle of an engagement. In TU22.1 the RPK-74 E remains a reliable B-tier LMG, with the niche of being the most ergonomic option in the family.

### Damage math

Sustained DPS at 700 RPM equals 700 / 60 multiplied by 50,000, giving 583,333 raw DPS. With a typical 50 percent crit chance and 80 percent crit damage, the RPK-74 E pushes to roughly 825,000 DPS before talents. Headshots scale to 1.55x, so the weapon prefers crit and amplified damage rolls over a strict headhunter approach.

## Talent slot

The RPK-74 E carries a single mod talent slot. The most useful rolls in TU22.1:

- **Optimist** - up to 35 percent weapon damage as the magazine empties. The 75-round magazine ramps Optimist faster than the Stoner, slower than the HK121.
- **Steady Handed** - 25 percent recoil reduction. The RPK already has the lightest recoil in the LMG family, but Steady Handed pushes it to laser-flat.
- **Boomerang** - heal on critical-hit damage. Niche but useful on builds with high crit chance and no Vigilance.

Optimist remains the default damage roll. Steady Handed turns the RPK into a precision tool.

## Top Builds

### 1. Heartbreaker RPK (PvE Heroic)

Heartbreaker rewards body shots, and the RPK's flat recoil makes it the easiest LMG to land body-only fire on. Optimist on the gun, Vigilance on the chest, Picaro's Holster, and Memento backpack. Output is roughly 1.4 to 1.5 million DPS sustained, slightly behind the Stoner but with smoother handling.

### 2. Negotiator's Dilemma Hybrid

The RPK pairs well with a Negotiator's Dilemma exotic chest because both weapons share similar handling profiles. Use the RPK as the primary and the Negotiator's marks as the burst-damage spike. Striker gear set boosts both.

### 3. Hard Wired Skill Support

In a skill build, the RPK is the LMG most likely to feel comfortable as a backup weapon. Hard Wired's cooldown reset on damage taken keeps skills active, and the RPK provides clean filler damage between skill cycles. Steady Handed makes mid-range engagements stress-free.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, the RPK-74 E is a comfortable mid-tier LMG. It does not lead the damage charts, but it is the easiest to land headshots with and the fastest to bring up from a sprint. On Heroic content it competes with the Stoner for the title of most-forgiving LMG. Boss DPS is roughly 15 percent lower than the M60, which is enough to feel the difference but not enough to invalidate the choice.

In PvP, the RPK is the most viable LMG outside the M60. Its handling lets it strafe and aim at speeds normally reserved for ARs, and the 75-round magazine gives plenty of follow-up fire after the first burst. Time-to-kill on a 6-million-armor opponent is competitive but slower than the M60. The RPK is the LMG to bring to the Dark Zone if you value mobility over burst damage.

## FAQ

### Is the RPK-74 E worth using in TU22.1?

Yes, but only for specific builds. The RPK is a great pick for Heartbreaker, hybrid skill setups, and PvP-leaning loadouts. For pure boss DPS, the M60 outclasses it. The RPK shines when handling and mobility matter as much as damage.

### Should I roll Optimist or Steady Handed?

Optimist for damage builds, Steady Handed for headhunter or PvP setups. The RPK's already-flat recoil means Steady Handed is rarely necessary on PvE builds.

### What attachments should I prioritize?

Extended magazine, critical-hit-chance optic, critical-hit-damage muzzle, and a heavy grip. The RPK's standard 75-round magazine can be swapped for a 100-round drum at the cost of handling.

### How does the RPK compare to the Pakhan exotic?

Pakhan is an exotic LMG with a 200-round magazine and reload-on-empty mechanics. It outdamages the RPK on long sustained fights but loses on burst output. The RPK is more flexible and easier to slot into varied builds.

## Closing

The RPK-74 E is the LMG for players who want belt-fed comfort without belt-fed weight. It is the most ergonomic option in the family, and its handling makes it the natural pick for hybrid playstyles that mix LMG suppression with AR mobility. Per-shot damage is the lowest in the class, so you will not lead damage meters with it, but you will outlast teammates who are wrestling M60 recoil. Pick the RPK-74 E if you value smooth play over peak numbers, and pair it with Heartbreaker or Striker for the best results.
