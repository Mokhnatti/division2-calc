## At a Glance

The PF45 is The Division 2's high-capacity service pistol — a polymer-framed .45 ACP designed for sustained fire rather than per-shot ceiling. With 90,000 base damage, 280 RPM, and a generous 17-round magazine, the PF45 represents a complete philosophical opposite of the 686 Magnum: where the magnum gives you six brutal trigger pulls, the PF45 hands you almost three times the volume at a slightly faster cyclic rate. The trade-off is per-shot damage, which is the lowest of the standard 1911-platform pistols, but the cumulative output is excellent for stack management, Memento rotation, and Pestilence cycling.

In TU22.1, the PF45 fills a "rotational pistol" niche. It is not the pistol you switch to for a one-tap finisher — that role belongs to the 686 or Custom 1911 — but it is the pistol you switch to when you need to dump rounds quickly, feed stacks, apply Pestilence ticks, or cycle Memento pickups. The 17-round capacity is genuinely transformative for build crafting: it allows the PF45 to function almost like an SMG in a sidearm slot, especially on builds that want to maintain fire pressure during primary weapon reloads.

Theoretical sustained DPS sits at approximately 420,000 before mods, which the PF45 actually achieves more reliably than most pistols thanks to its large magazine and fast reload. The recoil pattern is mild and well-controlled — the polymer frame absorbs vertical kick, and the 280 RPM rate doesn't push hard enough to break aim discipline. Beyond 22 m, falloff begins, and damage drops to roughly 55% of base by 50 m. The PF45 is best used inside its optimal range, which is right where most sidearm engagements happen anyway.

The 17-round mag is the headline number. Most pistols force you to reload after 6-8 shots, breaking your damage rhythm. The PF45 lets you cycle a full Memento stack refresh, dump into a Pestilence application, and finish a red-bar all on a single magazine. Reload speed mods become less important on this weapon because you reload less often.

## Talent

Standard rarity drops on the PF45 do not have a fixed talent, so brand bonuses determine its rolled effect. **Perpetuation** is the meta pick: every primary reload adds a stack of pistol damage, and the PF45's high mag size means you stay in pistol mode longer to spend those stacks before swapping back.

**Pummel** is the second-best roll, especially for AR/Rifle primary builds where pistol crit kills generate primary damage stacks. **Vindictive** is a niche pick for group play — it grants nearby allies bonus crit chance on hit. Avoid **Sadist** unless you specifically run a Bloodsucker's build, and skip **Frenzy** rolls (the talent doesn't synergize well with the PF45's mag-dump style).

## Top Builds

The PF45 fits naturally into several archetypes:

**Memento Cycler Plus:** The PF45 is the single best Memento stack-feeder in the game thanks to its 17-round mag and fast reload. Pair with Coyote's Mask, Memento backpack, Striker chest, and a high-RPM SMG primary. The PF45 fires long enough on a single mag to refresh stacks without forcing a reload break.

**Pestilence Pressure Build:** Pestilence rewards constant tick application, and the PF45 lets you keep a Pestilence parasite alive on a target longer than any other pistol. Run with Heartbreaker chest piece for survivability and a high-DPS rifle primary.

**Hunter's Fury PvP:** The PF45's mag size makes it competitive in Dark Zone and Conflict where sustained fire matters more than per-shot damage. With Hunter's Fury 4-piece, the PF45 can apply enough armor damage to shred opponents through their plate without forcing a reload mid-fight.

## PvE-PvP

In PvE, the PF45 is a strong secondary for stack-management builds. It doesn't compete with the 686 Magnum for elite finishing, but it dramatically outperforms it for sustained engagements and rotational pressure. On Heroic and Legendary content, the PF45 won't carry your damage output — but it will make sure your primary's stacks stay ramped, your Memento stays cycling, and your Pestilence ticks land continuously. That is a meaningful contribution even when the PF45 isn't doing the headline damage.

In PvP, the PF45 is one of the most underrated sidearms in the game. The 17-round mag is genuinely overpowered in tight skirmishes — opponents with 6-shot revolvers run dry while you continue to apply pressure. The 280 RPM and predictable recoil make it accurate in Conflict, and the fast reload means you can pop in and out of cover without losing engagement uptime. Where it loses is at long range, where the 90k base damage drops too far on falloff to compete with marksman pistols.

## FAQ

**Is the PF45 better than the M1911?**
For sustained fire and Memento rotation, yes — the 17-round mag and 280 RPM outclass the M1911's 7-round, 250 RPM profile. For per-shot damage, the M1911's 100k beats the PF45's 90k, but barely.

**Does the PF45 work with extended mag mods?**
Yes, and it scales well — you can push the mag size higher with the right mod, though the diminishing returns curve flattens past 20 rounds.

**Can the PF45 one-tap headshot in PvP?**
Generally no — 90k base damage isn't enough to one-tap a healthy agent at any meaningful range, even with full crit stacking. Use it for sustained pressure and follow-up shots, not as a hero pistol.

**Should I roll Perpetuation or Pummel on the PF45?**
Perpetuation if your primary reloads often (LMG, AR with extended mag); Pummel if your primary is an MMR or rifle that benefits from pistol-driven damage stacks.

## Closing

The PF45 is The Division 2's "pistol that thinks it's an SMG" — and that identity is its biggest strength. It won't win you per-shot damage benchmarks against the 686 Magnum or Custom 1911, but it will outlast every other sidearm in a sustained engagement and outpace every other pistol for stack maintenance. In TU22.1, where Memento and Pestilence rotations are central to optimized DPS builds, the PF45's combination of mag size, reload speed, and consistent damage makes it one of the most underrated pistols in the game. If you build around its strengths, the PF45 quietly becomes the most-fired weapon on your loadout.
