## At a Glance

The SVD is a semi-automatic marksman rifle modeled on the iconic Russian Dragunov platform. Within The Division 2 it occupies the comfortable middle of the MMR family - faster cadence than the bolt-action M44, larger magazine than the SR-1, but lower per-shot damage than either. With 200,000 base damage, 120 RPM, and a 10-round magazine, the SVD is the most versatile semi-auto MMR in the game. Reload is a tidy 2.4 seconds, and the 2.5x headshot multiplier is shared across the MMR family.

The SVD's identity is sustained pressure rather than burst spike. You will not one-shot yellow elites the way an M44 can, but you will land follow-up shots faster than any other MMR in the game. In TU22.1 the SVD has quietly become the best entry-level marksman rifle thanks to its forgiving handling, larger magazine, and clean sight picture. It is also the most beginner-friendly MMR for players learning to play at marksman ranges.

### Damage math

Sustained body-shot DPS equals 120 / 60 multiplied by 200,000, giving 400,000 raw DPS - the highest sustained body-shot DPS of any base MMR. Apply the 2.5x headshot multiplier with a typical 50 percent crit chance and 80 percent crit damage profile, and the SVD reaches roughly 1.3 to 1.5 million DPS on headshot-only fire. The 10-round magazine and 2.4-second reload give an effective uptime above 70 percent on sustained engagements.

## Talent slot

The SVD carries a single mod talent slot. The strongest picks for TU22.1:

- **Headhunter** - 100 percent of headshot damage as a stack to the next body shot. Strong on the SVD because the larger magazine lets you stack multiple times before reload.
- **Lucky Shot** - up to 28 percent magazine bonus and reload speed scaling on missed shots. Excellent forgiveness for a beginner-friendly weapon.
- **Steady Handed** - 25 percent recoil reduction. Niche, but makes the SVD into a near-laser at long range.

Headhunter is the default damage roll. Lucky Shot is preferred for new players or PvP-leaning loadouts.

## Top Builds

### 1. Headhunter SVD (PvE Heroic)

Stack Headshot Damage on chest and backpack, Critical Hit Chance and Damage in attachments, Picaro's Holster, and a Vile mask. Headhunter procs land a body shot after a headshot, and with a 10-round magazine you can land two stacked body shots before reloading. Output: 1.3 to 1.6 million DPS on tagged enemies.

### 2. Striker SVD

Striker rewards consistent hits, and the SVD's larger magazine lets you maintain stacks far better than the M44 or SR-1. A four-piece Striker setup with a Coyote's Mask and Memento backpack delivers steady DPS in adds-heavy content. Headhunter on the gun.

### 3. Determined Group MMR

For group play, the Determined chest grants critical hit chance on weakpoint hits, which the SVD's 2.5x headshot multiplier triggers reliably. Pair with a Brazos de Arcabuz holster for reload speed and a Memento backpack for stacking damage.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, the SVD is the most forgiving MMR in the game. Its larger magazine and faster cadence mean missed shots do not derail your engagement, and Headhunter stacks proc reliably. The SVD is the marksman rifle of choice for new players learning to fight at range and for veterans who want a low-stress option. Boss DPS sits between the SR-1 and the M44 - lower than the SR-1 in headshot-only fights, higher than the M44 in any sustained scenario.

In PvP, the SVD is the best-balanced MMR. The 10-round magazine forgives the first miss in a duel, the 2.4-second reload is fast enough to recover from a botched engagement, and the 120 RPM cadence outpaces the M44 for follow-up shots. Time-to-kill on a 6-million-armor opponent is roughly 0.7 seconds with two headshots. Steady Handed shines in PvP because long-range duels punish recoil.

## FAQ

### Is the SVD better than the SR-1?

For sustained damage and forgiveness, yes. For burst output, the SR-1 wins because it stacks Headhunter on a tighter window. The SVD is the MMR you bring when you expect to take more than three shots per engagement.

### Headhunter or Lucky Shot?

Headhunter for damage builds, Lucky Shot for forgiving or PvP setups. Most players keep both rolls in the stash.

### What attachments should I prioritize?

Critical-hit-chance optic, critical-hit-damage muzzle, extended magazine, and a heavy grip. The SVD's standard 10-round magazine can be swapped for a 13-round magazine on the Brazos de Arcabuz preset.

### How does the SVD compare to the Mantis exotic?

Mantis is an exotic MMR with a damage-stacking mechanic on consecutive shots and a unique scope. Mantis outdamages the SVD on long sustained shots but loses on burst windows. The SVD is more flexible and easier to slot into varied builds.

## Closing

The SVD is the marksman rifle for players who want forgiveness without giving up damage. It is the most ergonomic MMR in the game, the easiest to learn, and the most reliable for sustained engagements. Per-shot damage is the lowest of the three legendary MMRs, but the larger magazine and faster cadence more than make up for it. In TU22.1 the SVD sits at the top of the A tier of MMRs, just behind the SR-1 for pure burst and the M44 for one-shot scenarios. Pick the SVD if you want a workhorse marksman rifle, and pair it with Headhunter or Striker for the strongest results.
