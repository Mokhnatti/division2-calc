## At a Glance

The Hollow Man is a Named SMG in The Division 2 that has quietly become one of the most reliable close-quarters tools in the agent's arsenal. With a fast 950 RPM, a 30-round magazine, and a clean 1.8 second reload, it sits in the sweet spot between sustained DPS and burst damage. Its real selling point, though, is the talent: a peak +25% weapon damage amplification at full stacks that turns this little submachine gun into a flat damage multiplier for any build with consistent uptime.

If you have ever felt that SMGs were stuck in the shadow of ARs and rifles for endgame content, the Hollow Man is the kind of weapon that brings the class back into the conversation. It is not the highest base damage SMG you can equip, but the talent stacks fast, refreshes quickly, and rewards aggressive play.

## Stats

### Base numbers

- Base damage: 25,000 per bullet
- Rate of fire: 950 RPM
- Magazine: 30 rounds
- Reload: 1.8 seconds
- Headshot multiplier: x1.5
- Optimal range: 0 to 18 meters
- Falloff: damage drops sharply past 25 meters
- Accuracy: high while crouched, moderate while moving
- Stability: above-average for an SMG in this RPM bracket

### Damage per second math

At 950 RPM you fire roughly 15.83 bullets per second. With a base of 25,000 that lands you at about 395,750 base DPS before any modifiers. Headshots multiply by 1.5, and once your build's weapon damage, critical hit chance and critical hit damage stacks come online, real-world DPS climbs into millions on geared agents. Add the talent's +25% amplification at peak stacks and you are looking at a damage profile that competes directly with other top-tier named SMGs.

### Time-to-kill in PvE

Against a standard red NPC at WT5 with a moderate red-build, the Hollow Man kills in roughly 0.4 to 0.6 seconds with body shots. Against purple veterans the TTK lands around 1.0 to 1.4 seconds, and yellow elites take roughly 1.8 to 2.5 seconds depending on your stack uptime, armor mitigation and headshot ratio.

## Talent: Peak Stacks Amplifier

The Hollow Man's talent ramps up weapon damage as you put rounds into targets. Each hit grants a stack, and at full stacks you receive +25% weapon damage amplification. The amp is multiplicative against weapon damage, which means it stacks well with red core attribute, weapon damage rolls on chest and backpack, and brand bonuses such as Providence Defense and Fenris Group AB.

### Stacking mechanics

- Stacks build per bullet impact, not per fired round, so missed shots do not contribute
- Maximum stack count is reached within roughly half a magazine on a single target
- Stacks decay after a short period of not landing hits, but the decay is forgiving enough that you can swap targets without losing them
- Reloading does not drop stacks, which is unusual for SMG talents and a major quality-of-life win

### How to maximize uptime

Stay in cover that allows you to peek targets repeatedly. Aim for sustained fire rather than tap-firing, and prioritize landing the first half of the mag on a single enemy to lock in stacks before swapping to others. The talent does not require a kill to refresh, so trash mobs are perfectly fine for keeping the buff alive.

## Top Builds

### Red glass cannon

Stack red cores, run Glass Cannon and a chest with Obliterate, and pair Hollow Man with Providence Defense brand pieces for the headshot damage uplift. The +25% amp from the talent is essentially free weapon damage on top of your already-bloated red bonuses, and the SMG's high fire rate keeps Glass Cannon's damage debuff window short and manageable.

### Striker tank

Striker's Battlegear feeds on consistent hits, and a 950 RPM SMG with sticky stack mechanics is a perfect partner. The Hollow Man's talent stacks while Striker stacks, so you ramp up two damage curves simultaneously. Reload uptime is your friend here, and the 1.8 second reload makes it easy to maintain Striker stacks between mags.

### Hunter's Fury skirmisher

For shotgun-and-SMG hybrid playstyles, Hollow Man slots in as your secondary or primary depending on what your shotgun rolls. Hunter's Fury already amplifies damage and grants armor on close kills, which complements Hollow Man's effective range and the talent's amp.

## PvE vs PvP

### PvE feel

In PvE the Hollow Man shines on Heroic and Legendary content. The fast stacking, forgiving decay and quick reload make it a strong primary for room clears, and the headshot multiplier of x1.5 means you can sneak in surprise kills against named bosses if you land critical chains. It is less specialized than weapons like the Chatterbox, but more consistent across a wider variety of fights.

### PvP feel

In the Dark Zone and conflict modes the Hollow Man is sneaky-strong. The bullets-to-kill numbers favor aggressive flanks, and the talent's amp makes the difference between a four-bullet kill and a six-bullet kill against a standard skirmisher. Recoil control past 15 meters is the limiting factor, so commit to closing distance.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- Strong talent that adds flat amplification with low setup cost
- Reload does not drop stacks
- Excellent fire rate for stack uptime
- Fits a wide range of builds, not locked to one archetype
- Above-average stability for the RPM bracket

### Cons

- Base damage is modest compared to the upper tier of named SMGs
- Effective range falls off hard past 25 meters
- Stack ramp punishes burst-fire playstyles
- Headshot multiplier of x1.5 is below what crit-build min-maxers prefer

## FAQ

### Where does Hollow Man drop?

The weapon is a Named SMG, and like other named items in TU22.1 it has a chance to drop from any high-end loot source. Targeted loot for SMG-tagged activities raises the chance, and Dark Zone caches are also viable. Players have reported particularly good luck farming Heroic Countdown sessions, but that is anecdotal rather than a confirmed weighted source.

### Does the talent stack with other amplification effects?

Yes. The +25% weapon damage amp is multiplicative against weapon damage, so it layers cleanly with brand bonuses, Vigilance, Frenzy and similar effects. Stacking with another weapon-damage amp talent on the secondary does not double-dip in a broken way, but it does sum into your overall damage curve.

### Is Hollow Man better than Chatterbox?

It depends on your build. Chatterbox has a more aggressive ramp that rewards swap-cancel reloads, and is generally king for raw kill speed. Hollow Man trades that ceiling for consistency, broader build compatibility, and a less fiddly playstyle. Pick Chatterbox for raid speedruns, pick Hollow Man for general endgame content.

### Should I roll for crit chance or weapon damage rolls on a Hollow Man build?

Both, but if you have to choose one, weapon damage tends to scale better with the talent's amp. Crit chance and crit damage are fantastic on top of that, but Hollow Man does not have a crit-focused talent, so crit is a multiplier of an already amplified base.

### Does the talent's amp affect skill damage?

No. The amp applies to weapon damage only and does not extend to skills, grenades, or signature weapons.

## Closing Thoughts

The Hollow Man is the kind of named weapon that does not scream for attention but earns it once you put a few hours behind it. It rewards aggressive, sustained fire without punishing you for swapping mags, and its talent adds a clean +25% to the damage equation that scales with the rest of your build rather than competing with it. If you main SMGs, it should be in your collection. If you have been a rifle-and-AR player, the Hollow Man is a good reason to revisit the close-range bracket.

For builds that thrive on consistent uptime, this gun is a quiet upgrade. For meme-tier glass cannon experiments, it is a stable platform that lets the rest of your gear do the talking. Either way, it deserves a spot in the rotation.
