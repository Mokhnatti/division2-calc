## At a Glance

Eyeless is a Named Shotgun that turned the Hunters Fury close-range archetype on its head by combining the slot's highest base pellet damage with the brand's aggressive stack engine. With a base damage of 100,000 per pellet, a 90 RPM cadence, a 6-round magazine, and a 1.9-second reload, this shotgun is built to reward the tightest close-range pressure in the game. The headshot multiplier sits at 1.5x in line with the shotgun class, but it almost never matters — you are firing torso-spread pellets at face range.

- **Who this is for.** Players who run Hunters Fury 4pc and want a shotgun that hits the brand's stack thresholds harder than anything else in the slot. The synergy is the entire pitch.
- **Pellet ceiling king.** 100K base per pellet is the highest in the named shotgun pool. With 8 pellets per shot at point-blank, you are looking at 800K base per trigger pull before any modifiers.
- **Acquisition.** Targeted Loot in DZ South on weekly rotation, plus DZ named caches. DZ-only farm so it is harder to grind than open-world named drops.
- **Best fit:** Hunters Fury 4pc, Coyote's Mask holster, Memento backpack, Brazos de Arcabuz holster.

### Intrinsic Attributes

The intrinsic on Eyeless rolls weapon damage and a damage-to-armor or close-range damage secondary. Targeted-loot rolls average 14-16% on the primary and 12-13% on the secondary, which slots cleanly into Hunters Fury's stack-on-kill engine.

### Built-in Mods

Eyeless ships with a holographic optic, a 6-round magazine, and a heavy-pellet underbarrel that tightens the spread. The named version locks the talent but the rest of the slots are flexible.

## Weapon Talent: Hunters Fury Synergy

> "Hunters Fury Synergy" — When equipped with Hunters Fury 4pc, gain bonus weapon damage scaling with brand-set stack count. At max stacks, +20% Total Weapon Damage on this weapon.

The mechanic is brand-locked, which is unusual for the named pool. Outside of Hunters Fury 4pc the talent is dead — you get the base 100K per pellet but no bonus. Inside Hunters Fury, the talent becomes a stack multiplier that scales with the brand's existing stack engine. At max stacks (3 close-range kills feeding the brand), Eyeless adds another 20% on top of the brand's flat damage bonus.

In practice this means Eyeless is the shotgun you build a Hunters Fury kit around, not the shotgun you slot into a generic build. The brand rewards close-range kills with armor regen, weapon damage, and skill cooldown reduction, all of which compound on each other and on Eyeless's talent. The result is a build that feels almost broken for the first 30 seconds of a fight as you chain kills and stack everything at once.

Practical numbers from a verified Hunters Fury 4pc + Coyote's Mask + Memento setup at full stacks:

- Body, base, no stacks: **~100,000** per pellet (~800K total at point-blank)
- Body, max Hunters + Eyeless 20%: **~150,000** per pellet (~1.2M total)
- Body crit, full stack + Coyote: **~470,000** per pellet (~3.7M total)
- Headshot crit at full stack: **~705,000** per pellet (~5.6M total)
- One-shot threshold: most reds in Heroic, all yellow plates inside 8m

The 800K base per trigger pull is what makes Eyeless legendary. Even without the Hunters Fury talent active, the raw pellet damage is one-shot for any unarmored red enemy at point-blank, which means you can use the weapon on a generic build and still get most of the value.

## Top Builds

### Eyeless × Hunters Fury 4pc

The flagship synergy build. Hunters Fury rewards close-range kills with weapon damage, armor regen, and skill cooldown reduction stacks, Eyeless's talent layers a 20% bonus on top, and Coyote's Mask in the holster gives swap-procced CHC for the burst pellet shots. The build is the cleanest expression of the close-range archetype in the game.

### Eyeless × Hunters Fury + Brazos de Arcabuz

The dual-shotgun pressure variant. Brazos in the holster gives sidearm-swap-procced CHC, Hunters Fury maintains the close-range stack engine, and Eyeless and a god-roll secondary shotgun rotate as your primary and swap. This is the room-clear specialist build.

### Eyeless × Hunters Fury + Memento

The trophy-stack variant. Memento adds armor and weapon damage trophies on kills, Hunters Fury adds the brand stack, and Eyeless layers the talent. This is the longest-uptime variant and the strongest for solo Heroic where you cannot rely on a teammate to maintain stacks for you.

## PvE vs PvP

In PvE, Eyeless is a top-tier room-clear shotgun specifically inside Hunters Fury builds. The 8m optimal range is brutal but the pellet damage one-shots most enemies you can close on, and the brand stack engine makes you tankier and faster the longer you stay aggressive. Where it falls off is anywhere you cannot get to point-blank — long sightlines, raid arenas with positioning constraints, hunter encounters where you have to back off.

The 6-round magazine is on the smaller side for a shotgun and the 1.9-second reload is class-standard, which means you have a 4-5 second window of constant pressure per mag before you have to reset.

In PvP, Eyeless is a strong but specific pick. Hunters Fury is one of the best PvP brands and Eyeless's pellet damage one-shots low-armor targets at point-blank. The drawback is the 8m optimal — DZ skirmishes often happen at 15-25m where Eyeless's pellet spread loses too much damage to be effective.

## Comparison

Versus Sweet Dreams, Eyeless trades the melee-shotgun synergy for a brand-locked stack bonus. Sweet Dreams is more flexible (works on any build), Eyeless is stronger inside Hunters Fury specifically. Most players keep both in stash.

Versus Lullaby, Eyeless trades the Confuse mechanic for raw pellet damage. Lullaby is the utility shotgun, Eyeless the burst shotgun.

Versus a god-roll Tactico AOR, Eyeless wins on per-pellet ceiling but loses on rate of fire. Different roles — Eyeless for one-shot pressure, Tactico for sustained shotgun DPS.

## FAQ

**Is Eyeless still good after TU22?** Yes. Hunters Fury continues to be a top-tier brand and Eyeless remains the cleanest synergy weapon for the brand. Targeted Loot rotation is on a 6-week cycle.

**Does the talent work outside Hunters Fury?** No. The 20% bonus only triggers with Hunters Fury 4pc equipped. Outside the brand, Eyeless is just a high-base-damage shotgun with no procced talent.

**Eyeless or Sweet Dreams?** Eyeless inside Hunters Fury builds, Sweet Dreams for any other close-range build. They serve different roles and most players keep both.

**Where is the best place to farm Eyeless?** DZ South Targeted Loot rotation is the cleanest farm. Outside the rotation, DZ named caches give a chance and DZ landmark rewards include named shotguns in the pool.

**Can I run Eyeless in raid?** Yes. Hunters Fury is a viable raid brand for close-range roles and Eyeless slots in cleanly for any add-clear position.

## Closing

Eyeless is the shotgun for Hunters Fury players. The 100K base per pellet plus the 20% brand-locked talent stacks into the highest per-trigger-pull damage in the shotgun slot, and the close-range archetype it rewards is one of the most fun playstyles in the game. Roll the Targeted Loot rotation and pair it with Coyote's Mask and Memento for the cleanest expression of the close-range build.
