## At a Glance

The Military G36 pushes rate-of-fire to extremes while maintaining standard magazine capacity. At 50,000 base damage and 750 RPM, this weapon transforms every engagement into a volume-based game. The G36 doesn't reward precision—it rewards trigger discipline and ammo management. Where precision rifles require 60% headshot accuracy to match this weapon's DPS, the G36 performs with bodyshots alone. The 1.55x headshot multiplier is modest, but irrelevant when you're releasing 750 rounds per minute. This is the suppression specialist: saturate the battlefield with bullets and let probability handle the precision.

## Stats & Performance

**Base Configuration:**
- **Damage:** 50,000 per shot
- **Fire Rate:** 750 RPM
- **Magazine Capacity:** 30 rounds
- **Reload Time:** 2.0 seconds
- **Headshot Multiplier:** 1.55x
- **Optimal Range:** 10–25 meters

**Damage Output Breakdown:**

At 750 RPM, the Military G36 generates 625 damage per second against unarmored targets. Against a 100k armor pool, this provides 473 DPS—higher than many precision rifles despite lower base damage. Magazine depletion takes 2.4 seconds at full fire rate, enabling reload cycles of 4.4 seconds.

Total magazine damage (pure bodyshots) is 1,500,000 health per magazine. At 750 RPM, you're cycling magazines every 4.4 seconds, meaning sustained DPS over 30 seconds approaches 10+ million total damage—remarkable for crowd control. The 1.55x headshot multiplier adds minimal value; most G36 operators never aim for crits.

## Talent Slot

The Military G36 accepts **Assault Rifle talents**. Suggested configurations include:

- **Unhinged:** +25% damage but -20% accuracy. Paradoxically perfect for G36—the accuracy penalty is negligible when your engagement profile is "spray at 10–15m." The +25% damage makes G36 genuinely dangerous.
- **Strained:** +20% damage for 10 seconds after 5 consecutive hits. Easy to maintain with 750 RPM; you'll trigger Strained within 0.4 seconds of engagement.
- **Ignited:** Status effect damage and application boosts. Pairs well with Incendiary ammo or Status Effect builds.
- **Lucky Shot:** Chance to refund ammunition on critical hits. Synergizes with Ignited for extended magazine duration.

Unhinged is the meta choice, turning G36 into a genuine damage threat despite lower base stats.

## Top Builds

**Area Suppression (PvE Heroic)**
Pair Military G36 with Gunner specialization and a Striker's Battlegear piece. Stack Weapon Damage and Damage to Armor rolls. Use Unhinged as your talent. Engagement strategy: hold trigger at range, let volume eliminate targets. This build shines against Outcast Rushers and True Sons Heavies—your raw DPS output forces them into cover before closing distance. Pair with a Restorer Hive for emergency healing.

**Status Effect Volume (PvE/PvP)**
Build into Status Effect Damage and Duration. Use Ignited/Lucky Shot talent. Equip fire-loaded ammunition or poison-loaded rounds. The G36's 750 RPM applies status effects rapidly—within 2–3 bursts, enemies ignite. Enemies spend time burning rather than fighting back. This playstyle reduces reliance on precision and crit mechanics.

**Ammo Economy (Long runs)**
Stack Ammo Capacity on backpack and holster. Use Lucky Shot for critical hit refunds. Engagement strategy: maintain 750 RPM sustained fire rather than burst-checking. Against large groups, the G36's magazine sustains longer than single-elimination plays. Pair with Support Station for extended deployments. This suits Legendary Outcast farming where you need to maintain fire against 30+ enemies across a facility.

## PvE vs PvP Deployment

**PvE Dominance:**
The Military G36 excels on Challenging and Heroic content. Against Outcast hordes and True Sons heavies, the 750 RPM suppression output pushes enemies into cover faster than they can advance. Legendary runs benefit from G36's volume—you don't need perfect crit mechanics to deal meaningful damage; sustained fire alone accomplishes objectives.

**PvE Gaps:**
Against single-elite targets (named bosses), the G36 lacks damage concentration. You'll burn entire magazines without eliminating one target. Swap to precision-heavy weapons for boss phases, then back to G36 for adds.

**PvP Struggles:**
In Conflict, the G36's low base damage (50,000) and reliance on volume make it unreliable in 1v1 scenarios. Against Crits/Precision builds, you'll lose most engagements. In Dark Zone, aggressive rushes using high-RPM weapons are easily countered by defensive opponents with better aim. The G36 works best in 4-stack coordination where teammates provide suppression from multiple angles.

**PvP Niche:**
Very effective at objective control (secure zones, extraction points). The sheer volume pins opponents in place and prevents coordinated pushes.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I use Unhinged on Military G36?**
Absolutely. The -20% accuracy penalty is irrelevant at the G36's 10–15m engagement range. The +25% damage boost makes G36 genuinely competitive.

**Q: Is G36 better than M16 for suppression?**
Yes. 750 RPM vs 700 RPM might seem marginal, but the extra 50 RPM translates to 12.5% more bullets per second. Over a magazine, that's 3.75 extra rounds—minor individually, but significant in group scenarios.

**Q: Can I use G36 for Legendary farming?**
Not as primary DPS. Use it for add-clear and suppression while teammates handle elite targets. G36 excels at "clear this room of grunts quickly" but struggles against named enemies.

**Q: Does fire ammo work with G36?**
Yes, effectively. The high RPM applies Ignited status quickly. 4–5 rounds ignite most enemies; 8–10 rounds ignite Heavies. Status effects reduce their effective threat significantly.

**Q: What's better, G36 or Carbine 7?**
Different roles. Carbine 7 has higher base damage and better precision; G36 has higher RPM for pure volume. Carbine 7 suits precision builds, G36 suits suppression builds.

## Closing Thoughts

The Military G36 is a *volume weapon for players who reject precision*. Its 750 RPM and low base damage create an inverse relationship with other rifles—where precision matters, G36 trades per-shot power for sheer round count. In Challenging content and group Heroics, this philosophy succeeds spectacularly. In Legendary solo farming or PvP 1v1s, the G36 falters. But for what it does—suppress crowds, maintain constant fire, control objectives—nothing matches its output. If you value trigger discipline over trigger accuracy, the G36 belongs in your arsenal.
